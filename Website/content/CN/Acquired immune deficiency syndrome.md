# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). HIV damages the immune system, interfering with the body's ability to fight infections and diseases. AIDS is the most severe phase of HIV infection, characterized by a critically weakened immune system, leading to opportunistic infections and certain cancers. Transmission occurs through contact with infected bodily fluids, such as blood, semen, and breast milk. While there is no cure, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can manage the virus, improving quality of life and reducing transmission risk.

## Highlights

- **Increasing Trend in Cases:** From 2014 to 2024, AIDS cases in mainland China have generally increased, peaking in December 2018 with 7,897 cases, indicating a growing epidemic. <br/>

- **Variable Death Rates:** Deaths have fluctuated, with notable peaks in December 2018 (2,444 deaths) and December 2021 (2,486 deaths), reflecting periods of higher mortality. <br/>

- **Recent Decline in Cases:** In 2024, there is a slight decline in monthly cases compared to previous years, with December 2024 reporting 4,909 cases, suggesting potential improvements in prevention or reporting. <br/>

- **Seasonal Patterns:** Lower cases are typically reported in early months, possibly due to reporting delays or other factors. <br/>

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) cases and deaths in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals several trends. Initially, there is a noticeable increase in both reported cases and deaths over the years, with some fluctuations. For instance, the number of cases generally rises from 2014 through 2019, peaking in December 2018 with 7,897 cases. This upward trend is mirrored in the number of deaths, which also peaks in the same month with 2,444 deaths. This pattern suggests a growing burden of AIDS during this period, possibly due to increased transmission rates or improved reporting and diagnosis.

From 2020 onwards, the data shows a more variable pattern with some months experiencing significant drops in cases, such as January 2020 with 2,759 cases, likely influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic and its impact on healthcare systems and reporting. However, the overall trend remains high, with cases and deaths fluctuating but not returning to pre-2018 levels. By 2024, the data indicates a slight decline in both cases and deaths compared to the peak years, suggesting potential improvements in treatment, prevention, or reporting practices. The consistent reporting of cases and deaths highlights the ongoing public health challenge posed by AIDS in China, necessitating sustained efforts in prevention, treatment, and education.

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals several trends in the number of cases and deaths. Initially, there is a noticeable increase in both cases and deaths from 2014 through 2019. For instance, the number of cases rose from 5,411 in December 2014 to a peak of 7,897 in December 2018, with deaths also increasing from 1,410 to 2,444 in the same period. This upward trend suggests a growing epidemic, possibly due to increased transmission rates or improved reporting and diagnosis.

From 2020 onwards, the data shows fluctuations with some months experiencing a decline in cases and deaths, such as in January 2020 with 2,759 cases and 952 deaths, followed by a resurgence in subsequent months. By 2024, the data indicates a stabilization in the number of cases and deaths, with figures like 4,909 cases and 1,878 deaths in December 2024. This stabilization could be attributed to enhanced public health interventions, increased awareness, and better access to antiretroviral therapy. However, the persistent number of deaths highlights ongoing challenges in managing the disease effectively, emphasizing the need for continued efforts in prevention, treatment, and education.
