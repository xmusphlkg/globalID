# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). It impairs and damages the immune system, decreasing the body's ability to fight off infections and diseases. HIV is transmitted through blood, semen, vaginal fluids, and breast milk. Without treatment, HIV progresses to AIDS, characterized by opportunistic infections and certain cancers that rarely occur in healthy individuals. While there is no cure, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can control the virus, enabling individuals to live longer, healthier lives and reducing the risk of transmission. Prevention focuses on safe practices and education.

## Highlights

- **Rising Trends:** From 2014 to 2024, there was an overall increase in AIDS cases and deaths, with peaks in 2018 and 2019.<br/>
- **Seasonal Patterns:** Cases typically dip in January and February, likely due to reduced detection during Chinese New Year.<br/>
- **Stabilization:** Case and death numbers stabilized between 2020 and 2024, though fluctuations continued.<br/>
- **Recent Decline:** As of August 2024, both cases and deaths show a slight decline compared to earlier months in 2024.<br/>

The data on AIDS cases and deaths in mainland China from August 2014 to August 2024 show significant fluctuations over the decade. The number of cases ranged from a low of 1,815 in January 2023 to a peak of 7,897 in December 2018. Similarly, the number of deaths ranged from 624 in February 2015 to 2,486 in December 2021. These variations suggest both seasonal patterns and potential changes in public health interventions, awareness, or access to treatment over the years. Notably, the trend shows a general increase in reported cases and deaths from 2014 to around 2019, followed by a complex pattern of rises and dips, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic years (2020–2021).

The increase in cases and deaths during certain months, such as December 2018 and November 2021, may indicate a correlation with delayed diagnosis or reduced access to healthcare services during holiday periods. Additionally, the more recent data (2023–2024) suggests some stabilization in case numbers, although the death rates remain concerningly high, especially during the first quarter of 2024. Overall, the persistent fluctuations highlight the ongoing challenges in managing AIDS within the region, requiring sustained public health efforts.

The trend of deaths related to Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 demonstrates both seasonal fluctuations and a general increase over time. The data indicates significant spikes in mortality during the latter months of most years, particularly from October to December, as observed from 2017 to 2023. The death toll reached its highest levels in late 2018 and 2019, peaking in November and December, where deaths exceeded 2,000 per month. This upward trend can be associated with increased case reporting but might also reflect broader epidemiological factors such as delayed diagnosis or access to treatment during that period.

Post-2019, the mortality figures show a fluctuating but somewhat stable pattern, with noticeable surges, especially in the latter half of each year, consistent with earlier periods. Notably, there is a marked decrease in reported cases in early months of each year, but deaths still remain relatively high, suggesting that many individuals may succumb to the disease after long periods of illness. This pattern highlights potential gaps in treatment accessibility or the late-stage detection of the disease. By mid-2024, the number of deaths has reduced slightly compared to the peak periods, though they remain considerable, indicating the persistent burden of AIDS-related mortality despite overall progress in treatment and awareness.
