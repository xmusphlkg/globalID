# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a severe condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). HIV weakens the immune system by attacking CD4 cells, making the body vulnerable to infections and certain cancers. AIDS represents the most advanced stage of HIV, diagnosed when CD4 counts drop below 200 cells/mmÂ³ or specific illnesses arise. Transmission occurs through unprotected sex, contaminated needles, and from mother to child during birth or breastfeeding. Although there's no cure, antiretroviral therapy effectively manages the disease and significantly slows its progression.

## Highlights

- **Stable Trend:** Since 2023, cases have consistently hovered around 5000 per month, indicating a plateau in new infections. <br/>
- **Fluctuating Mortality:** Monthly deaths have varied, with a slight decrease from 2023 to 2024, suggesting improved management but persistent challenges in reducing mortality. <br/>
- **Seasonal Impact:** Earlier years showed a notable dip in cases and deaths in the early months, but this trend has weakened recently, pointing to reduced seasonal variation in transmission or reporting. <br/>
- **Comparison with Pre-2023 Data:** Compared to the pre-2023 period, the post-2023 data indicates relative stability in both cases and deaths, signaling ongoing but controlled transmission. <br/>

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) cases and deaths in mainland China from July 2014 to July 2024 reflects a significant variation in both reported cases and deaths over this ten-year period. Notably, there is a general upward trend in both cases and deaths until 2019, peaking notably in 2018 and 2019. For example, in November and December of 2018, the number of reported cases reached over 7,500, with deaths exceeding 2,400. This peak corresponds with a general increase in the disease's impact during these years, which might be attributable to several factors including better reporting, increased transmission rates, or other epidemiological factors.

Post-2019, the data shows a fluctuation in cases and deaths, with a slight decrease observed during 2020, potentially linked to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and related public health measures, which might have affected reporting and healthcare access. The data in subsequent years from 2021 onwards indicates periodic spikes, particularly in the last quarter of each year, possibly due to seasonality or other factors influencing HIV transmission and progression to AIDS. Despite the apparent fluctuations, the consistent presence of high numbers of both cases and deaths emphasizes the ongoing public health challenge that AIDS represents in China, necessitating sustained and possibly enhanced public health interventions.
