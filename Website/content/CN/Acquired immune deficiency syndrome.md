# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). HIV damages the immune system, interfering with the body's ability to fight infections and diseases. AIDS is the final stage of HIV infection, occurring when the immune system is severely compromised. Transmission occurs through contact with infected bodily fluids, such as blood, semen, and breast milk. While there is no cure, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can manage the virus, allowing individuals to live longer, healthier lives. Prevention includes safe sex practices, needle exchange programs, and regular testing.

## Highlights



- **Rising Trend**: From 2015 to 2025, AIDS cases and deaths in mainland China generally increased, peaking in late 2018 and 2019. <br/>

- **Seasonal Patterns**: Lower case numbers are often seen early in the year, with increases later, indicating a seasonal pattern. <br/>

- **Recent Decline**: Early 2025 shows a slight decline in cases and deaths compared to previous peaks, suggesting improvements in management or reporting. <br/>

- **Fluctuating Mortality**: The mortality rate has varied, with some months showing higher death rates relative to cases, indicating differences in healthcare access or disease severity. <br/>

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) cases and deaths in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals several trends. Initially, there is a noticeable increase in both cases and deaths from 2015 through 2019. For instance, the number of cases rose from 2,294 in February 2015 to a peak of 7,366 in November 2019, while deaths increased from 624 to 2,245 over the same period. This upward trend could be attributed to various factors, including improved reporting and diagnosis, increased transmission rates, or changes in public health policies and awareness.

From 2020 onwards, the data shows fluctuations with some months experiencing significant spikes, such as December 2021 with 7,490 cases and 2,486 deaths. However, there is a general stabilization in the number of cases and deaths post-2020, with occasional peaks and troughs. This stabilization might reflect the impact of enhanced public health interventions, better access to antiretroviral therapy, and increased public awareness and education about HIV/AIDS prevention. The data for 2023 and 2024 suggests a slight decline in both cases and deaths, indicating potential progress in controlling the epidemic, although the numbers remain significant, underscoring the ongoing public health challenge.

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals several trends in the number of cases and deaths. Initially, there is a noticeable increase in both cases and deaths from 2015 through 2019. For instance, the number of cases rose from 2,294 in February 2015 to a peak of 7,897 in December 2018, while deaths increased from 624 to 2,444 over the same period. This upward trend could be attributed to various factors, including improved reporting mechanisms, increased awareness, and possibly a rise in the actual incidence of the disease.

From 2020 onwards, the data shows fluctuations in the number of cases and deaths, with some months experiencing significant spikes. For example, November 2021 saw 7,490 cases and 2,486 deaths, one of the highest in the dataset. However, there is a general stabilization in the number of cases and deaths from 2022 to 2025, with occasional peaks and troughs. This stabilization might suggest improvements in treatment and management of the disease, as well as the impact of public health interventions. The data for 2023 and 2024 indicates a slight decline in both cases and deaths, which could be a positive sign of effective control measures and better access to antiretroviral therapy.
