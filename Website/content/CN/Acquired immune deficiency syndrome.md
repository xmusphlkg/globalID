# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: December 2025

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic, life-threatening condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). HIV attacks and gradually destroys CD4+ T-cells, a key component of the immune system, increasing vulnerability to opportunistic infections and certain cancers. AIDS represents the most advanced stage of HIV infection, typically defined by a critically low CD4+ cell count or the presence of specific opportunistic diseases. Transmission occurs primarily through unprotected sex, contaminated needles, blood transfusions, and from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding. Early detection and treatment can delay progression.

## Highlights

- **Declining case and death numbers:** Since peaking in 2018–2019, reported cases and deaths from AIDS in Chinese mainland have decreased notably by late 2025, reaching 4,560 cases and 2,027 deaths in December 2025. <br/>
- **Stabilization in recent years:** Monthly new cases now fluctuate between 3,700–4,800, with deaths ranging from 1,400–1,700, suggesting a stabilization in transmission and disease burden compared to earlier years. <br/>
- **Seasonal fluctuations persist:** Lower case and death numbers are typically seen at the year’s start, but overall seasonal trends remain consistent year-to-year. <br/>
- **Persistent public health challenge:** Despite improvements, AIDS remains a significant public health concern, requiring continued prevention and treatment efforts. <br/>

Analysis of monthly AIDS case and death data for mainland China from December 2015 to December 2025 reveals notable trends in incidence and mortality. There is a clear cyclical pattern, with the lowest caseloads routinely observed in January and February, likely influenced by seasonal factors and reporting practices, followed by steady increases in subsequent months and pronounced peaks in late autumn and early winter, especially November and December. The years 2018 to 2021 record the highest monthly case counts, often exceeding 6,000, and deaths at times rising above 2,000, indicating escalation in burden, possibly reflecting improved detection, expanded reporting, or underlying epidemiological growth during that time.

Starting from 2022, both case numbers and deaths show a gradual downward trend with less marked seasonal peaks, suggesting positive impacts from intervention and management programs, or changing transmission dynamics. The cyclical effect persists, but the amplitude diminishes, and post-pandemic changes in 2023 are visible, potentially related to health system adjustments or delayed reporting, such as the notably low case count in January 2023 (1,815 cases). Mortality trends closely paralleled case notifications, underlying persistent clinical challenges. Overall, while seasonal and trend effects point to ongoing transmission and need for vigilance, the more recent decline indicates progress but underscores that AIDS remains a significant health issue requiring sustained public health efforts.

Across a 10-year span, reported deaths due to Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in mainland China have steadily increased until 2021, after which both annual deaths and case counts turned more variable. From 2015 through 2019, monthly AIDS deaths rose from around 1,000–1,500 per month to peaks exceeding 2,000 by late 2018 and through much of 2019. This increase closely parallels rising case reports, with seasonal dips in winter months and surges in late autumn, suggesting factors such as delayed diagnosis, reporting practices, and possibly cyclical access to healthcare or treatment disruptions. The November-December periods recurrently display mortality spikes, which may reflect reporting consolidation at year-end, or increased death following earlier undiagnosed infections.

After 2020, deaths remained elevated, often surpassing 1,700–2,400 monthly, but exhibited greater fluctuations—likely influences from the COVID-19 pandemic, which may have affected HIV care continuity and access to antiretroviral therapy. Notably, the highest monthly deaths occurred in late 2021–early 2022 (up to 2,486 in December 2021) and again in subsequent late-year months, before a downward trend emerged in 2023–2025, where monthly deaths declined to about 1,400–1,700. This recent reduction may signal improvements in diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment adherence, or could reflect changes in surveillance methodology. However, despite the recent decline, the burden of AIDS-related deaths remains persistently high, highlighting ongoing gaps in prevention, late diagnosis, and retention in care for people living with HIV in mainland China.
