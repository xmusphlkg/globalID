# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a life-threatening condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). This virus attacks and weakens the body's immune system, specifically targeting CD4 cells (T cells), reducing the body's ability to combat infections and diseases. HIV is primarily transmitted through unprotected sexual contact, sharing needles, and from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding. While no cure exists, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can effectively manage the condition, allowing individuals to live longer, healthier lives. Early diagnosis and consistent treatment are crucial in preventing the progression of HIV to AIDS.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing Trend in Cases and Deaths:** From 2023 to mid-2025, both cases and deaths have shown a slight overall decline after reaching high levels between 2018-2021, suggesting improved AIDS control measures. <br/>

- **Fluctuations in Monthly Cases:** Monthly cases exhibit fluctuations with peaks notably during transitional months, indicating possible seasonal influences or variations in surveillance and reporting. <br/>

- **Impact of Health Interventions:** The declines post-2021 might reflect successful health interventions, increased awareness, or improved treatment access, potentially reducing transmission and mortality. <br/>

- **Ongoing Challenge:** Despite the downward trend, a substantial number of cases and deaths persist, highlighting the ongoing challenge of AIDS management in the region. <br/>

The AIDS epidemic data from mainland China between July 2015 and July 2025 shows a general increase in reported cases, peaking noticeably at year-end. This trend suggests seasonal fluctuations, possibly due to cyclical reporting practices or targeted health campaigns. Initially, there is growth in cases, increasing from about 4,700 in mid-2015 to over 6,300 by December 2016. This pattern of rising cases towards the end of each year is consistent, possibly reflecting heightened awareness or testing efforts.

In terms of mortality, the data reveals a correlation between elevated case numbers and subsequent increases in deaths. However, mortality rates are more variable with significant peaks and valleys throughout the years. Notable spikes occur towards the year's end, aligning with heightened case numbers. Between November 2018 and December 2021, mortality rates significantly increased, surpassing 2400 at times, indicating possible gaps in treatment or care accessibility or changes in virus virulence.

Towards the end of the observed period, a decline in mortality rates is noted, particularly by mid-2025. This decrease may suggest improvements in healthcare interventions, more effective treatment regimens, or better public health strategies to manage AIDS. Overall, while fluctuations exist, the data highlights a considerable challenge in managing both the case counts and mortality associated with AIDS in mainland China.

Analyzing deaths from Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in the Chinese mainland reveals important trends. Beginning mid-2015, there's variability in monthly deaths, often aligning with case numbers but not perfectly. Seasonal patterns show lower reports early each year, peaking towards year-end, from 2015 through 2019. These trends might reflect factors like reporting practices or socio-economic influences affecting diagnosis timing.

Post-2020, after the initial COVID-19 impact, trends stabilize but with fluctuations. Early 2020 saw fewer cases, yet deaths didn't drop proportionately, possibly due to healthcare disruptions during the pandemic affecting timely diagnosis and treatment. In the following years, despite stable case reports, months like February 2023 show high deaths, pointing to improved diagnosis but challenges in prompt intervention. The later years indicate reduced deaths compared to peaks like 2018, suggesting better public health efforts and access to preventive care.

Overall, these patterns in AIDS-related deaths emphasize the need for ongoing healthcare strategies and government action to safeguard affected populations. Improved awareness, accessibility to treatment, and healthcare services seem to play a pivotal role in the observed mortality reduction. Effective public health policies and interventions remain critical in mitigating AIDS impacts on vulnerable groups across the Chinese mainland.
