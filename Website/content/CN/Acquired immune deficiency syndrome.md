# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). HIV attacks the body's immune system, specifically the CD4 cells (T cells), weakening the immune response and making individuals more susceptible to infections and certain cancers. AIDS is the most severe phase of HIV infection, characterized by a significantly compromised immune system and the occurrence of opportunistic infections or cancers. Without treatment, HIV can progress to AIDS in several years, but antiretroviral therapy can effectively manage the virus, allowing individuals to lead healthy lives.

## Highlights

- **Fluctuating Case Numbers:** From 2015 to 2025, AIDS cases in mainland China fluctuated significantly, peaking in December each year, with the highest recorded in December 2018 at 7,897 cases. <br/>

- **Mortality Trends:** Deaths generally mirrored case trends, peaking in November and December, with the highest monthly deaths in December 2018 at 2,444. <br/>

- **Recent Decline:** As of January 2025, there is a decline in both cases (2,832) and deaths (1,375), indicating potential improvements in disease management. <br/>

- **Seasonal Patterns:** A recurring pattern shows lower cases and deaths in early months, suggesting seasonal influences on transmission. <br/>

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in the Chinese mainland reveals a concerning trend over the years, particularly from 2015 to 2025. Initially, there was a significant increase in reported cases, peaking in December 2018 with 7,897 cases. This spike may indicate a combination of factors, including improved testing and reporting mechanisms, as well as a potential rise in transmission rates. The mortality rates, while fluctuating, also show a troubling pattern, with the highest number of deaths recorded in December 2018 at 2,467. This suggests that despite advancements in treatment and awareness, the disease continues to pose a serious public health challenge.

In the subsequent years, particularly from 2019 to 2024, the data indicates a more stabilized but still high number of cases and deaths. The monthly case counts generally hover around the 5,000 mark, with notable peaks in certain months. The death toll, while showing some fluctuations, remains significant, indicating that many individuals are still succumbing to the disease. The data from 2023 and 2024 suggests a slight decline in cases compared to the peak years, but the persistent number of deaths highlights the ongoing need for effective interventions, public health strategies, and continued education to combat the spread of HIV/AIDS in the region.

The data on Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 shows significant trends in reported cases and deaths. Initially, there was a marked increase in both metrics from 2015 through 2019, with deaths rising from 886 in January 2015 to 2284 in December 2019. This increase may reflect higher transmission rates or improved reporting mechanisms. Seasonal fluctuations are also evident, with higher cases and deaths typically reported in the latter months of each year, suggesting potential seasonal influences on transmission.

From 2020 onwards, the data presents a more complex picture, likely impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Early 2020 saw a dip in reported cases and deaths, possibly due to healthcare disruptions. However, by 2021, numbers began to rise again, peaking in December 2021 with 2486 deaths. The following years exhibit fluctuations, indicating that AIDS continues to pose a significant public health challenge. The data for 2024 and early 2025 suggests a slight decline in deaths, which may indicate improved access to treatment or prevention strategies. Nonetheless, further analysis is necessary to confirm these trends and understand the underlying factors influencing the data.
