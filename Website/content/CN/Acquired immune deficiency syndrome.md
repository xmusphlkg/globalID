# Acquired immune deficiency syndrome

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a life-threatening condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). HIV targets and weakens the body's immune system, specifically by attacking CD4 cells (T cells), leading to a diminished ability to combat infections and diseases. As HIV progresses, the immune system is severely compromised, leading to AIDS, the most advanced stage of the infection. Transmission occurs through contact with infected bodily fluids, such as blood, semen, and breast milk. While there's no cure for HIV/AIDS, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can manage the virus and help individuals lead healthier lives.

## Highlights

1. **Steady Increase in AIDS Cases**: The number of reported AIDS cases in China has risen from 4,464 in November 2014 to 4,760 in November 2024, indicating an ongoing rise in the disease burden.<br/>

2. **Relatively Stable Death Rates**: While cases have increased, deaths have remained steady, with 1,763 deaths recorded in November 2024.<br/>

3. **Seasonal Variations**: Both cases and deaths tend to peak in the winter months (November-December).<br/>

4. **Increasing Mortality in Recent Years**: Mortality rates have gradually increased, with 2023 and 2024 showing higher death counts, particularly in the later months.<br/>

The data on AIDS cases in mainland China from 2014 to November 2024 shows a steady increase in both reported cases and deaths, with notable fluctuations. There was a sharp rise in cases and deaths around late 2018, with December 2018 recording the highest numbers: 7,897 cases and 2,444 deaths. This surge could reflect better reporting or a genuine increase in transmission, influenced by social, behavioral, and healthcare factors. 

The case-to-death ratio fluctuates, indicating that while both cases and deaths have risen, mortality remains a persistent challenge. Notably, deaths tend to peak mid- to late-year, particularly in July and November, suggesting delays in treatment or the emergence of complications. Despite these spikes, there is evidence of improvements in treatment and healthcare systems, as seen in the lower death rates in 2024 compared to 2018-2019. 

While the overall trend is concerning, the data highlights the need for continued investment in prevention, awareness, and healthcare infrastructure to reduce both the incidence of new cases and the associated mortality. The fluctuating figures underscore the complexity of managing AIDS in China, where both progress and challenges coexist.
