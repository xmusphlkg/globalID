# Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis (AHC) is a highly contagious eye infection characterized by sudden onset of redness, swelling, and bleeding in the conjunctiva, the membrane covering the white part of the eye. It is primarily caused by enteroviruses, such as Enterovirus 70 and Coxsackievirus A24. Symptoms include eye pain, excessive tearing, and sensitivity to light. AHC spreads rapidly through direct contact with infected individuals or contaminated surfaces. While the condition is self-limiting, typically resolving within a week, maintaining good hygiene and avoiding close contact with infected individuals are crucial to prevent outbreaks.

## Highlights

- A significant surge in cases was observed in July 2023, with 13,425 reported cases, marking the highest incidence in the dataset. <br/>
- The overall trend shows a gradual decline in cases from 2023 to January 2025, with 1,340 cases reported in January 2025. <br/>
- Throughout the years, there have been no reported deaths, indicating a low mortality rate associated with Acute Hemorrhagic Conjunctivitis in the Chinese mainland. <br/>
- Seasonal fluctuations are evident, with peaks typically occurring in the summer months, particularly from June to August. <br/>

The data on Acute Hemorrhagic Conjunctivitis (AHC) in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals several notable trends. Initially, the number of cases shows a seasonal pattern, with peaks typically occurring in the summer months, particularly from June to September. This pattern is consistent with the known epidemiology of AHC, which often sees increased transmission during warmer months due to factors such as increased outdoor activities and higher humidity, which can facilitate the spread of the virus. The data from 2015 to 2019 shows a gradual increase in cases, with a significant peak in July 2019, where cases reached 5,265. This period also marks the first recorded deaths in August 2019 and February 2020, although the overall mortality rate remains extremely low.

A dramatic surge in cases is observed in 2023, with an unprecedented spike in September, recording 125,264 cases. This surge could be attributed to several factors, including potential changes in the virus's transmissibility, public health measures, or reporting practices. The subsequent months show a sharp decline, suggesting effective intervention measures or natural epidemic burnout. The data from 2024 onwards indicates a return to the previous seasonal pattern, albeit with lower case numbers, suggesting that the 2023 spike was an anomaly rather than a new trend. Overall, the data highlights the importance of continuous monitoring and preparedness to manage sudden outbreaks effectively.

The data for Acute Hemorrhagic Conjunctivitis (AHC) in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 shows a consistent pattern of cases with very few deaths reported. Over the years, the number of cases fluctuates, with notable peaks in the summer months, which is typical for viral conjunctivitis outbreaks. The data indicates a significant spike in cases in 2023, particularly in September, with over 125,000 cases reported, yet no deaths were recorded during this period. This suggests that while AHC can spread rapidly and affect a large number of individuals, it remains a non-lethal condition in the vast majority of cases.

The few deaths reported, specifically in August 2019 and February 2020, are outliers in the dataset. These isolated incidents could be attributed to complications in individuals with pre-existing health conditions or other factors unrelated to the typical course of AHC. The absence of deaths in subsequent years, despite high case numbers, reinforces the understanding that AHC is generally not life-threatening. The data underscores the importance of public health measures to control the spread of AHC, especially during peak seasons, while also highlighting the need for further investigation into the rare instances of mortality associated with the disease.
