# Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis (AHC) is a highly contagious viral eye infection characterized by sudden onset of symptoms including redness, swelling, excessive tearing, and subconjunctival hemorrhage, leading to visible blood spots on the whites of the eyes. It is primarily caused by enteroviruses, particularly Coxsackievirus A24 and Enterovirus 70. The disease is known for spreading rapidly in crowded conditions via direct contact with eye secretions or contaminated surfaces. AHC usually resolves within a week without serious complications, though supportive treatments like artificial tears and cool compresses can alleviate discomfort. Proper hygiene is crucial in preventing its spread.

## Highlights

- **Seasonal Pattern:** AHC consistently peaks from June to September, with higher incidence in summer and early fall months. <br/>

- **Outbreak Highlight:** A massive outbreak occurred in 2023, with a record 125,264 cases in September aloneâ€”far exceeding trends from previous years. <br/>

- **Mortality:** The disease has extremely low fatality, with only 3 deaths reported between 2015 and 2025, despite high case numbers. <br/>

- **Recent Status:** As of March 2025, reported cases (2,044) remain low and consistent with pre-2023 levels, indicating a return to baseline activity. Continued monitoring is advised ahead of the typical seasonal peak. <br/>

Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis (AHC) incidence in mainland China demonstrates notable fluctuations over the observed period, with a pronounced annual cycle characterized by elevated case numbers in summer months, typically peaking in July and August. For example, 2015 to 2017 exhibit peaks in July, followed by a reduction in cases towards the winter months. However, a significant spike is observed in 2019 and beyond, culminating in record-breaking numbers in July, August, and September 2023, indicating possible outbreaks or a change in reporting accuracy or health practices.

Despite the episodic increases, it is important to note the consistent absence of deaths over most of the years, with rare occurrences in August 2019 and February 2020. The data appears to reflect a high incidence of AHC but with minimal mortality, suggesting standard treatments and preventive measures are largely effective. In the context of public health, the elevated case numbers in recent years, particularly 2023, necessitate further investigation into environmental, viral, or sociological factors contributing to this sharp rise, as well as reinforcing preventive strategies to curb the spread during anticipated high-incidence months.

The first death occurred in August 2019, associated with just one fatality amid 4,208 reported cases, followed by two deaths in February 2020 with a notably smaller case count of 1,672. This highly skewed mortality-to-case ratio suggests that AHC, while widely prevalent, is largely non-life-threatening in this region. The spike in cases and isolated mortalities might reflect local epidemiological dynamics, healthcare accessibility, or surveillance specificity during those months.

Significant peaks in case numbers are observed, especially in mid-2023, with figures like 125,264 cases reported in September with no resultant fatalities, reinforcing the non-lethal nature of the condition despite its potential outbreaks. Seasonal fluctuations in case numbers are apparent, likely influenced by environmental factors, population density during specific months, or perhaps increased transmissibility under varying climatic conditions.
