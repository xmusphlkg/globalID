# Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis (AHC) is a rapidly spreading viral eye infection characterized by sudden onset of conjunctival redness, swelling, pain, photophobia, and a watery discharge. It is primarily caused by enterovirus 70 and coxsackievirus A24 variants. The condition frequently leads to subconjunctival hemorrhage, resulting in bright red eye appearance. AHC is highly contagious, predominantly affecting tropical and subtropical regions, and can lead to large outbreaks due to its person-to-person transmission via direct contact or contaminated surfaces. While usually self-limiting, symptoms typically resolve within one to two weeks without serious long-term effects.

## Highlights

- Seasonal peaks observed in summer months, notably in July and August, with an alarming surge in July-October 2023, peaking at September with 125,264 cases. <br/>
- A substantial decline in case numbers after October 2023 is evident, with a consistent decrease maintaining until July 2024, signaling potential stabilization. <br/>
- Mortality remains low, with only three deaths recorded over the entire observed period, underscoring a generally non-lethal nature of the disease. <br/>
- The recent reduction in case numbers from the 2023 peak suggests effective control measures or changes in environmental factors contributing to current containment. <br/>

The data on acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis (AHC) in mainland China from mid-2014 to mid-2024 shows significant seasonal patterns, with cases peaking in summer months, particularly July and August. This trend is consistent across the years, reflecting the seasonal nature of AHC, which thrives in warmer, humid conditions that facilitate the spread of the virus.

A notable anomaly occurs in September 2019, with a sharp increase in cases, but the most significant surge happens in 2023. In September 2023, cases spiked dramatically to 125,264, a stark contrast to previous years. This unprecedented surge could be due to factors such as an emerging viral strain, increased post-pandemic interactions, or environmental changes that enhanced virus transmission. Despite this surge, the mortality rate remained low, consistent with AHC being generally non-fatal.

Following the 2023 spike, case numbers returned to more typical levels by mid-2024, suggesting effective interventions or a natural resolution to the outbreak. This pattern highlights the importance of continued monitoring and preparedness to manage such outbreaks effectively.

This indicates high transmissibility or periodic outbreaks, which the public health system appears to handle effectively. The absence of deaths outside the brief period in late 2019 and early 2020 reinforces the perception of acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis as a condition more remarkable for its incidence variability than for lethality.

Overall, these findings emphasize the importance of maintaining vigilant monitoring and swift outbreak management to handle acute hemorrhagic conjunctivitis effectively.
