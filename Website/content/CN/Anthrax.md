# Anthrax

Version: October 2025

## Introduction

Anthrax is an acute infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis, which forms resilient spores. It primarily affects herbivorous animals but can also infect humans who come into contact with contaminated animal products. There are three main forms of the disease in humans: cutaneous, inhalation, and gastrointestinal, with inhalation being the most deadly. Anthrax spores can be found in soil and can survive for decades. Symptoms vary depending on the route of infection but often include fever, chills, and swelling. Prompt treatment with antibiotics and vaccination can prevent severe health outcomes. Anthrax is also considered a potential bioterrorism threat.

## Highlights

- **Consistent Seasonal Outbreaks**: June to October show consistently higher case numbers, with a pattern of increase each year, peaking in August. <br/>
- **Fluctuating Incidence**: The number of cases shows substantial variation over the years, with peaks in 2018, 2021, and 2025, especially during summer months. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate**: Despite fluctuations in case numbers, deaths remain relatively low, underlining effective management and treatment strategies. <br/>
- **Rising Case Trend in Recent Years**: From 2023 onwards, there is a notable upward trend in the number of cases, suggesting emerging challenges in controlling outbreaks. <br/>

The anthrax case data from mainland China between October 2015 and October 2025 show variable incidence rates with distinct seasonal peaks. August repeatedly records the highest number of cases: 115 in 2021, 123 in 2023, and 106 in 2025, suggesting seasonal influences, possibly related to agricultural practices or livestock interaction patterns during summer months. The timing of these peaks might be linked to grazing patterns or other environmental factors that heighten exposure to anthrax spores.

Mortality related to anthrax remains relatively low across the dataset, with most months reporting zero deaths, indicating generally effective case management and healthcare interventions. However, certain months such as August 2018 with two deaths and July 2023 with one death show sporadic increases in fatalities. This could suggest outbreaks of more virulent strains or localized challenges in healthcare response during peak periods. The presence of deaths in high-case months could reflect increased challenge in treating cases swiftly to prevent mortality.

Despite the fluctuations in case numbers and occasional spikes in fatalities, the data underscores anthrax's manageable yet persistent threat in China. Enhanced vigilance and preventive measures during high-incidence months are crucial to controlling transmission and reducing fatalities. This highlights the need for continuous public health efforts and case surveillance, especially in times and areas of heightened risk, to effectively manage and mitigate anthrax impact.

Notably, higher case numbers often occur during summer months, such as in June and August, suggesting possible seasonal or environmental influences on anthrax transmission and severity. From 2021 onwards, there is an observable pattern of deaths clustering towards the latter months of the year, although the total number of deaths remains low throughout the period.
