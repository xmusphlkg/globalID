# Anthrax

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis. Primarily affecting herbivorous animals such as cattle, sheep, and goats, it can also infect humans through exposure to contaminated animal products or direct contact. The disease manifests in several forms—cutaneous, inhalational, gastrointestinal, and injectional—each with distinct symptoms. Cutaneous anthrax is most common, leading to skin sores, while inhalational anthrax is the deadliest form, characterized by severe respiratory distress. Anthrax spores can survive in harsh conditions, making the pathogen a potential bioterrorism threat. Early diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics are essential for effective management.

## Highlights

- Anthrax incidence in the Chinese mainland has shown seasonal peaks around August in most years, with notable spikes in August 2023, August 2024, and August 2025. <br/>
- There is a fluctuating trend in reported cases, with a gradual increase in recent months leading to a high of 106 cases in August 2025. <br/>
- Mortality remains relatively low with sporadic increases, such as a recorded death in August 2025, despite increasing case numbers. <br/>
- Overall, the period from 2023 to 2025 indicates a rising trend in cases, necessitating enhanced public health interventions and monitoring efforts. <br/>

The data from 2015 to 2025 shows the seasonal pattern of Anthrax cases in mainland China, highlighting a consistent increase during summer months, notably in July and August. For example, August each year from 2021 to 2024 saw a sharp rise in cases, peaking at 123 in August 2023. This pattern likely stems from climatic and agricultural factors, as encounters with livestock and contaminated environments rise during this season. While case numbers fluctuate annually, there's no clear upward or downward trend, implying that anthrax remains a persistent but controlled challenge.

Mortality from anthrax remains low, with sporadic deaths across the timeline. Some years, like 2020, witnessed no deaths, hinting at efficient health interventions or timely medical treatment. Though case numbers vary significantly each year, the absence of concentrated fatalities indicates that while the infection rate might rise, fatal outcomes are infrequent, likely due to robust healthcare responses.

Overall, the data suggest anthrax transmission has periodic spikes but remains largely manageable. This underscores the significance of preventive strategies and readiness during peak seasons to mitigate outbreaks. Despite the rise in infection rates during certain months, effective public health measures seem to keep mortality rates steady and low, ensuring that the disease's impact on the population remains limited.

The reported anthrax data from the Chinese mainland from 2015 to August 2025 shows a pattern of sporadic cases with occasional fatalities. The total number of deaths within this timeframe is notably low compared to the number of cases, indicating a relatively low case fatality rate. Most of the months report zero deaths, with occasional months showing one or two fatalities, suggesting that while anthrax cases do occur, they infrequently result in death. The data indicates that fatalities are not consistently associated with the months with the highest number of reported cases, suggesting that factors other than pure case numbers might influence mortality, such as variations in outbreak severity, healthcare access, or timely treatment interventions.

An analysis of the yearly trends shows that there are no significant upward or downward trends in mortality, despite fluctuations in the number of cases. Certain periods, such as August 2018, 2021, and 2022, display peaks in case numbers with some associated deaths. The year 2024 appears to have a slightly higher number of monthly fatalities compared to previous years, though the increase is not dramatic. Overall, the data suggests a consistent but manageable public health challenge, with a focus potentially needed on understanding the underlying causes of any uptick in fatalities during specific years, ensuring timely health responses, and effective management strategies to maintain low mortality rates despite fluctuations in case numbers.
