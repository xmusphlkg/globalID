# Anthrax

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis. It primarily affects herbivorous animals, but humans can become infected through direct or indirect contact with sick animals. There are three main forms of anthrax: cutaneous, inhalation, and gastrointestinal, each with varying symptoms and severity. Cutaneous anthrax, the most common form, occurs through skin contact and is characterized by a black sore. Inhalation anthrax, the most severe form, results from breathing in spores and can be fatal without prompt treatment. Vaccines and antibiotics are effective preventive and treatment measures.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers**: From 2015 to 2025, anthrax cases have increased, with peaks in July and August, indicating a seasonal pattern likely linked to agriculture. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate**: Despite rising cases, the mortality rate remains low, with occasional deaths, suggesting effective treatment and management. <br/>

- **Recent Stability**: In the past two years, case numbers have stabilized, and deaths have slightly decreased, indicating improved control measures. <br/>

- **Targeted Interventions Needed**: The data highlights the need for focused interventions during peak months and in high-incidence areas to further reduce cases and mortality. <br/>

The anthrax data from mainland China (2015-2025) shows seasonal peaks, particularly in July and August, aligning with agricultural activities and livestock handling. For example, August 2023 saw the highest cases at 123. This seasonal pattern suggests a link between anthrax outbreaks and environmental or occupational factors during warmer months.

Mortality rates remain low relative to case numbers, indicating effective medical interventions. However, occasional increases in deaths, such as in June 2017 and August 2022, suggest the need for further investigation into specific circumstances or strains. Over the years, there is a gradual increase in cases, with notable spikes in 2021 and 2023. These spikes could result from changes in reporting, increased awareness, or actual transmission increases due to environmental or socio-economic factors.

Overall, the data highlights the importance of continuous monitoring and targeted public health strategies to manage anthrax outbreaks effectively. The consistent seasonal pattern and low mortality rate suggest that while anthrax remains a public health concern, current interventions are largely effective. However, the occasional rise in deaths and case spikes indicate areas where public health responses could be strengthened, particularly in understanding and mitigating the factors contributing to these increases.

The data on anthrax cases and deaths in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals a relatively low mortality rate despite fluctuations in the number of cases. Over the ten-year period, the number of cases varied significantly, with peaks observed in the summer months, particularly in July and August. This seasonal pattern suggests a possible link to environmental factors or agricultural practices, as anthrax is often associated with livestock and can be influenced by climatic conditions.

Despite the variability in case numbers, the death toll remained low, with only sporadic fatalities reported. The highest number of deaths in a single month was two, occurring in June 2017, December 2017, August 2018, and August 2022. This low mortality rate could be attributed to effective public health interventions, timely medical treatment, and possibly the nature of the cases, which may have been predominantly cutaneous anthrax, a less lethal form of the disease. The data underscores the importance of continued surveillance and preventive measures, particularly in high-risk periods and regions, to maintain control over anthrax outbreaks and minimize fatalities.
