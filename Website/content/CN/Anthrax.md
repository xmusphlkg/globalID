# Anthrax

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium *Bacillus anthracis*. It primarily affects herbivorous mammals but can also infect humans. The bacteria form spores that can survive in harsh conditions for years. There are three forms of anthrax: cutaneous, inhalational, and gastrointestinal, each varying in severity and mode of transmission. Cutaneous anthrax, the most common form, occurs through skin contact with spores, while inhalational and gastrointestinal anthrax result from inhaling spores or consuming contaminated food. Prompt antibiotic treatment is crucial for effective management and recovery.

## Highlights

- **Increasing Trend**: Anthrax cases have shown a generally increasing trend from 2014 to 2024, with peak numbers observed in summer months, particularly in July and August. <br/>
- **Seasonal Peaks**: The highest number of cases consistently occur in the summer months, suggesting a strong seasonal pattern in anthrax incidence. <br/>
- **Mortality Rates**: Deaths have remained relatively low compared to the number of cases, indicating improved management and treatment, although sporadic increases in mortality are noted. <br/>
- **Current Situation**: As of June 2024, there have been 41 cases reported with no deaths, reflecting a moderate increase compared to the same period in previous years. <br/>

The reported data for Anthrax in the Chinese mainland from 2014 to 2024 reveals significant variability in the number of cases and deaths over the years. Initial years (2014-2016) showed a relatively low and fluctuating number of cases, with notable peaks in the summer months. For instance, the highest monthly case count within this period occurred in August 2016 with 87 cases and one death. Similarly, July 2016 had a spike with 65 cases but no deaths, indicating potential seasonal trends in the incidence of Anthrax.

From 2017 to 2024, there was a discernible increase in the frequency of high case counts, particularly during the summer months. Noteworthy spikes include August 2017 with 78 cases, August 2018 with 103 cases, and August 2023 with 123 cases. The annual trend seems to peak consistently in the warmer months, suggesting a possible correlation with environmental or agricultural factors prevalent during these periods. The overall mortality rate remained low, with sporadic deaths reported, indicating that while Anthrax cases surged intermittently, the lethality did not show a parallel increase, possibly reflecting improvements in medical response and treatment protocols.

The data shows fluctuations in the number of cases each month, with noticeable peaks in certain months, such as August 2018 and August 2023, which saw 103 and 123 cases, respectively. Despite these peaks in cases, the number of deaths did not correlate directly with the number of cases, indicating effective medical interventions or variations in the virulence of the outbreaks.

A closer examination of the deaths reveals a few sporadic months with fatalities, often without a consistent pattern. For instance, August 2014 and June 2017 had lower numbers of cases compared to other peak months but resulted in deaths. This sporadic pattern suggests that while the overall case-fatality ratio is low, individual outbreaks can still result in deaths, possibly due to delays in treatment or specific circumstances surrounding those cases. The data underscores the importance of continuous surveillance and rapid response to outbreaks to maintain the low mortality rate observed.
