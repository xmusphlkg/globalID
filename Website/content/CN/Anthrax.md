# Anthrax

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis. It primarily affects herbivorous animals, but humans can become infected through direct or indirect contact with sick animals. The disease manifests in three forms: cutaneous, inhalational, and gastrointestinal, depending on the route of exposure. Cutaneous anthrax, the most common form, occurs through skin contact with contaminated materials, while inhalational anthrax results from inhaling spores, and gastrointestinal anthrax follows ingestion of contaminated meat. Prompt diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics are crucial, as inhalational anthrax can be fatal if not treated early. Vaccines are available for high-risk groups.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers:** Anthrax cases in the Chinese mainland have generally increased from 2014 to 2024, with significant peaks in August 2018, August 2021, and August 2023. <br/>
- **Seasonal Patterns:** There is a consistent rise in cases during summer months, particularly in July and August, indicating a seasonal trend. <br/>
- **Low Mortality:** Deaths remain low relative to cases, though 2024 has seen a slight increase in fatalities. <br/>
- **Current Situation:** The latter half of 2024 has experienced a notable rise in cases, suggesting a need for improved surveillance and control measures. <br/>

The data on anthrax cases in mainland China from December 2014 to December 2024 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, the number of cases was relatively low, with a gradual increase observed from mid-2015. Notably, there were significant spikes in cases during the summer months, particularly in July and August, which could be attributed to environmental factors or increased human-animal interactions during these periods. For instance, August 2018 and August 2021 saw particularly high case numbers, with 103 and 115 cases, respectively. This seasonal pattern suggests a potential link to agricultural activities or climatic conditions that favor the spread of anthrax.

The mortality rate remained low throughout the period, with sporadic deaths reported. The data indicates that while the number of cases fluctuated, the number of deaths did not correlate directly with the peaks in case numbers. This could imply effective medical interventions or variations in the virulence of the anthrax strains. The highest number of deaths in a single month was two, occurring in June 2017, August 2018, and August 2022. The consistent low mortality rate suggests that while anthrax remains a public health concern, the healthcare system's response has been largely effective in managing the disease. Continued monitoring and targeted interventions during peak months could further mitigate the impact of anthrax in the region.
