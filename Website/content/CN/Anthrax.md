# Anthrax

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Anthrax is an acute infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis. It commonly affects herbivorous mammals but can also infect humans through direct or indirect contact with contaminated animals or their products. The bacterium forms durable spores that can survive in harsh environmental conditions for years. Anthrax manifests in three primary forms: cutaneous, inhalational, and gastrointestinal, each varying in lethality and symptoms. Prompt diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics are crucial to managing the disease. Due to its potential as a bioterrorism agent, anthrax remains a significant concern in public health and biosecurity.

## Highlights

**Key Epidemiological Trends and Current Anthrax Situation in Chinese Mainland (as of May 2024):**<br/>

1. **Seasonal Peaks:** Cases spike in the summer months (July-August), with the highest being 123 cases in August 2023 and 115 in August 2021. <br/>

2. **Mortality Trend:** Death rates are relatively low but consistent, with an average of 1-2 deaths in high-incidence months, totaling 15 deaths from 2014 to May 2024. <br/>

3. **Overall Incidence:** Incidence is increasing over time, with higher peaks in recent years (e.g., 123 cases in August 2023 compared to earlier years). <br/>

4. **Monthly Variability:** Case numbers show substantial monthly variability, indicating sporadic outbreaks rather than consistent year-round transmission. <br/>

The Anthrax case data from the Chinese mainland from May 2014 to May 2024 indicates seasonal trends, with cases peaking in summer, especially from June to September. For example, August 2023 saw the highest peak with 123 cases. This reoccurring seasonal surge suggests environmental factors or increased human activities during summer may raise Anthrax exposure risks, necessitating targeted public health strategies during these months.

Anthrax-related fatalities are infrequent and show no clear seasonal pattern, hinting at effective treatment methods keeping mortality low despite occasional spikes in cases. Notable instances of fatalities include July 2014, December 2015, and August of various years, such as 2016, 2018, and 2021. The sporadic deaths against the backdrop of relatively high case counts underscore the importance of immediate medical response and understanding the dynamics contributing to these fatal cases.
