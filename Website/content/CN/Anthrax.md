# Anthrax

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by *Bacillus anthracis*, a spore-forming bacterium. It primarily affects livestock but can also infect humans through direct contact with infected animals or contaminated products. The disease manifests in three forms: cutaneous, inhalational, and gastrointestinal. Symptoms vary by form but can include fever, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Inhalational anthrax is particularly severe and often fatal without prompt treatment. Anthrax can be used as a bioterrorism agent due to its ability to form resilient spores. Vaccines and antibiotics can prevent or treat infection if administered early.

## Highlights

1. **Seasonal peaks**: Anthrax cases are highest in late summer, particularly in July, August, and September, with notable peaks in 2021 (115 cases) and 2024 (91 cases).<br/>

2. **Low mortality**: Fatalities are rare, with only 6 deaths reported since 2014, the most recent in May 2024.<br/>

3. **Fluctuating case numbers**: Monthly cases vary but typically range between 20 and 60, with no clear long-term trend.<br/>

4. **Localized outbreaks**: Cases tend to occur in localized clusters, especially during the summer months, without widespread national outbreaks.<br/>

The incidence of anthrax in the Chinese mainland from 2014 to 2024 shows notable seasonal peaks, especially during the warmer months (June to September). These spikes are likely tied to increased agricultural activities, where direct contact with livestock or contaminated products is more common. While case numbers fluctuate, 2015 and 2021 stand out with particularly high counts, reaching over 100 cases in certain months. However, the overall mortality remains low, with only a few deaths recorded each year, suggesting that control measures, such as vaccination and early treatment, have been effective.

Notably, there are occasional outbreaks with higher severity, such as in August 2016 and 2021, which resulted in a small number of deaths. Despite these variations, the overall pattern indicates a relatively controlled situation, with no major sustained outbreaks. The consistency in the low fatality rate points to successful medical interventions and preventive measures, especially in rural areas with higher risks. Though anthrax remains a public health concern in certain agricultural regions, the data shows that with proper management, the impact of the disease remains limited, especially in terms of mortality.

The reported data on anthrax-related deaths in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 shows that fatalities have remained rare, despite periodic outbreaks. Most months throughout the years reported no deaths, even during periods of higher case numbers. The first recorded death occurred in December 2015, followed by occasional fatalities in subsequent years, including one in May and another in August 2016. The highest mortality rates were observed in June and December 2017, with two deaths each. Overall, the death rate appears to be consistently below 1% of reported cases, indicating that while anthrax outbreaks occurred regularly, they did not typically result in high fatalities.

Fatalities were sporadic, with no clear seasonal or geographic trend linking death to specific outbreaks. For example, both 2018 and 2024 saw fatalities during months with high case counts (103 cases in August 2018 and 91 in August 2024), but these did not correspond to a particular season or region. The occasional deaths, even during larger outbreaks, suggest that while outbreaks may be linked to increased case numbers, other factors—such as early detection and treatment—likely contributed to preventing widespread fatalities. The overall low death rate over time may reflect improved public health measures or more effective management of cases.
