# Anthrax

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Anthrax is an infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis, affecting mainly herbivorous animals, with humans contracting it through contact with infected animals. The bacterium forms resilient spores that can endure harsh conditions for years. There are four anthrax types: cutaneous, inhalation, gastrointestinal, and injectional, each with distinct transmission modes and severity. Symptoms range from skin lesions to severe respiratory issues. Early diagnosis and antibiotic treatment are vital for recovery. Due to its lethality and durable spores, anthrax is considered a potential bioterrorism threat, necessitating vigilance and preparedness in public health strategies.

## Highlights

- **Peak Incidences**: The data consistently shows higher Anthrax case surges between July and September each year, with notable peaks in August. <br/>
- **Mortality Trends**: Despite variable case numbers, deaths remain relatively low, often reaching zero or one per month. <br/>
- **Seasonal Pattern**: A noticeable seasonal pattern emerges, with higher cases in summer months (June to August) and reduced cases in colder months. <br/>
- **Recent Situation**: As of July 2024, there is a continuation of this trend with cases reaching 56, indicating vigilance is needed during peak months while maintaining effective preventive measures. <br/>

From 2014 to 2024, anthrax cases in mainland China show seasonal patterns with notable surges, particularly in the summer months of July and August. The data indicates that cases often peak during these months, possibly due to increased agricultural activities, as anthrax is commonly associated with livestock. Peaks in cases are observed in August 2018 (103 cases), August 2021 (115 cases), and August 2023 (123 cases), suggesting a periodic rise that may be tied to environmental or human factors.

Despite fluctuating case numbers, the mortality rate remains low relative to the number of infections. Deaths are sporadic, with most years recording zero fatalities. Notable exceptions occur during or after peak months, such as in August 2016 and August 2018, where increased cases may have led to a slight rise in deaths. This pattern suggests that while anthrax outbreaks can surge, the fatality rate does not consistently increase, possibly due to effective medical interventions or less virulent outbreaks.

Overall, while there is a clear seasonal trend in anthrax cases with occasional spikes, the low fatality rates indicate effective disease management and medical response. Continued surveillance and preventive measures are crucial to maintain low mortality and control the spread, especially during the high-risk months.

The data on anthrax cases and associated deaths in mainland China from July 2014 to July 2024 reveals an overall low mortality rate, despite fluctuations in the number of reported cases over this period. In examining the monthly dataset, there is a sporadic occurrence of deaths, often isolated to single incidents or limited to two deaths in a month. Notably, the years with slightly higher case counts, like 2015, 2016, 2017, and particularly in 2018 and 2021, do not consistently correspond to an increased number of deaths, which suggests a disconnect between the rise in cases and fatalities. In some years, such as 2020, we observe no reported deaths despite a regular reporting of cases, indicating potential effective interventions or treatments during these months.

The temporal distribution of anthrax fatalities appears relatively disjointed from the overall case trends. Peaks in cases like in August 2023 and July 2021 were accompanied by a death but did not show a proportional increase in fatalities relative to the number of cases. This pattern could point toward advances in healthcare responses or public health interventions effectively mitigating death despite case occurrences. Furthermore, the consistency in months reporting zero deaths underscores an encouraging trend of controlling anthrax impacts, suggesting that systemic health strategies, possibly along with public education and prompt case management, play a significant role in reducing the mortality impact amidst varying case volumes.
