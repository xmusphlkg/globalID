# Anthrax

Version: June 2025

## Introduction

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis, a gram-positive, rod-shaped bacterium. It primarily affects herbivorous animals such as cattle, sheep, and goats but can also infect humans who come into contact with contaminated animal products. The disease can manifest in cutaneous, inhalational, gastrointestinal, and injection forms, each varying in severity. Human infection often results from occupational exposure and can lead to severe illness or death if untreated. Symptoms depend on the route of entry but may include blisters, fever, respiratory distress, and vomiting. Anthrax can be controlled through vaccination and appropriate handling of potentially infected animals.

## Highlights

- Anthrax cases peak during summer months, notably from July to September, suggesting a seasonal pattern in transmissions. <br/>
- Since 2015, there have been periodic surges, with notable increases in August each year, peaking at 123 cases in 2023. <br/>
- Despite these spikes, mortality rates are low and sporadic, highlighting effective control measures, though vigilance against outbreaks remains critical. <br/>
- As of June 2025, there is a moderate rise in cases compared to previous months, underscoring the need for continued monitoring and preventive actions as summer approaches. <br/>

The data on anthrax cases in mainland China from mid-2015 to mid-2025 reveals fluctuating incidence patterns with seasonal trends and periodic spikes. Initially, in 2015, cases increased steadily, peaking in August with 60 cases before a gradual decline by year-end. A seasonal trend emerges, with cases often rising during the summer months (June to September), especially notable in August, such as in 2016 (87 cases) and 2018 (103 cases). These peaks suggest potential seasonal influences, possibly related to environmental factors or human activities.

Throughout the period, deaths due to anthrax remain relatively low compared to cases, indicating effective management or treatment protocols. Deaths appear sporadically, often aligning with high-incidence months, such as December 2015, May 2016, August 2016, and June 2017, indicating timing might follow or slightly lag peak case months. After 2020, annual high case counts are noted, with significant peaks continuing in summer months, like August 2021 (115 cases) and August 2023 (123 cases). Despite isolated higher counts, an upward trend in general incidence is mildly observable over time, underscoring ongoing challenges in controlling anthrax spread and highlighting the need for sustained surveillance and preventive measures. The consistent presence of cases underlines the importance of addressing factors contributing to persistence and seasonal surges.
