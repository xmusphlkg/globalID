# Brucellosis

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Brucellosis is a contagious bacterial infection caused by the Brucella species. It primarily affects livestock such as cattle, goats, and sheep, and can be transmitted to humans through direct contact with infected animals or consumption of contaminated animal products. Symptoms in humans include fever, sweats, malaise, anorexia, headache, and muscle pain. Chronic infection can lead to more severe complications like arthritis and endocarditis. Brucellosis is a significant zoonotic disease with considerable impact on public health and the agricultural economy, particularly in regions where livestock farming is prevalent.

## Highlights

- The number of Brucellosis cases in mainland China shows a seasonal pattern, peaking mostly in the spring and summer months. This trend has persisted consistently across the years from 2014 to 2024. <br/>
- There is a general increasing trend in the total number of cases over the years, with recent years like 2023 and 2024 experiencing higher monthly averages compared to earlier years. <br/>
- Mortality remains extremely low, with occasional deaths reported that do not appear to correlate directly with the number of cases. <br/>
- The data for August 2024 indicates a decrease in cases compared to the previous month, suggesting a potential seasonal decline or a temporary fluctuation. <br/>

Brucellosis cases in mainland China demonstrate clear seasonal and annual fluctuations from 2014 to 2024. The number of cases tends to rise in the spring and summer months, peaking between April and July each year. For instance, 2021 saw a significant surge in cases, reaching 9,670 in June, and a similar pattern occurred in 2022 and 2023 with peak cases in the same period. This seasonal trend may be linked to agricultural activities, particularly animal husbandry, which is known to be a key factor in Brucellosis transmission. However, after each peak, cases typically decline by the end of the year, as seen across the years.

Deaths due to Brucellosis are relatively rare, with no clear annual trend. The highest monthly death count was observed in July 2018 and July 2021, both recording two fatalities. Despite the large number of cases, the overall death toll remains low, with most months reporting zero deaths. This suggests that while Brucellosis is widely spread, it is not commonly fatal in China, likely due to timely medical intervention and public health measures. However, the variation in case numbers between years implies a need for sustained monitoring and possible adjustments in prevention efforts to address the persistent seasonal spikes.
