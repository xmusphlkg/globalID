# Brucellosis

Version: April 2025

## Introduction

Brucellosis is a zoonotic infectious disease caused by bacteria of the genus *Brucella*. It primarily affects livestock such as cattle, goats, sheep, and pigs, and can be transmitted to humans through direct contact with infected animals or consumption of unpasteurized dairy products. In humans, brucellosis presents with nonspecific symptoms including fever, sweats, malaise, and muscle pain, often leading to chronic illness if untreated. The disease poses significant public health and economic burdens, especially in regions with poor veterinary controls. Diagnosis involves serological tests and cultures, while treatment typically includes prolonged antibiotic regimens.

## Highlights

- Brucellosis cases in mainland China exhibit consistent seasonal peaks, with higher incidence from April to August annually. <br/>
- Overall case counts have remained substantial, averaging over 6,000 monthly cases during peak seasons in recent years (2023â€“2025). <br/>
- Mortality remains very low despite high incidence; no deaths were reported in April 2025 and only a few per year since 2015. <br/>
- There is a slight decline in April 2025 cases (6,487) compared to April 2024 (7,141), suggesting potential early containment or reduced exposure. <br/>

The analysis of the reported brucellosis cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals a noticeable seasonal pattern, with case numbers generally increasing in the warmer months and declining towards the end of each year. Data suggests a peak in brucellosis occurrences typically during late spring to early summer, subsiding by the fall and winter months. Despite yearly fluctuations, this seasonal trend is consistently visible across the dataset. The year 2021 and 2022 notably show significant spikes, with cases reaching as high as 9,670 in June 2021 and 9,943 in June 2022, which reflects potential outbreak years. 

Mortality remains extremely low in relation to the total number of cases, indicating that while brucellosis is widespread, it is rarely fatal in China. Cases of death due to brucellosis are sporadic, with most months reporting no fatalities. However, there are occasional exceptions, indicating isolated instances where the disease could have led to more severe complications. As for trends over the examined years, while there is some fluctuation in the number of deaths, with small peaks occasionally aligned with high case months, the fatality rate remains comparatively low relative to the incidence rate, suggesting effective case management or inherent disease characteristics resulting in low mortality.
