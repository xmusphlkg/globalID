# Brucellosis

Version: September 2025

## Introduction

Brucellosis is a bacterial zoonotic infection caused primarily by species of the genus Brucella, including Brucella melitensis, Brucella abortus, Brucella suis, and Brucella canis. It primarily affects livestock such as cattle, goats, sheep, and pigs, and can be transmitted to humans through direct contact with infected animals or consumption of unpasteurized dairy products. In humans, brucellosis leads to flu-like symptoms such as fever, sweats, malaise, and joint pain, and can become chronic if untreated. Prevention involves controlling the disease in animals, vaccination, and maintaining hygiene standards in animal handling and food processing.

## Highlights

- **Increasing Cases:** After a decrease in 2020, brucellosis cases in the Chinese mainland have increased, peaking at 9943 cases in June 2022, with consistent high numbers since. <br/>
- **More Deaths:** Deaths, once rare, have become more frequent since 2021, with one to two deaths monthly, indicating increased severity or complications. <br/>
- **Seasonal Peaks:** Cases consistently peak from March to July, suggesting a seasonal pattern in disease incidence. <br/>
- **Recent Decline:** There's a slight decline from August 2025, marking a decrease from the peak of 7187 cases in June 2025, while deaths remain high in September. <br/>

Despite increases in case numbers, the mortality rate remains low, most often resulting in zero to one deaths per month, underscoring effective healthcare management.
