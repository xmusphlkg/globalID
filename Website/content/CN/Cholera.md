# Cholera

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Cholera is an acute diarrheal illness caused by infection of the intestine with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. It is typically contracted through the consumption of contaminated water or food, leading to severe dehydration and electrolyte imbalance from rapid fluid loss. Symptoms include watery diarrhea, vomiting, and leg cramps. Without prompt treatment, cholera can be fatal within hours, but it is easily treatable with rehydration solutions. The disease is most prevalent in areas with poor sanitation, crowded living conditions, and inadequate clean water supply. Outbreaks are often intensified by natural disasters and conflicts disrupting infrastructure.

## Highlights

1. **Cholera Cases Declining**: The number of cholera cases in mainland China has significantly declined in recent years, with no reported cases in several months of 2024, including November.<br/>
2. **Seasonal Variability**: Cholera cases tend to rise during the warmer months (May to September), with peaks occurring in July and August.<br/>
3. **Zero Mortality**: Despite occasional outbreaks, there have been no reported deaths due to cholera since 2014.<br/>
4. **Stable Situation in 2024**: As of November 2024, cholera remains under control with minimal sporadic cases, indicating effective public health interventions.<br/>

The cholera data for the Chinese mainland shows a generally low and fluctuating incidence of cases from 2014 to 2024, with no reported deaths. Although there are occasional spikes in cases, the majority of months report zero cases. The highest number of reported cases in a single month occurred in August 2018, with 12 cases, followed by other notable peaks in August 2019 (12 cases) and August 2022 (10 cases). These fluctuations in case numbers appear to be sporadic, with no consistent seasonal or geographic patterns emerging across the years, which could suggest localized outbreaks or imported cases rather than widespread community transmission.

Despite these sporadic outbreaks, the consistent absence of deaths associated with cholera is a notable feature of the dataset, possibly reflecting improvements in early detection, effective response measures, and access to treatment. The data also shows long periods of zero cases, particularly between 2020 and 2024, which could be indicative of effective public health interventions or a reduced risk of cholera transmission during this period. The absence of any reported deaths further supports the notion that the health system in China has been able to manage cases effectively, preventing fatalities even during occasional outbreaks.
