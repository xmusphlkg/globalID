# Dengue

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by the dengue virus, transmitted primarily by Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. It presents with flu-like symptoms, including high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, rash, and mild bleeding. Severe forms, like dengue hemorrhagic fever and dengue shock syndrome, can be life-threatening. Dengue is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide, particularly in urban and semi-urban areas. Control measures focus on mosquito prevention and vector control, as no specific antiviral treatment exists; supportive care is the mainstay of management.

## Highlights

- **Recent Increase in Cases:** Dengue cases in July 2024 show a significant rise (554 cases), continuing the upward trend from 2023, indicating potential seasonal or cyclical factors. <br/>
- **Seasonal Peaks:** The data consistently demonstrates peak cases in the latter half of each year, particularly in September and October, suggesting a seasonal pattern. <br/>
- **Low Mortality:** Despite fluctuations in case numbers, mortality remains low, with only a few deaths reported annually, indicating effective management or lower virulence of the circulating strains. <br/>
- **Comparative Decline:** The peak in cases in 2019 was the highest in recent years, but subsequent years show a decline, although 2023 saw a resurgence. <br/>

The data on dengue cases in mainland China reveals fluctuating patterns with periodic significant peaks. In 2014, cases began to surge in July, peaking at nearly 29,000 by October, followed by a sharp decline. This seasonal trend, peaking during late summer and early autumn, reflects typical patterns due to mosquito vector activity. In 2015, cases rose again in September and October, indicating dengue’s cyclical nature. Although 2016 showed a resurgence, with cases peaking at over 600 in August and September, the trend remained consistent with this seasonal pattern.

From 2017 to 2019, dengue outbreaks continued to fluctuate, with notable peaks occurring in late summer. The year 2019 marked a significant outbreak, with a peak of over 8,000 cases in September. Conversely, from 2020 onwards, the numbers drastically reduced, likely due to COVID-19 public health measures that restricted the spread of infectious diseases. However, in 2023, cases spiked again, beginning in July and peaking in September, although mortality remained low.

This epidemiological trend indicates that dengue outbreaks in China align with warmer months, consistent with Aedes mosquito life cycles. The data underscores the importance of vector control and public health strategies to mitigate outbreak impacts. Despite these fluctuations, low mortality rates suggest effective clinical management, emphasizing the need for vigilant monitoring and adaptable responses to future outbreaks.
