# Dengue

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by the dengue virus, primarily transmitted through the Aedes aegypti mosquito. It is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. Symptoms of dengue range from mild flu-like signs to severe manifestations such as dengue hemorrhagic fever or dengue shock syndrome, which can be life-threatening. Common symptoms include high fever, severe headache, joint and muscle pain, rash, and bleeding tendencies. There is no specific antiviral treatment for dengue, but supportive care can help manage symptoms. Prevention focuses on controlling mosquito populations and avoiding bites using repellents and insecticide-treated nets.

## Highlights

- Dengue cases in Chinese mainland have exhibited a cyclical pattern with significant peaks between July and October annually, indicating seasonality in transmission. <br/>

- The year 2024 recorded the highest number of cases, with October alone witnessing over 11,000 cases, highlighting a potential outbreak or increased transmission that year. <br/>

- The number of deaths associated with Dengue has remained relatively low with isolated occurrences, indicating potentially effective treatment protocols or milder disease severity. <br/>

- As of August 2025, Dengue cases have decreased compared to 2024, suggesting improved control measures or variability in external factors affecting transmission. <br/>

The data on dengue cases in mainland China reveals a distinct seasonal pattern, with cases peaking typically from July to October each year. This cyclical trend is likely driven by environmental factors, such as rainfall and temperature, which favor mosquito breeding. For example, September 2019 recorded a substantial peak of 8,036 cases, indicative of optimal conditions for vector activity during that period. The seasonal nature underlines the need for enhanced vector control measures in these peak months to manage outbreaks effectively.

Yearly data also shows a decline in dengue cases from November to May, with 2021 having particularly low numbers, possibly due to improved public health measures or shifts in transmission dynamics, possibly influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic impacts. Despite the generally low lethality, with only five deaths during the observed period, ongoing vigilance remains critical given the resurgence observed in 2024, where October recorded the highest single-month total of 11,083 cases.

Analyzing periodic fluctuations could offer insights into factors beyond climatic conditions, such as urbanization or changes in public health practices, which may influence outbreaks. Identifying such factors would support predictive modeling and intervention strategies. Continued monitoring and nuanced public health responses are necessary to anticipate and mitigate future dengue epidemics effectively, especially in peak transmission periods.
