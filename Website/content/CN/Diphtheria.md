# Diphtheria

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

Diphtheria is a highly contagious bacterial infection caused by Corynebacterium diphtheriae, affecting the respiratory tract and occasionally the skin. The bacteria produce a toxin leading to symptoms such as sore throat, fever, and swollen glands. A hallmark sign includes a thick, gray membrane covering the throat and tonsils, potentially obstructing breathing. Prevalent before widespread immunization, diphtheria primarily spreads via respiratory droplets or direct contact. Effective prevention is achieved through DPT (diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus) vaccination. Prompt administration of antitoxin and antibiotics is crucial for treatment, reducing the risk of serious complications such as myocarditis and neurological issues.

## Highlights

- Diphtheria cases in the Chinese mainland have remained sporadic and rare, with only isolated incidences reported between 2016 and 2023.  
<br/>  
- The disease has achieved near elimination status, given the consistently low case count and zero reported deaths throughout the observed period.  
<br/>  
- There were only six cases reported over 10 years, demonstrating effective public health interventions and potentially high vaccination coverage.  
<br/>  
- As of July 2025, the absence of new cases over nearly two years suggests sustained control and continued vigilance by health authorities in combating diphtheria.  
<br/>
