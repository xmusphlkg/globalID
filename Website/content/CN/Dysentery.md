# Dysentery

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Dysentery is an inflammatory disease of the intestine, particularly the colon, resulting in severe diarrhea with blood. Commonly caused by bacterial infections like Shigella or amoebic parasites like Entamoeba histolytica, dysentery is characterized by abdominal pain, fever, and a frequent urge to defecate. Transmission often occurs through contaminated food or water, making it prevalent in areas with poor sanitation. Treatment typically involves rehydration and, in bacterial cases, antibiotics. Preventive measures include improved sanitation, access to clean water, and good hygiene practices, significantly reducing the incidence of this potentially severe illness.

## Highlights

- **Steady Decline:** Dysentery cases in mainland China have displayed a steady decline from 2014 to 2024, with notable reductions in annual cases, particularly after 2018. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variation:** Higher incidence is consistently observed during summer months (June to August), correlating with warmer weather which may facilitate transmission. <br/>

- **Mortality Trends:** Deaths from dysentery have remained low, with sporadic fatalities over the years, highlighting improved clinical management and possibly better overall healthcare. <br/>

- **Current Situation:** The number of cases in September 2024 is notably lower compared to the early period of analysis, indicating effective public health interventions and possibly improved hygiene and sanitation. <br/>

The dysentery case data for mainland China from September 2014 to September 2024 shows a fluctuating pattern, with seasonal peaks occurring consistently during the summer months, especially from May to August. These peaks are likely linked to warmer weather, which can facilitate the spread of dysentery through contaminated water and food. For instance, cases often rise above 15,000 during summer months in most years, with July and August 2015 and 2016 showing especially high numbers exceeding 18,000 cases. In contrast, winter months, particularly from December to February, show significantly lower case counts, reflecting a seasonal decline in transmission.

The number of deaths remains relatively low across the entire dataset, indicating effective clinical management or less severe outbreaks. Mortality peaks are sporadic, with most months reporting zero deaths. Notable exceptions include 2020, where three deaths occurred in January, coinciding with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, which might have complicated disease surveillance and healthcare access. Overall, the data suggests that while dysentery remains a recurring public health issue in China, particularly during warmer months, mortality rates are largely controlled.

The analysis of dysentery-related deaths in mainland China from September 2014 to September 2024 shows that mortality rates remained consistently low despite fluctuations in the number of reported cases. The total cases vary seasonally, often peaking in the summer months, particularly from June to August, which is typical for gastrointestinal infections. However, deaths remained rare, with the highest single-month toll reaching three, such as in July 2015 and January 2020. Most months recorded zero deaths, and when deaths occurred, they appeared sporadically without a clear correlation to higher case numbers.

Over the 10-year period, the number of deaths due to dysentery declined, especially in the later years. For instance, from 2021 onward, the majority of months reported no deaths, even as cases ranged from 1,500 to 7,000 per month. This decline in fatalities, despite the consistent presence of dysentery cases, likely reflects improvements in healthcare, timely treatment, and better management of dysentery. While dysentery remains a public health concern, the near-zero mortality rate indicates effective control of severe cases.
