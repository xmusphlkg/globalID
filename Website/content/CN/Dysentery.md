# Dysentery

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Dysentery is an inflammatory disease of the intestines, particularly the colon, causing severe diarrhea with blood and mucus. It's primarily caused by infections, including bacteria (Shigella, Salmonella), amoebas (Entamoeba histolytica), and viruses. Common symptoms include abdominal pain, fever, and frequent bowel movements. Transmission occurs through contaminated food, water, or contact with infected feces. Treatment typically involves rehydration, antibiotics for bacterial causes, and antiparasitic medications for amoebic dysentery. Preventive measures include proper sanitation, clean drinking water, and good hygiene practices.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing trend over the years**: Dysentery cases have significantly decreased from 19,689 in June 2014 to 4,197 in June 2024. <br/>
- **Seasonal fluctuations**: Higher case numbers consistently occur in summer months (June to August), indicating a seasonal pattern. <br/>
- **Mortality rates**: Despite fluctuations in cases, the death rate has remained low, with only sporadic fatalities reported. <br/>
- **Recent stabilization**: Since 2020, monthly cases have stabilized below 5,000, reflecting effective public health interventions and improved sanitation. <br/>

The data on dysentery cases in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals notable trends and patterns in disease incidence and mortality. There is a distinct seasonal fluctuation, with cases generally peaking during the summer months (June to August) and declining in winter. This pattern likely reflects environmental factors, such as temperature and sanitation conditions, which can influence the spread of dysentery. The number of cases showed a decreasing trend over the years, particularly after 2017, suggesting improvements in public health measures or changes in reporting practices.

Mortality rates from dysentery remained low throughout the observed period, with occasional spikes in the number of deaths, but never exceeding a few cases per month. This low mortality, despite the relatively high incidence, may indicate that most cases were not severe or were effectively treated. The slight increase in deaths in specific months, such as January 2020 and May 2021, could be due to variations in the virulence of strains or other public health challenges during those periods. Overall, the data suggests that while dysentery remains a health concern, its impact in terms of mortality has been relatively contained in recent years.

Over the observed period, there are noticeable peaks in both cases and deaths during certain months, but no clear seasonal trend is evident. Moreover, the overall trend shows a reduction in cases towards the latter part of the period, especially from 2020 onwards, likely due to improved public health measures and treatments.
