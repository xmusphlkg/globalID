# Gonorrhea

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacteria Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It affects moist areas like the urethra, cervix, rectum, throat, and eyes. Transmission occurs through vaginal, anal, and oral sex. Men often experience painful urination and penile discharge, while women may have increased vaginal discharge or bleeding between periods. Many cases are asymptomatic. Without treatment, gonorrhea can lead to complications such as infertility and a heightened risk of HIV infection. Diagnosis is confirmed through lab tests, and antibiotics are effective for treatment. Prompt medical attention is crucial to prevent long-term health issues.

## Highlights

- **Rising Incidence:** Since 2015, Gonorrhea cases in the Chinese mainland have generally increased, peaking around summer months. By August 2025, the count was at 10,477, indicating a persistent high incidence. <br/>
- **Seasonal Pattern Emergence:** There is a noticeable pattern where cases tend to peak during mid-year months, particularly July and August, reflecting possible seasonal influences on transmission.<br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate:** Despite fluctuations in case numbers, mortality remains extremely low, with sporadic deaths reported, suggesting effective management and treatment.<br/>
- **Recent Increase Post-2023:** A surge in cases noted from early 2025 suggests recent factors contributing to increased transmission, warranting further investigation and preventive measures.<br/>

Over the years, a general upward trend in cases is observed, indicating increasing transmission dynamics.

Throughout these years, deaths related to Gonorrhea are minimal, with occasional isolated fatalities recorded, suggesting effective management or potential under-reporting. Zero deaths occur in most months, reflecting advances in medical treatments and healthcare, alongside possible misclassification issues. Post-pandemic data shows a trend toward stabilization, even though cases remain higher than pre-pandemic levels.

However, deaths were reported infrequently, with a total of only 15 deaths over the entire period. This translates into an extremely low case-fatality rate, highlighting that gonorrhea, although prevalent, does not typically result in high mortality in this population. In this context, the observed deaths may be attributed to complications or co-morbidities, rather than direct fatalities from the infection itself.

The recorded pattern of fatalities does not seem correlated to the fluctuations in case numbers. For example, high incidence periods such as from mid-2017 to the end of 2018 and in mid-2023 show no corresponding rise in deaths. This suggests that the healthcare system in the Chinese mainland may have effective measures and treatments in place to manage gonorrhea, preventing the progression to severe outcomes. Additionally, the clustering of deaths in certain years, notably in January 2020 and later in 2021 through 2022, indicates that other factors, possibly seasonal or environmental, could influence mortality.
