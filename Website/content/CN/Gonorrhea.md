# Gonorrhea

Version: June 2025

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It primarily affects the mucous membranes of the reproductive tract, but can also impact the mouth, throat, eyes, and rectum. Common symptoms in men include painful urination and pus-like discharge from the penis, while women may experience increased vaginal discharge, pelvic pain, or bleeding between periods. However, many individuals may remain asymptomatic. Untreated gonorrhea can lead to serious health consequences, including infertility. It can be diagnosed through laboratory tests and is usually treatable with antibiotics, although antibiotic-resistant strains are emerging.

## Highlights

- **Increasing Trend:** Gonorrhea cases in mainland China have shown a general upward trend from 2015 to 2025. This is evident from the overall increase in reported monthly cases, particularly after 2017. <br/>  
- **Recent Stability:** From 2023 to mid-2025, monthly cases appear to stabilize around 9,000 to 10,000, indicating a plateau in the epidemic trajectory. <br/>  
- **Low Mortality Rate:** Despite fluctuating case numbers, the death rate remains extremely low, with most months reporting zero deaths, suggesting effective clinical management. <br/>  
- **Potential Seasonal Patterns:** There seems to be a pattern of decreasing cases in February, likely due to underreporting or changes in healthcare-seeking behavior. <br/>

Initially, monthly cases ranged from about 8500 to 10000 in 2015, with typical seasonal peaks in the latter half of the year, possibly due to increased travel and holiday interactions. Notably, case numbers rose steadily from mid-2015 into 2017, peaking at 13803 in August 2017, suggesting a rise in prevalence or improved reporting and diagnostics.

Between 2018 and early 2020, cases slightly decreased but remained around 11000 monthly. A significant decline occurred in early 2020, particularly in February with 3524 cases, likely influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic affecting healthcare access and reporting. Cases rebounded, reaching up to 12000 towards the end of 2020, indicative of post-pandemic effects. From 2021 onward, there was a gradual decline, reducing to below 10000 by 2025. Deaths were sporadic, with very low fatality rates, indicating effective treatment protocols.

Recent trends from 2023 show a stable yet slight decline into 2025. Seasonal dips in February are common, suggesting either seasonal impacts or public health campaigns at the year's start.
