# Gonorrhea

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*. It primarily affects the mucous membranes of the urethra, cervix, rectum, and throat. Symptoms can include painful urination, abnormal discharge from the genitals, and, in women, pelvic pain. However, many infected individuals, especially women, may be asymptomatic. If left untreated, gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications, including infertility, pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) in women, and epididymitis in men. Diagnosis is typically confirmed through laboratory testing, and treatment involves antibiotics.

## Highlights

- **Increasing Trend in Cases**: From 2014 to mid-2024, there has been a general upward trend in gonorrhea cases, peaking around 2020-2021 before stabilizing at a higher baseline compared to earlier years.<br/>
- **Seasonal Variations**: Monthly case data indicate fluctuations, with noticeable drops in February, likely due to reporting or seasonal factors affecting transmission.<br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate**: Deaths associated with gonorrhea remain very low, with sporadic occurrences primarily from 2014 to 2024, suggesting effective treatment and management of complications.<br/>
- **Recent Stability**: As of June 2024, case numbers are slightly lower than in the peak years, but still significantly higher than the early 2010s, indicating persistent public health challenges.<br/>

From 2014 to 2024, the reported gonorrhea cases in mainland China exhibit significant fluctuations, with a general upward trend observed over the years. Initial data in 2014 show monthly cases ranging from approximately 7,881 to 9,101. A slight decline is observed in early 2015, notably in February with 5,912 cases, which is the lowest recorded during the analyzed period. However, subsequent months and years show a gradual increase, peaking in July and August of 2017 with cases reaching around 13,010 and 13,803 respectively. This pattern suggests a seasonal variation, with higher incidence rates during summer months. 

The trend continues with higher monthly averages in the latter years, particularly from 2017 to 2023, where cases frequently surpass 10,000 per month, peaking in August 2017. The year 2020 marks a notable exception, where the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic caused a sharp decline in cases, particularly in February (3,524 cases), the lowest since the start of the dataset. However, post-2020 data indicate a recovery and subsequent increase in case numbers, consistent with pre-pandemic levels, albeit with minor fluctuations. Deaths associated with gonorrhea remain rare, with only a few isolated incidents reported across the years, indicating a relatively low fatality rate despite the rising number of cases.

The reported data for Gonorrhea in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals a remarkably low mortality rate associated with the disease. Out of the 669,882 recorded cases, only 19 deaths were reported, indicating an overall mortality rate of approximately 0.0028%. This exceedingly low death rate suggests that Gonorrhea, while prevalent, rarely leads to fatal outcomes in this population. The deaths are sporadically distributed across the years, with a slight concentration observed in 2020, which alone accounted for six of the total deaths.

Throughout the years, monthly case numbers show significant variability, with higher peaks typically observed in the summer months. For instance, the highest monthly cases recorded were 13,803 in August 2017. Despite these fluctuations in case numbers, the deaths remain exceedingly rare and do not exhibit any clear correlation with the spikes in reported cases. This trend underscores the effectiveness of the healthcare system in managing and treating Gonorrhea, preventing it from escalating to fatal stages. The data highlights the importance of continuous surveillance and robust healthcare measures to maintain this low mortality rate amidst varying infection rates.
