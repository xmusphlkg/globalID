# Gonorrhea

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It primarily affects the mucous membranes of the urethra, cervix, throat, and rectum, but can also infect the eyes and joints. Common symptoms include painful urination, abnormal genital discharge, and itching, though many individuals, especially women, may be asymptomatic. It is transmitted through sexual contact, including vaginal, anal, and oral sex. Untreated gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications, including infertility and increased risk of HIV. Diagnosis is typically done through laboratory tests, and treatment involves antibiotics, though emerging drug resistance is a growing concern.

## Highlights

- The number of gonorrhea cases in mainland China showed a general increasing trend from 2015 to 2018, peaking in 2020 at 11691 cases in December.<br/>
- A marked fluctuation is observed in the number of cases post-2020 with a notable decline to around 6000 cases in December 2022, followed by a subsequent increase up to March 2025.<br/>
- The dataset indicates very low mortality associated with gonorrhea, with only occasional deaths scattered across the years analyzed.<br/>
- Recent data from March 2025 shows 8,367 cases, reflecting a moderate stabilization compared to previous years.<br/>

An analysis of Gonorrhea cases in the Chinese mainland from March 2015 to March 2025 reveals a significant trend in the number of reported cases over the ten-year period. Initially, in 2015, the cases ranged from 7,632 in April to 10,046 in December, with moderate fluctuations. However, the number of cases significantly increased from 2016 onwards, peaking consistently during the summer months, with July and August recording the highest numbers. For instance, cases rose sharply from 10,530 in July 2016 to 13,803 in August 2017, indicating possible seasonal impacts on transmission rates.

A notable drop in cases is observed in February each year, likely due to fewer reporting days and reduced healthcare access during holidays. In 2020, there was an unusual dip, notably in February and March, likely due to the COVID-19 pandemic and related lockdowns affecting reporting and healthcare access. Despite this, case numbers rebounded quickly post-lockdown. From 2023 onwards, the fluctuations stabilize somewhat, with seasonal peaks still present but less dramatic.

Regarding mortality, the data shows very few deaths related to Gonorrhea, indicating that while it remains a public health concern due to its high incidence, it is not typically fatal with proper treatment. Overall, the trend demonstrates a rise in cases over time with seasonal peaks and a brief impact from the COVID-19 pandemic.
