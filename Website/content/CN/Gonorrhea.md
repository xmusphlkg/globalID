# Gonorrhea

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It is transmitted through vaginal, anal, or oral sex and can infect the genitals, rectum, and throat. Common symptoms include painful urination, abnormal genital discharge, and in men, testicular pain. However, many people may be asymptomatic. If left untreated, gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications, including infertility, pelvic inflammatory disease in women, and increased risk of HIV. Diagnosis is generally made through laboratory testing of urine samples or swabs, and treatment typically involves antibiotics. Preventive measures include practicing safe sex and regular STI screenings.

## Highlights

- **Overall Increase in Cases**: Gonorrhea cases have increased steadily from 7,825 in May 2014 to 8,611 in May 2024, indicating a rising trend over the decade. <br/>
- **Monthly Variability**: The number of cases exhibits significant monthly fluctuations, experiencing notable peaks during summer months (e.g., July and August). <br/>
- **Low Mortality**: Despite the increasing number of gonorrhea cases, deaths remain exceptionally low, with sporadic occurrences across the years. <br/>
- **Impact of COVID-19**: There was a noticeable dip in case numbers during early 2020, potentially reflecting the impact of COVID-19 lockdowns and disruptions. <br/>

The reported data for gonorrhea in mainland China from mid-2014 to May 2024 depicts a clear trend of fluctuation in monthly cases, with an overall increasing tendency observed particularly pronounced from 2014 to 2018. Monthly cases rose from around 7,825 in May 2014 to a peak of 13,803 in August 2017, reflecting a surge in reported infections. This upward trajectory could be attributed to multiple factors, including increased testing, better reporting mechanisms, and possibly heightened transmission rates. Beginning in late 2017, the monthly cases displayed some stability, often staying within a range of 10,000 to 13,000 cases until the early months of 2019, before showing another rise in the latter half of 2019 and fluctuating consistently above 10,000 until about early 2021.

However, the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic had a significant impact on the number of reported gonorrhea cases, especially noticeable from early 2020. Case numbers dropped significantly in February 2020 to 3,524 cases—a likely result of reduced healthcare-seeking behavior, prioritization of COVID-19 measures, and interruptions in routine medical services. Post-2020, there was a gradual resurgence in monthly cases, with fluctuations stabilizing around 9,000 to 12,000 by late 2021 and onward. Mortality rates remained extremely low throughout the period, with occasional singular deaths reported, signifying the relatively non-lethal nature of this condition but underscoring the need for sustained public health efforts to manage its spread effectively.
