# Gonorrhea

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*. It commonly affects the genitals, rectum, and throat. Symptoms in men may include painful urination and discharge from the penis, while women may experience abnormal vaginal discharge or pelvic pain, though many individuals are asymptomatic. If left untreated, gonorrhea can lead to serious complications such as pelvic inflammatory disease, infertility, and increased susceptibility to HIV. The infection is treatable with antibiotics, but antibiotic resistance is a growing concern. Safe sexual practices can reduce the risk of transmission.

## Highlights

1. **Steady Gonorrhea Incidence**: The number of gonorrhea cases in the Chinese mainland has shown a general trend of stability with monthly case counts fluctuating between 5,000 and 13,000, with no significant increase in recent years. <br/>
2. **Minimal Mortality**: Deaths related to gonorrhea remain extremely rare, with only a handful of cases recorded (less than 10 deaths annually). <br/>
3. **Declining Cases Post-2020**: After a peak around 2017-2018, cases have seen a gradual decline, with lower case counts reported in 2022 and 2023. <br/>
4. **Current Situation (Nov 2024)**: Gonorrhea remains a prevalent but manageable public health issue, with 9,211 cases reported in November 2024 and no deaths recorded. <br/>

The reported data on Gonorrhea cases in mainland China from November 2014 to November 2024 shows a consistent and relatively stable number of cases over the years, with notable seasonal fluctuations. The cases predominantly follow an increasing trend from 2014 through 2017, peaking in mid-2017 at over 13,800 cases in August. After this peak, the numbers began to stabilize, with monthly counts fluctuating between approximately 8,000 and 12,000. The data also shows that while the total number of cases is generally high, deaths remain very low, with the majority of months recording zero deaths. This trend suggests that Gonorrhea in China is largely non-fatal, but still a persistent public health concern.

From a temporal perspective, there are some seasonal patterns, with cases typically rising in the warmer months (May to August) and slightly declining during colder months (November to February). The absence of significant mortality despite the relatively high incidence might reflect the effectiveness of treatment regimens, which are typically available and effective in managing the disease. However, the persistent rate of cases, especially in urbanized regions, points to the ongoing challenge of controlling transmission. A closer examination of these cases could provide insights into the underlying factors, such as risky sexual behaviors, inadequate condom use, or gaps in healthcare accessibility, that continue to fuel Gonorrhea transmission despite awareness and treatment options.
