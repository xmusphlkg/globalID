# Gonorrhea

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It primarily affects the mucous membranes of the genitals, rectum, and throat. The infection is transmitted through sexual contact with an infected person, including vaginal, anal, or oral sex. Symptoms can include painful urination, discharge, and, for women, pelvic pain, though many individuals may be asymptomatic. If left untreated, gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications such as pelvic inflammatory disease in women and infertility in both genders. The infection is typically treatable with antibiotics, though antibiotic resistance is becoming a growing concern.

## Highlights

- **Stable Case Numbers**: From 2023 to 2024, gonorrhea cases in China have been relatively stable, with slight fluctuations. Peaks occurred in July 2023 (10,104 cases) and August 2023 (10,924 cases). <br/>

- **Long-term Trends**: Over the past decade, data show seasonal variation, with lower cases in February and higher counts from June to August. October 2024 cases (8,998) are within typical fluctuation ranges, indicating consistent patterns. <br/>

- **Mortality Rates**: Deaths from gonorrhea remain rare, indicating effective treatment and timely medical intervention. <br/>

- **Public Health Implications**: Despite stable numbers, the ongoing presence of gonorrhea underscores the need for continued prevention, education, and regular screenings. <br/>

The data on gonorrhea cases in mainland China from October 2014 to October 2024 show a fluctuating but overall upward trend in reported cases. Initially, monthly cases ranged from 8,000 to 9,000, with notable seasonal declines in February, likely due to national holidays. A significant increase is observable starting in 2016, with many months exceeding 10,000 cases, suggesting a rise in transmission or enhanced surveillance and reporting.

A peak was noted in August 2017, reaching 13,803 cases, the highest within the dataset. These elevated numbers continued into the following years, reflecting persistent public health challenges. The COVID-19 pandemic led to a substantial dip in early 2020 as mobility restrictions and healthcare disruptions took effect, with a marked decrease in February and March. However, cases rebounded rapidly by mid-2020.

From 2021 onwards, monthly cases frequently surpassed 9,000, peaking in the mid-year months of July and August, suggesting seasonal trends in transmission. The mortality associated with gonorrhea during this decade was minimal, with few deaths reported, emphasizing public health concerns primarily over antibiotic resistance rather than mortality. This data highlights the ongoing need for effective public health strategies and robust antibiotic stewardship to manage gonorrhea's public health impact in China.
