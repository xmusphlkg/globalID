# Gonorrhea

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Gonorrhea is a common sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae. It primarily affects the mucous membranes of the urethra, cervix, rectum, throat, and eyes. Transmission occurs through sexual contact with an infected person. Symptoms may include painful urination, abnormal discharge, and, in women, pelvic pain. However, many individuals may be asymptomatic. Untreated gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications, including infertility and increased risk of HIV. Diagnosis is typically made through laboratory tests, and treatment involves antibiotics, though antibiotic resistance is an increasing concern. Regular screening and safe sex practices are crucial for prevention.

## Highlights

- **Rising Cases (2015-2019):** Gonorrhea cases in mainland China increased significantly, peaking in 2019 with monthly cases often over 10,000. <br/>

- **COVID-19 Impact (2020):** A sharp drop in cases occurred in early 2020, likely due to the pandemic's effect on healthcare access, but cases rebounded by year-end. <br/>

- **Stabilization and Decline (2021-2025):** From 2021, cases began to stabilize and decline, with 2025 showing a continued decrease. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remained very low, with only sporadic deaths, indicating effective treatment and management. <br/>

The data on gonorrhea cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, there is a noticeable increase in reported cases from 2015 through 2017, with monthly cases rising from around 5,912 in February 2015 to a peak of 13,803 in August 2017. This upward trend suggests a growing public health challenge, possibly due to increased transmission rates, improved reporting, or both. The data also shows a seasonal pattern, with lower case numbers typically reported in the early months of each year, which could be attributed to reporting delays or actual seasonal variations in transmission.

From 2018 onwards, the number of cases appears to stabilize somewhat, with fluctuations but no clear upward or downward trend. However, the data from 2020 shows a significant drop in cases in February and March, likely due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, which may have affected healthcare access and reporting. Post-2020, the case numbers gradually return to pre-pandemic levels, indicating a recovery in reporting or healthcare access. Deaths remain rare throughout the period, with only sporadic occurrences, suggesting that while gonorrhea is prevalent, it is not typically fatal when treated. The data underscores the importance of continuous monitoring and public health interventions to manage and reduce the spread of gonorrhea.

The data on gonorrhea cases and deaths in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals a consistent pattern of reported cases with very few associated deaths. Over this period, the number of cases generally increased, peaking in the summer months, which could be attributed to seasonal variations in social behavior and healthcare access. Despite the rising number of cases, the mortality rate remains extremely low, with only sporadic deaths reported. This suggests that while gonorrhea is prevalent, it is not typically life-threatening, likely due to effective treatment options available in China.

The sporadic deaths reported, such as those in December 2015, April 2016, and January 2020, may indicate isolated cases of complications or co-morbidities rather than a direct consequence of the infection itself. The data underscores the importance of continued public health efforts to manage and treat gonorrhea effectively, focusing on prevention, early detection, and treatment to maintain low mortality rates. The consistent reporting of cases also highlights the need for ongoing surveillance and public health education to prevent the spread of this sexually transmitted infection.
