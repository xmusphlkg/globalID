# Hepatitis A

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It is primarily spread through the ingestion of food or water contaminated by feces from an infected person or through close personal contact. Symptoms, which can appear 2 to 6 weeks after exposure, include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and jaundice. While the illness can be mild, it can also lead to severe liver disease in rare cases. Vaccination is a highly effective preventive measure. Good sanitation and personal hygiene practices are essential in controlling its spread.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing Cases**: Hepatitis A cases in Chinese mainland have declined significantly since 2014, from over 2,600 to about 1,000 by October 2024, reflecting effective control measures and vaccination. <br/>
- **Low Mortality**: Deaths remain low, often zero monthly, indicating effective healthcare and management. <br/>
- **Recent Increase**: Early 2024 saw a rise in cases, with March reaching 1,542. Ongoing surveillance is crucial to prevent outbreaks. <br/>
- **Stabilization**: Since mid-2024, cases stabilized around 1,100, showing consistent control efforts keeping the situation in check. <br/>
