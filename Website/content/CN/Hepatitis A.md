# Hepatitis A

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It is typically spread through ingestion of contaminated food or water or close contact with an infected person. Symptoms include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and jaundice. Unlike other types of hepatitis, Hepatitis A does not cause chronic liver disease and is usually self-limiting. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent Hepatitis A, and good hygiene practices, such as handwashing, can reduce the risk of transmission.

## Highlights

- **Overall Decline in Cases**: Hepatitis A cases have steadily decreased from around 2000-2700 cases per month in 2014 to below 1200 cases per month by June 2024. This trend suggests improved public health measures and awareness.<br/>
- **Stabilization Period**: Since mid-2020, cases have generally stabilized around 1000-1500 per month, indicating a plateau in the declining trend.<br/>
- **Low Mortality**: Throughout the period, deaths have remained minimal, with sporadic occurrences and no significant spikes, reflecting effective clinical management.<br/>
- **Recent Increase in Cases**: There was a slight uptick in early 2024, particularly in March and April, which could indicate seasonal variation or temporary public health challenges.<br/>

The reported data for Hepatitis A in mainland China from mid-2014 to mid-2024 shows significant fluctuations in the number of cases and deaths. The incidence of Hepatitis A cases peaked in September 2014 with 2,656 cases, after which there was a general decline. Despite this initial peak, another substantial rise was observed in March 2019, reaching 1,938 cases, followed by a slight drop, and then again peaking in March 2024 with 1,542 cases. The data reveals a pattern of seasonal variations, with higher numbers generally reported from late summer to early autumn, possibly indicating seasonal factors influencing the disease's transmission.

The death rates remained relatively low throughout the observed period, with sporadic spikes. The highest number of deaths in a single month was recorded in December 2015, with four deaths, while several months reported zero fatalities. Notably, despite the high number of cases, the case-fatality rate remained low, suggesting effective clinical management and possibly high levels of immunity within the population. The downward trend in both cases and deaths from 2020 onwards coincides with the global COVID-19 pandemic, which might have influenced public health behaviors and reduced the spread of other communicable diseases, including Hepatitis A. Overall, the data highlights the importance of continued surveillance and targeted public health interventions to manage and reduce Hepatitis A incidence in China.
