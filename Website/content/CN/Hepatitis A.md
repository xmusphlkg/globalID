# Hepatitis A

Version: May 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It is typically transmitted through ingestion of contaminated food or water or close contact with an infected person. Symptoms include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, jaundice, and elevated liver enzymes. Unlike other forms of viral hepatitis, hepatitis A does not cause chronic liver disease and is usually self-limiting, with full recovery in most cases. Effective prevention includes practicing good hygiene, ensuring safe food and water, and vaccination, which provides long-term protection. Outbreaks are more common in areas with poor sanitation.

## Highlights

- Hepatitis A cases in mainland China showed a long-term declining trend from 2015 to 2023, with monthly cases falling from over 1,700 to under 1,000 by late 2022 and early 2023. <br/>
- A resurgence began in early 2024, with monthly cases rising steadily and reaching a peak of 2,293 in January 2025, the highest since 2016. <br/>
- Despite rising case numbers in 2024â€“2025, deaths remain low, with only 3 total fatalities recorded since January 2024. <br/>
- The latest data from May 2025 shows 1,862 cases and no deaths, indicating elevated but stabilizing transmission with continued low mortality. <br/>
