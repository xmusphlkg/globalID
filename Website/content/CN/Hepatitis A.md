# Hepatitis A

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection caused by the hepatitis A virus. It is typically transmitted through the ingestion of contaminated food or water or through direct contact with an infectious person. Symptoms often include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, jaundice, and dark urine, though some people, especially children, may remain asymptomatic. The disease is usually self-limiting, with most individuals recovering completely without lasting liver damage. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent hepatitis A, and maintaining good hygiene and sanitation can reduce transmission risks. Outbreaks are more common in regions with poor sanitation.

## Highlights

1. **Declining Case Numbers**: Hepatitis A cases in China have decreased from over 2,600 monthly in 2014 to below 1,100 in 2024, showing a steady decline. <br/>

2. **Low Mortality**: Deaths from Hepatitis A have remained minimal, with only a few fatalities each year, including 2 in 2024. <br/>

3. **Seasonal Peaks**: Cases typically increase in spring and summer, especially in March and June, with slight fluctuations across months. <br/>

4. **Effective Surveillance**: Disease monitoring and prevention efforts continue, with manageable case numbers and no significant outbreaks in recent years. <br/>

Hepatitis A cases in mainland China have shown fluctuating trends from 2014 to 2024, with a noticeable decline after 2018. The number of reported cases peaked in late 2014, reaching 2,733 in December, and remained high through much of 2015 and 2016. However, from 2019 onwards, the incidence gradually decreased, with monthly cases often falling below 1,500 after 2021. This decline suggests improvements in control measures, such as vaccination and hygiene practices. A slight uptick in cases occurred in March 2024, with 1,542 cases, but overall numbers have remained low.

Despite the fluctuations in case numbers, the mortality rate has been consistently low, with only sporadic deaths recorded throughout the years. Most months, especially from 2020 onward, report no fatalities, reflecting the relatively mild course of Hepatitis A in China. For instance, there were no deaths in 2020, 2021, or 2022, except for a few isolated incidents. In 2024, while the number of cases showed some variability, fatalities remained minimal. This pattern suggests the effectiveness of public health interventions, such as vaccination campaigns and public health awareness, in reducing both the burden of disease and associated mortality.
