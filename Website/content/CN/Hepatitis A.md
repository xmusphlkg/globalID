# Hepatitis A

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious liver infection caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It is typically transmitted through ingestion of contaminated food or water or direct contact with an infected person. The disease can cause symptoms such as fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and jaundice. While it is generally not as severe as other types of viral hepatitis, it can lead to significant illness and, in rare cases, liver failure. Vaccination and good hygiene practices are effective measures to prevent Hepatitis A. Recovery usually confers lifelong immunity.

## Highlights

- **Reduction in Cases Over Decade:** Hepatitis A cases in Chinese mainland decreased from 1970 in May 2014 to 1419 in May 2024, indicating effective control measures. <br/>
- **Fluctuations in Monthly Counts:** Monthly cases show periodic fluctuations, with notable peaks like 2198 in March 2016 and 1542 in March 2024, indicating variable transmission rates. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remains low, rarely exceeding 1-2 deaths per month, suggesting better treatment and prevention. <br/>
- **Recent Trends and Stability:** Cases have stabilized around 1000-1500 per month in recent years, reflecting sustained control but underscoring ongoing public health efforts. <br/>

Analyzing the Hepatitis A data from mainland China from May 2014 to May 2024 reveals a fluctuating trend in case numbers with sporadic deaths throughout the decade. Initially, cases rose, peaking at 2733 in December 2014, with deaths remaining relatively low (0-2 per month). A gradual decline in cases commences in 2015, indicating an early response to manage the outbreak. By 2017, a notable stabilization in case numbers is observed, with monthly counts adjusting to lower levels between 1300-1700, and deaths remaining minimal.

From 2018 onwards, a consistent decrease in case numbers is evident, with cases frequently falling below 1000 from 2021, reaching as low as 532 in late 2022. This period marks a significant downturn in the spread of Hepatitis A, likely reflecting enhanced public health interventions and vaccination efforts. The later years, specifically from 2023 to early 2024, see a stabilization in case numbers around the 1000 mark, with a brief spike to 1542 cases in March 2024. Death occurrences are infrequent and isolated, suggesting that while Hepatitis A case numbers have fluctuated, their impact on mortality has been relatively contained, highlighting the efficiency of preventive measures and healthcare responses in minimizing fatalities associated with the disease.

Analyzing the Hepatitis A data from the Chinese mainland (May 2014 to May 2024) reveals low mortality relative to case counts. The number of deaths remained sporadic, with the highest monthly death count being four in December 2015. Most months recorded zero to one death, indicating either a less severe impact of the disease or effective healthcare management in treating infections.

The data show a seasonal pattern in Hepatitis A cases, with higher numbers typically reported during warmer months (May to September) and fewer cases in cooler months (October to April). This pattern aligns with the increased spread of foodborne illnesses in warmer climates. Despite seasonal changes in case numbers, death rates did not show a corresponding seasonal pattern.

Over the decade, the overall number of reported cases declined, notably dropping after 2022. This reduction may reflect successful public health measures such as improved sanitation, vaccination efforts, or changes in reporting practices. Regardless, these findings emphasize the importance of maintaining public health interventions to continue reducing the incidence and mortality of Hepatitis A.
