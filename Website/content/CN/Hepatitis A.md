# Hepatitis A

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious viral infection that affects the liver, caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It is primarily transmitted through the consumption of contaminated food or water or through direct contact with an infected individual. Symptoms typically appear within 2 to 7 weeks after exposure and may include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, jaundice, and fever. While most cases resolve spontaneously without complications, preventive measures such as improved sanitation, hygiene practices, and vaccination are crucial. Vaccination is highly effective and is recommended for high-risk populations and individuals traveling to areas with high HAV prevalence.

## Highlights

- **Recent Surge**: Hepatitis A cases in 2025 peaked in January with 2,293 cases, showing a dramatic increase compared to previous years, indicating potential outbreak or heightened transmission. <br/>
- **Decline Since Peak**: Following the January surge, cases steadily declined to 1,311 by August 2025, demonstrating effective response and control measures. <br/>
- **Stable Mortality**: Despite fluctuations in case numbers, deaths have remained low, highlighting advances in clinical management and preventive strategies over the years. <br/>
- **Comparison with Prior Years**: The current situation reflects a non-seasonal pattern, unlike in prior years where case numbers had stabilized from 2021â€“2024. <br/>

The temporal trend of Hepatitis A cases in mainland China shows discernible fluctuations over the years, with notable variances in monthly case counts. Initially, from 2015 to 2017, case numbers exhibit a relative stability, ranging between approximately 1400 to 2200 monthly cases. However, this pattern shifts starting from 2018, marking a noticeable decline in case numbers, particularly from late 2019 into 2020, with counts descending to as low as 1100 monthly. This notable reduction could be indicative of improved public health interventions, perhaps attributed to increased vaccine coverage or enhanced sanitation and public education campaigns.

Analyzing mortality related to Hepatitis A, although the total number of deaths remains low, usually between zero to two deaths per month, slight changes in fatality trends do appear. Significantly, a comprehensive drop in fatalities is observed starting from mid-2020 onwards, and the month's data from 2022 onwards consistently report zero deaths across numerous months. The numbing of fatalities corresponds with the overall decrease in disease prevalence, underscoring effective clinical management and public health policies reducing severe outcomes from the infection. Collectively, the data suggests a positive trajectory concerning the control of Hepatitis A, although vigilance in maintaining these gains and constantly monitoring emerging patterns remains imperative to avoid potential resurgence.
