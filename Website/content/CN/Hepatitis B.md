# Hepatitis B

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis B is a viral infection caused by the Hepatitis B virus (HBV), primarily affecting the liver. It is transmitted through contact with infectious body fluids, such as blood, semen, and vaginal secretions. Acute infection can range from asymptomatic to severe illness, while chronic infection can lead to serious health issues like cirrhosis and liver cancer. Prevention is highly effective through vaccination, and treatment options include antiviral medications. Globally, Hepatitis B remains a major public health concern, with significant efforts directed towards immunization and safe healthcare practices to reduce transmission.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers**: From 2014 to 2024, Hepatitis B cases in mainland China have seen an overall increasing trend, peaking at 152,967 in March 2024. <br/>
- **Fluctuations in Mortality**: Deaths remained relatively stable, ranging between 17 and 80 per month, with an increase noted in 2024, especially in August with 42 deaths. <br/>
- **Seasonal Patterns**: Case numbers show seasonal peaks, with higher cases typically occurring from March to August each year. <br/>
- **Recent Surge**: Significant increases in reported cases in 2024, with August reaching 140,045 cases, indicating an ongoing burden of the disease. <br/>

The data on Hepatitis B cases in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals significant fluctuations in both the number of cases and associated deaths. Between 2014 and 2019, there was a steady rise in reported cases, peaking at over 113,000 cases in some months. Notably, the number of deaths remained relatively low compared to the cases, with the highest death toll in January 2020 at 80 deaths, possibly influenced by external health factors or concurrent health crises. 

After 2020, there is a noticeable dip in case numbers, particularly in February 2020, likely reflecting disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. However, as conditions normalized, case numbers gradually climbed, reaching their highest point in 2024, with March recording over 152,000 cases. Deaths, though fluctuating, generally remained below 50 per month. The rise in case numbers in recent years could reflect improved surveillance and reporting or a genuine increase in Hepatitis B infections. These trends highlight the ongoing public health challenge posed by Hepatitis B in mainland China.
