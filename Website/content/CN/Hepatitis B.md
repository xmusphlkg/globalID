# Hepatitis B

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis B is a potentially life-threatening liver infection caused by the Hepatitis B virus (HBV). It is a major global health problem and the most serious type of viral hepatitis. The virus is transmitted through exposure to infectious blood, semen, and other body fluids. It can lead to chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, and liver cancer. While a vaccine is available and effective for prevention, treatment options for chronic cases aim to manage and control the infection rather than cure it. Early diagnosis through blood tests is crucial for effective management and reducing the risk of serious liver complications.

## Highlights

- **Upward Trend in Cases:** Hepatitis B cases in Chinese mainland increased significantly, from 91,936 in May 2014 to 139,523 in May 2024. <br/>
- **Deaths Relatively Stable:** Deaths remained stable with minor reduction over time, despite the rise in cases. <br/>
- **Peaks in Infection:** Marked monthly spikes are evident, such as March 2024 with 152,967 cases, suggesting seasonal or periodic factors. <br/>
- **Continued Public Health Concern:** Persistent high case numbers in 2024 highlight Hepatitis B as an ongoing epidemiological concern in China. <br/>

The reported cases of Hepatitis B in mainland China from May 2014 to May 2024 show significant variability and an overall upward trend in cases over the years. Monthly case counts often fluctuated, with noticeable peaks and troughs. For instance, May 2014 reported 91,936 cases, which exhibited an increase reaching distinct new high points such as 110,717 cases in March 2017, 120,659 in March 2018, and attaining a peak of 152,967 cases in March 2024. Notably, the months following each year's lunar new year (usually February or March) tend to show higher cases, possibly hinting at a seasonal or post-holiday uptick in diagnosis or reporting. 

The death counts remained relatively stable compared to the fluctuations in cases, although occasional spikes are present. Deaths ranged from a low of 17 deaths in February 2015 to a high of 80 deaths in January 2020, potentially reflecting case severity or health infrastructure issues at various times. The stability in death counts, despite rising case counts, could suggest improvements in medical interventions and management of the disease over the years. The consistency of death data and the gradual increase in cases emphasize the necessity for ongoing public health monitoring and intervention strategies to manage Hepatitis B effectively.

The dataset for Hepatitis B in mainland China from May 2014 to May 2024 shows variability in both cases and deaths. Analyzing the data reveals that while the number of cases generally trends upward, deaths do not always increase proportionally. For instance, December 2018 recorded 96,336 cases with 40 deaths, while February 2019 had 90,985 cases but only 31 deaths, suggesting other influencing factors like medical interventions or public health policies.

A significant spike in fatalities is seen in January 2020 with 80 deaths, likely impacted by the concurrent outbreak of COVID-19, which may have strained healthcare resources. Despite the rising annual case numbers, certain months consistently report lower deaths, possibly indicating seasonal effects. For example, in 2023, May recorded 17 deaths despite high case numbers, while December saw 32 deaths, showing significant variability.

This data suggests that factors such as healthcare accessibility, public health interventions, vaccination efforts, and socio-economic conditions play critical roles in influencing the death rate independently of the number of cases. Overall, while the rising number of cases remains concerning, varying death rates highlight the importance of continual improvements in healthcare and public health strategies to manage Hepatitis B effectively.
