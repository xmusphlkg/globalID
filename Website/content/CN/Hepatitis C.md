# Hepatitis C

Version: October 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis C is a viral infection caused by the hepatitis C virus (HCV) that primarily affects the liver. It is transmitted through blood-to-blood contact, most commonly through the sharing of needles, unscreened blood transfusions, and, less frequently, through sexual contact or from mother to child during childbirth. Hepatitis C can lead to both acute and chronic liver disease, with the latter potentially causing severe conditions such as cirrhosis and liver cancer over time. Many infected individuals are asymptomatic initially, making early detection challenging. Treatments include antiviral medications, which can often eliminate the virus from the body.

## Highlights

- **Case Trend:** Hepatitis C cases in the Chinese mainland showed fluctuations from 2015 to 2025, peaking around March annually, with a recent declining trend since late 2023. <br/>
- **Mortality Increase:** There has been a marked increase in deaths, particularly from 2023 onward, reaching significant peaks in late 2023 and fluctuating in 2024 and 2025. <br/>
- **Notable Peaks:** High case numbers in the years 2022 and 2023 were associated with dramatic increases in the number of deaths, signaling a potential worsening of disease management or new variant emergence. <br/>
- **Current Situation (2025):** While cases are decreasing steadily, mortality remains high and unpredictable, suggesting a need for enhanced health interventions. <br/>

From 2015 to 2019, the number of cases remained relatively stable with occasional spikes, indicating potential outbreaks or increased diagnostics during these periods. Annual data suggest an increase until 2019, after which monthly cases became more variable.

Concerning mortality, a troubling trend emerged starting in 2023, where deaths began to rise sharply. Previously, death rates were low and stable, often in single-digit figures per month. From mid-2023, however, monthly deaths escalated dramatically, peaking in 2024, where December saw 589 deathsâ€”far exceeding earlier years. By 2025, deaths remained high but showed some stabilization compared to the 2024 peak, indicating ongoing issues in managing the disease's impact.

The sharp increase in mortality rates calls for further investigation into healthcare infrastructure, treatment outcomes, and public health strategies. This analysis underscores the need for intensified surveillance and intervention efforts to address the rising mortality associated with Hepatitis C in this region.
