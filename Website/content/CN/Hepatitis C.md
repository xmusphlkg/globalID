## Introduction

Hepatitis C is a liver infection caused by the Hepatitis C virus (HCV). It is primarily spread through blood-to-blood contact, often via shared needles or unscreened blood transfusions. Many individuals with Hepatitis C are asymptomatic, but chronic infection can lead to serious liver damage, including cirrhosis and liver cancer. Diagnosis is typically through blood tests, and treatment involves antiviral medications, which can cure most cases. Preventive measures include avoiding needle sharing and ensuring blood products are screened. Vaccines are not available for Hepatitis C, making prevention and early detection crucial.

## Highlights



- **Rising Mortality**: From 2015 to 2025, deaths from Hepatitis C have increased significantly, especially from 2022, peaking in late 2024, indicating worsening disease severity. <br/>

- **Variable Case Numbers**: Case numbers have fluctuated, with peaks in March 2018, March 2019, and March 2023, suggesting seasonal or reporting influences. <br/>

- **Recent Case Decline**: In 2025, there is a decline in cases compared to previous years, possibly due to improved public health measures or reporting changes. <br/>

- **Public Health Concern**: The sharp rise in deaths in 2023 and 2024 underscores the need for enhanced healthcare strategies to manage Hepatitis C effectively. <br/>

The data on Hepatitis C cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, the number of reported cases fluctuated moderately, with monthly cases generally ranging between 15,000 and 25,000. However, a notable increase in the number of deaths is observed starting in 2022, with a significant spike in mortality rates from 2023 onwards. This increase in deaths, despite relatively stable case numbers, suggests a potential shift in the severity of the disease, changes in reporting practices, or possibly the emergence of more virulent strains or complications in treatment protocols.

From 2023, the data shows a dramatic rise in deaths, peaking in December 2024 with 589 deaths, despite a decrease in the number of cases compared to previous years. This discrepancy between case numbers and mortality rates could indicate several underlying issues, such as healthcare system strain, late-stage diagnosis, or inadequate access to effective treatments. The data suggests an urgent need for public health interventions focusing on early detection, improved treatment access, and possibly addressing any systemic healthcare challenges that may have arisen during this period.

The data on Hepatitis C in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 reveals a concerning trend in the number of deaths associated with the disease. Initially, from 2015 to 2019, the number of deaths per month remained relatively low, typically in the single or low double digits, despite fluctuations in the number of reported cases. This period suggests a relatively stable situation where the healthcare system might have been effectively managing the disease, keeping mortality rates low even as the number of cases varied.

However, starting in 2022, there is a noticeable and alarming increase in the number of deaths, with a significant spike observed in 2023 and 2024. For instance, deaths rose dramatically from 45 in August 2022 to 393 by December 2023, and further to 589 by December 2024. This sharp increase in mortality, despite the number of cases not showing a proportionate rise, could indicate several potential issues: a possible emergence of more virulent strains, reduced effectiveness of treatment protocols, or systemic healthcare challenges such as resource constraints or delayed diagnosis and treatment. The data suggests an urgent need for public health interventions to address these rising mortality rates, including enhanced surveillance, improved access to treatment, and possibly the development of new therapeutic strategies to combat the evolving nature of the disease.
