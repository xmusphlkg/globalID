# Hepatitis C

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis C is a liver disease caused by the Hepatitis C virus (HCV), primarily transmitted through contact with the blood of an infected person. It can lead to both acute and chronic infections, with many people experiencing chronic illness, which can progress to cirrhosis or liver cancer over several decades. Often asymptomatic in the early stages, symptoms can include fatigue, jaundice, and abdominal pain. Diagnosis is typically made through blood tests, and treatment involves antiviral medications which can cure the infection. Prevention strategies focus on safe blood handling and reducing needle-sharing practices among higher-risk groups.

## Highlights

### Key Epidemiological Trends of Hepatitis C in China (2024)

- **Rising Cases and Deaths**: Hepatitis C cases and deaths have steadily increased, with November 2024 reporting 17,027 cases and 454 deaths, reflecting a concerning trend.
  
<br/>

- **Monthly Fluctuations**: Case numbers vary significantly, with peaks such as May 2023 (20,963) and November 2024 (17,027), suggesting potential outbreaks or seasonal factors.

<br/>

- **Increased Mortality**: Mortality has sharply risen, with 454 deaths recorded in November 2024, highlighting growing severity of the disease.

<br/>

- **Public Health Concern**: Hepatitis C continues to be a significant public health issue, demanding enhanced surveillance and intervention efforts across the country.

From 2014 to 2024, hepatitis C cases in mainland China showed fluctuating trends, with notable peaks and troughs in reported cases and deaths. While case numbers consistently ranged from 10,000 to 25,000 per month, there were sharp increases in specific months, such as May and June 2023, when deaths surged dramatically to 151, 186, and 114, respectively. These spikes likely reflect either larger outbreaks or improved detection efforts. 

Despite fluctuations in case numbers, the death toll saw a concerning rise, especially from 2022 onward. Deaths in late 2023 and throughout 2024 reached unprecedented levels, with November 2024 reporting 454 deaths. This sharp increase could indicate a worsening disease burden, potentially due to delayed diagnoses, limited access to antiviral treatments, or more virulent strains. The strain on the healthcare system may have contributed to the rising mortality. 

Overall, the data highlights an urgent need for enhanced public health responses, including broader screening, increased access to treatment, and better disease management to curb the rising cases and fatalities associated with hepatitis C in mainland China.

While the monthly case numbers fluctuate, a notable increase in deaths has been observed since 2022. Starting from around mid-2022, the death toll rose sharply, culminating in an alarming peak by the end of 2023, with monthly fatalities reaching over 200 in several months. This surge in deaths, especially notable in the months of May to December 2023, reflects a significant escalation compared to previous years, where monthly deaths typically ranged between 5 and 20 per month. The highest reported number was 454 deaths in November 2024, underscoring an ongoing crisis in the epidemic.

Several potential factors may contribute to this rise in mortality. It's plausible that while the number of cases remains high, a lack of effective treatment access, increased comorbidities, or delayed diagnoses may have worsened health outcomes for many individuals with Hepatitis C. Additionally, healthcare system challenges or delayed interventions in response to the rising cases may have exacerbated the situation, leading to more severe outcomes. The continuing rise in mortality in 2024 suggests that this trend is not just a short-term anomaly but may indicate deeper, structural issues in both healthcare and public health management of Hepatitis C in China.
