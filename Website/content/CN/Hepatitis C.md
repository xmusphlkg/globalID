# Hepatitis C

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis C is a liver infection caused by the Hepatitis C virus (HCV), a blood-borne pathogen primarily transmitted through unprotected blood contact, such as sharing needles or syringes. Often asymptomatic in its early stages, Hepatitis C can develop into chronic liver disease, with potential complications including cirrhosis and liver cancer. Globally prevalent, it poses significant public health challenges due to its capacity for silent progression. Diagnosis typically requires blood tests to detect HCV antibodies and virus RNA. Treatment has advanced with successful antiviral therapies, leading to curative outcomes in many cases and emphasizing the importance of early detection.

## Highlights

- **Fluctuating Case Numbers:** Hepatitis C case numbers in Mainland China have shown annual variation, maintaining a moderate trend without dramatic changes. <br/>
- **Rising Mortality:** There was a marked increase in deaths from late 2023, peaking in late 2024, highlighting increased severity or new complications. <br/>
- **Recent Stability:** By mid-2025, diseases incidences are stable, but mortality remains higher compared to early 2023, indicating persistent challenges. <br/>
- **Need for Intervention:** Data suggests a need for focused interventions to manage rising mortality rates, potentially through enhanced healthcare access or new treatment approaches.<br/>

The data from 2015 to 2025 for Hepatitis C in the Chinese mainland shows initial stability in case numbers, generally ranging from 17,000 to 24,000 monthly, with occasional peaks in months like March 2017 and 2018. However, the death count reveals a significant trend change, initially recording low numbers mostly below 20 per month, but beginning a troubling surge mid-2022. This increase is dramatic, moving from 45 deaths in August 2022 to 589 in December 2024, indicating a critical public health challenge.

Several factors might explain this trend: augmented viral strain virulence, delays in diagnosis and treatment, or worsening outcomes due to population demographics, such as aging or comorbidities. The data suggests inadequacies in healthcare sectors, in terms of access to antiviral therapies, or broader public health strategies that may not adequately target vulnerable groups. Despite consistently high mortality rates, the case numbers saw a slight downturn by 2025, hinting at possible improvements in treatments or interventions, although this might be affected by disease dynamics or reporting changes.

Overall, while case numbers remained relatively controlled over the years, the rising mortality rate calls for urgent interventions. Enhancements in treatment protocols, better preventive measures, and robust management strategies are essential to mitigate this mortality trend and improve health outcomes related to Hepatitis C.

The data on Hepatitis C deaths in mainland China from July 2015 to July 2025 indicates significant trends over time. Initially, deaths were consistently low, generally remaining in the single digits each month from 2015 through 2020, even with reported cases fluctuating between 15,000 to over 24,000. This pattern suggests that during this period, treatment and management of Hepatitis C were likely effective due to available antiviral therapies and healthcare measures, maintaining mortality at a relatively low level.

A marked change is observed beginning in August 2022, with deaths increasing dramatically. By December 2024, reported monthly deaths rose sharply to 589. This persistent upward trend in mortality rates in 2023 and 2024, with several months exceeding 300 deaths, signals a significant decline in health outcomes for those infected. Potential factors contributing to this rise may include complications like advanced liver disease, which indicates potential difficulties within the healthcare system or possible challenges in treatment efficacy. Despite the reported number of cases remaining somewhat stable, the severe increase in deaths highlights a pressing health crisis.

Overall, these changes underscore the need for urgent investigation into healthcare capacities, treatment protocols, and potential viral resistance. Addressing these aspects is crucial for epidemiologists and policymakers to develop effective responses and improve patient outcomes in managing Hepatitis C in mainland China.
