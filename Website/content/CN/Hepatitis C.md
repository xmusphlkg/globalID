# Hepatitis C

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis C is a viral infection caused by the Hepatitis C virus (HCV), primarily affecting the liver. It is transmitted through blood-to-blood contact, often via sharing needles or other equipment used to inject drugs. Many individuals with Hepatitis C may remain asymptomatic for years, leading to chronic infection, which can result in severe liver damage, cirrhosis, or liver cancer. Diagnosis is typically made through blood tests, and treatment options have advanced significantly, with direct-acting antiviral medications achieving high cure rates. Preventive measures include safe injection practices and screening blood donations.

## Highlights

- **Rising Mortality Rates**: Deaths due to Hepatitis C have surged since 2022, peaking at 589 in December 2024, indicating a severe public health challenge. <br/>

- **Declining Case Numbers**: There is a downward trend in reported cases from 2023 to 2025, with January 2025 recording 14,831 cases, the lowest in recent years. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations**: Case numbers show seasonal peaks, often in March and July, suggesting seasonal factors in transmission. <br/>

- **Recent Decline in Deaths**: Despite high mortality in late 2024, January 2025 shows a drop to 25 deaths, hinting at possible improvements in treatment or reporting. <br/>

The data on Hepatitis C cases in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, the number of reported cases fluctuated moderately, with monthly cases generally ranging between 15,000 and 25,000. However, a notable increase in the number of deaths began around 2022, with a sharp rise in mortality rates observed from 2023 onwards. This increase in deaths, despite relatively stable case numbers, suggests a potential shift in the severity or management of the disease, possibly due to changes in the virus's virulence, healthcare access, or treatment efficacy.

From 2023, the data shows a dramatic escalation in deaths, peaking in December 2024 with 589 deaths, despite a decrease in reported cases. This discrepancy between case numbers and mortality could indicate an emerging public health crisis, possibly exacerbated by factors such as healthcare system strain, late-stage diagnosis, or the emergence of more lethal strains. The consistent rise in deaths, even as case numbers decline, underscores the need for urgent public health interventions, including enhanced surveillance, improved treatment protocols, and increased public awareness to mitigate the impact of Hepatitis C in the region.
