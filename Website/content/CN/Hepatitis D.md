# Hepatitis D

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis D, also known as Hepatitis Delta Virus (HDV) infection, is a severe form of viral hepatitis that occurs in conjunction with Hepatitis B. It is caused by the Hepatitis D virus, a small RNA virus that requires the presence of the Hepatitis B virus to replicate. Transmission routes include percutaneous or mucosal contact with infected blood, similar to Hepatitis B, such as through shared needles, unscreened blood transfusions, or from mother to child at birth. HDV infection can cause more severe liver disease than Hepatitis B alone, increasing the risk of cirrhosis, liver failure, and hepatocellular carcinoma.

## Highlights

- **Declining Trends:** Hepatitis D cases in mainland China have dropped significantly from an average of 42 cases per month in 2016 to fewer than 20 cases in 2024. <br/>

- **Zero Mortality Rates:** There have been zero reported deaths from Hepatitis D from 2016 to October 2024, indicating effective disease management. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations:** While overall cases have decreased, seasonal fluctuations persist, with occasional increases in certain months due to varying factors. <br/>

- **Current Situation (2024):** By October 2024, cases have decreased further, reaching only 7 to 9 cases per month in September and October, showing substantial disease control progress. <br/>

The analysis of the reported data for Hepatitis D cases in mainland China from January 2016 to October 2024 reveals some significant trends and fluctuations. Initially, there was a relatively higher incidence of reported cases averaging around 40-50 per month in 2016, stabilizing to a similar range until early 2019. This period was marked by varying monthly case numbers, with some months experiencing noticeable peaks, particularly in February 2017 and September 2017, where cases spiked to 52 and 59, respectively. These fluctuations might suggest seasonal trends or reporting anomalies.

From 2020 onwards, there was a marked decline in reported cases of Hepatitis D. The reduction became even more pronounced in the years 2022 and 2023, with monthly cases frequently falling below 25. The downward trend continued into 2024, with some months recording as few as 7 cases by September and October of that year. The consistent decline in cases in recent years could indicate improvements in public health interventions, better management, or changes in surveillance and reporting systems. Throughout the entire period, it is noteworthy that there were no reported deaths, suggesting effective clinical management of the infection when cases were identified.

The data on Hepatitis D cases in mainland China spanning from January 2016 to October 2024 reveals a consistent absence of reported deaths due to the virus, despite fluctuations in the number of cases. Over the observed period, monthly case numbers varied, initially showing higher variability and annual cycles, with a general downward trend during the years. For instance, peak monthly cases in the earlier years such as January 2016 report 60 cases, gradually declining in comparison to the later years such as 2024, which saw significantly reduced numbers with some months reporting as few as seven cases.

The persistent zero-death count across all these months suggests an effective management and medical response to hepatitis D in this region. The advent of antiviral therapies and effective hepatitis B interventions might have played substantial roles, since hepatitis D virus (HDV) infection is highly dependent on hepatitis B virus (HBV) presence. The stable and relatively low case counts coupled with an absence of mortality may highlight the success of public health measures, ongoing disease surveillance, and possible improvements in patient care for those co-infected with hepatitis B and D. This data underscores the importance of ongoing vigilance and intervention to maintain the non-lethal impact of hepatitis D infection in mainland China.
