# Hepatitis D

Version: October 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis D is a liver infection caused by the hepatitis D virus (HDV), a unique satellite virus that requires the presence of hepatitis B virus (HBV) to replicate. It is transmitted through contact with infected blood and bodily fluids, sharing similarities with HBV in transmission routes including unsafe injections, transfusions, and from mother to child at birth. Co-infection with HDV can lead to more severe liver disease, including cirrhosis and liver cancer, than HBV alone. Vaccination against HBV can prevent HDV infection, highlighting the importance of HBV immunization in controlling hepatitis D prevalence.

## Highlights

- **Trend of Decreased Cases**: Hepatitis D cases in the Chinese mainland have shown a decreasing trend from 2016 to 2025, declining from higher monthly averages around 40-50 cases in 2016-2017 to lower averages below 25 cases by 2025.<br/>
- **Significant Decrease Since 2019**: Starting in 2019, there was a noticeable drop in monthly cases, with numbers often staying below 30, indicating ongoing control measures or increased public health interventions.<br/>
- **Zero Mortality Rate**: Throughout 2016-2025, no deaths were reported, highlighting effective clinical management and low disease severity.<br/>
- **Recent Fluctuations**: In 2025, cases exhibit fluctuations, with a notable increase in September (27 cases), suggesting potential monitoring and prevention efforts.<br/>

In 2016 and 2017, the monthly case numbers were relatively high, averaging around 40 to 50, signifying notable Hepatitis D activity. From 2018, there is a gentle decline with occasional peaks, evident in March and September of 2019 and December 2021. Despite these peaks, the overall trend hints at reductions possibly due to public health efforts or improved diagnostic methods.

Notably, starting in 2020, there is a significant drop in cases, consistently falling below 30 per month through 2023 and into 2024. This decline could be the result of enhanced medical interventions, increasing awareness, or effective public health policies in combating the disease. However, late 2024 records a slight increase towards the yearâ€™s end, suggesting potential reporting variations or seasonal influences. Despite fluctuating case counts, it is remarkable that no deaths have been recorded throughout this period. This zero fatality rate might reflect effective management of the disease and improvements in healthcare infrastructure.

The data indicates a shift towards better containment and management of Hepatitis D in China, highlighting successful health strategies that have maintained low mortality despite variable infection rates.
