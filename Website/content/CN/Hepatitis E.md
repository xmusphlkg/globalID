# Hepatitis E

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis E is a viral liver infection caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV). It primarily spreads through fecal-oral transmission, often via contaminated drinking water. The disease is most common in developing countries with poor sanitation infrastructure. Symptoms include jaundice, fatigue, nausea, and liver enlargement, generally appearing 2-9 weeks post-exposure. While most cases resolve without intervention, the infection can be severe in pregnant women, causing fulminant hepatitis and liver failure. Preventive measures include improved sanitation, clean drinking water, and vaccination, which is currently available only in certain countries.

## Highlights

- Hepatitis E case counts have shown a general increasing trend, particularly from 2023 onwards, peaking at 3676 cases in March 2024. <br/>
- The year 2024 reflects a relatively high number of cases, with sustained figures exceeding 2800 cases monthly since January. <br/>
- Death rates have varied, generally fluctuating between 0 and 3 deaths monthly, with notable spikes in December 2017 and January 2024, recording 6 and 4 deaths respectively. <br/>
- Seasonal trends indicate higher case numbers in March and April across multiple years, suggesting potential seasonal influence. <br/>

An analysis of Hepatitis E cases and deaths reported in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals several trends and fluctuations. The data show periodic increases in cases, particularly in the spring, with peaks in March each year. For instance, March 2016 reported 3401 cases, March 2017 had 3352 cases, and March 2022 had 3131 cases. These peaks are likely due to seasonal factors or heightened diagnostic activities during these months, with March 2024 reaching a significant high of 3676 cases.

Mortality data indicate that deaths from Hepatitis E remain relatively low compared to the number of cases, but there are sporadic increases. Notable spikes include March 2016 with five deaths and December 2017 with six deaths. The trend of deaths does not follow the same distinct seasonal pattern as cases. A gradual reduction in both cases and deaths from early 2020 might be linked to the COVID-19 lockdowns and public health measures, which could have impacted the transmission or healthcare focus. Interestingly, March 2024 saw a significant rise in cases without any reported deaths, suggesting potential improvements in clinical management and care for Hepatitis E patients.
