## Introduction

Hepatitis E is a viral liver disease caused by the Hepatitis E virus (HEV). It primarily spreads through the fecal-oral route, often via contaminated water or food. HEV infection is prevalent in regions with poor sanitation and is most common in Asia, Africa, and parts of Central America. Symptoms include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and nausea. While the disease is usually self-limiting, it can be severe in pregnant women and individuals with pre-existing liver conditions. Prevention includes proper sanitation, clean drinking water, and good hygiene practices.

## Highlights

- **Rising Trend in Cases**: There has been a consistent increase in reported Hepatitis E cases since early 2024, with March recording the highest number of cases at 3676, indicating a worrying upward trend. <br/>
- **Moderate Mortality Rates**: Despite the rise in cases, death rates have remained relatively stable with minor fluctuations, suggesting effective management and treatment strategies. <br/>

- **Consistent Reporting**: Case reporting has shown a marked increase in completeness and frequency over the years, reflecting improved surveillance and data collection systems. <br/>

Interestingly, there was a significant drop in cases observed in the early months of 2020, possibly influenced by the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and associated public health measures.
