# Hepatitis E

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis E is a liver disease caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV), which primarily spreads through the consumption of contaminated water or food. It is prevalent in regions with poor sanitation, particularly parts of Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. The virus has four genotypes; genotypes 1 and 2 are found in humans, while genotypes 3 and 4 also infect animals and occasionally humans. Symptoms include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and nausea. Hepatitis E is usually self-limiting but can become severe in pregnant women and immunocompromised individuals. Effective preventive measures include improved sanitation and access to safe drinking water.

## Highlights

- Hepatitis E cases in China have fluctuated, with peaks typically in March. As of August 2025, there are 2712 cases. <br/>

- A rising trend is observed since 2024, peaking at 3748 cases in March 2025, the highest recorded. <br/>

- Mortality rates are low, with sporadic increases. In 2025, only 8 deaths reported through August. <br/>

- Efforts should focus on controlling transmission in months leading up to March, where heightened activity is common. Continued surveillance and public health strategies remain crucial.<br/>
