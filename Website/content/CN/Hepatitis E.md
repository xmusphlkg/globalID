# Hepatitis E

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Hepatitis E is a liver disease caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV), primarily transmitted through the fecal-oral route, often via contaminated water. It is prevalent in regions with poor sanitation, such as parts of Asia, Africa, and Central America. Symptoms include jaundice, fatigue, nausea, and abdominal pain, typically appearing 2 to 10 weeks after exposure. While the disease is usually self-limiting, it can be severe in pregnant women and individuals with weakened immune systems. Preventive measures include ensuring access to clean water, proper sanitation, and good hygiene practices.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers**: From 2015 to 2025, Hepatitis E cases have increased, peaking in March 2024 with 3,676 cases, indicating a growing prevalence. <br/>

- **Variable Mortality**: Deaths have fluctuated, with notable spikes in December 2017 (6 deaths) and January 2024 (4 deaths), showing occasional increases in mortality. <br/>

- **Seasonal Peaks**: Cases consistently peak in early months, especially March, suggesting seasonal transmission factors. <br/>

- **Current Status**: As of February 2025, there are 3,199 cases and 3 deaths, reflecting a high incidence but relatively low mortality. <br/>
