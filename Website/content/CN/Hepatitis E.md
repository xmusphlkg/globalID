# Hepatitis E

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis E is a liver disease caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV), primarily transmitted through the consumption of contaminated water or food. It is prevalent in regions with poor sanitation, particularly in Asia, Africa, and Central America. Symptoms include jaundice, fatigue, nausea, and fever, and the disease can be severe in pregnant women and individuals with weakened immune systems. Most cases resolve on their own, but severe infections may require hospitalization. Preventive measures include improving sanitation, drinking safe water, and practicing good hygiene.

## Highlights

- **Rising Incidence:** Hepatitis E cases have shown a notable increase in recent years, with a peak in March 2024 at 3676 cases. <br/>
- **Mortality Trends:** While the number of deaths has generally remained low, there were higher mortality spikes in March 2016 (5 deaths) and December 2017 (6 deaths). <br/>
- **Monthly Variability:** Case numbers exhibit significant monthly fluctuations, particularly higher in the spring months compared to other seasons. <br/>
- **Recent Decline:** After reaching a high in early 2024, there has been a decline in cases by June 2024, with 2738 cases reported. <br/>
