# Hepatitis

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver, often caused by viral infections. The most common types are Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E, each caused by different viruses. Hepatitis A and E are usually transmitted through contaminated food or water, while Hepatitis B, C, and D spread via blood or other body fluids. Symptoms can range from mild to severe and include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and liver failure. Vaccines are available for Hepatitis A and B, and treatments exist for chronic Hepatitis B and C.

## Highlights

1. **Rising Cases and Deaths**: From 2014 to June 2024, hepatitis cases and deaths have increased, peaking at 181,000 cases in March 2024 and 265 deaths in June 2024.<br/>
2. **Surge in Deaths Since 2023**: There has been a significant rise in deaths since 2023, with monthly fatalities often exceeding 200, indicating a worsening trend.<br/>
3. **Fluctuating Monthly Cases**: Monthly cases show fluctuations but an overall upward trend, especially from 2023 onwards.<br/>
4. **Possible Seasonal Trends**: Higher case numbers are often seen in the first half of the year, suggesting potential seasonal factors in disease spread.<br/>

The data on Hepatitis cases in mainland China from June 2014 to June 2024 reveals significant fluctuations in both the number of reported cases and the number of deaths. From mid-2014 to 2019, the monthly cases remained relatively steady, with occasional peaks and troughs, and an average range between 110,000 to 140,000 cases. Deaths during this period also remained relatively stable, generally fluctuating between 25 to 60 per month. However, a notable shift occurs starting in early 2020, coinciding with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, where the data indicates some volatility in reported cases and a marked increase in deaths, particularly from late 2022 onwards.

The period from 2020 to mid-2024 shows a significant and concerning trend. Although the number of cases initially sees a dip in February 2020, likely due to the impacts of pandemic-related disruptions, subsequent months show fluctuations with a generally rising trend in cases. More striking is the dramatic rise in deaths, especially from mid-2023 onwards, with a peak in December 2023 reporting 428 deaths. This sharp increase in mortality in recent years suggests a potentially worsening situation in the control and management of Hepatitis, possibly exacerbated by healthcare system strains and overlapping public health challenges. The consistent rise in both cases and deaths highlights the need for intensified public health interventions and resource allocation to address this escalating health concern.

The reported data on hepatitis cases and deaths in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 indicates significant fluctuations in both the number of cases and fatalities. Notably, while the monthly number of hepatitis cases shows variation, the overall trend does not exhibit a consistent upward or downward trajectory until the latter years. In contrast, the mortality data reveals a dramatic increase in the number of deaths starting around mid-2023. For instance, deaths rose sharply from 170 in May 2023 to 428 by December 2023, with the trend continuing into 2024, reaching 265 in June.

This surge in mortality suggests a potential worsening in the severity or management of hepatitis cases, or perhaps an emergence of a more virulent strain or other complicating factors such as co-infections. The significant rise in deaths compared to the relatively stable case numbers in earlier years points to a critical need for further investigation into healthcare infrastructure, treatment protocols, and public health interventions. Additionally, the marked increase in fatalities in recent years emphasizes the urgency of addressing these issues to mitigate the impact of hepatitis on public health in China.
