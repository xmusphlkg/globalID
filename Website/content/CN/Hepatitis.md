# Hepatitis

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver, commonly caused by viral infections, but it can also result from toxins, alcohol, medications, and autoimmune diseases. The most prevalent types are Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E, each caused by different viruses. Symptoms may include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and nausea, though some individuals remain asymptomatic. Chronic hepatitis, particularly types B and C, can lead to severe liver damage, cirrhosis, or liver cancer. Vaccines are available for Hepatitis A and B, while treatment options for other types focus on managing symptoms and preventing complications.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers:** Hepatitis cases in the Chinese mainland have increased significantly, with a peak of 181,006 cases in March 2024. <br/>

- **Increasing Mortality Rates:** Deaths have surged, especially from 2023, with December 2024 recording the highest at 632 deaths, indicating a worsening situation. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations:** There are seasonal fluctuations, with peaks often in the first half of the year, suggesting potential seasonal factors. <br/>

- **Public Health Concern:** The sharp rise in cases and deaths highlights an urgent need for enhanced public health interventions and resource allocation to control the outbreak. <br/>

The data on hepatitis cases in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals several trends and fluctuations. Initially, the number of cases remained relatively stable, with minor fluctuations from month to month. However, starting in 2023, there is a noticeable increase in both the number of cases and deaths. For instance, the number of cases in December 2023 reached 143,778 with 428 deaths, a significant rise compared to previous years. This upward trend continues into 2024, with cases peaking at 181,006 in March and deaths reaching a high of 632 in December. This suggests a potential outbreak or worsening of the hepatitis situation during this period.

The increase in deaths is particularly concerning, as it indicates a rise in the severity or complications associated with hepatitis infections. The data shows a sharp increase in mortality rates starting in 2023, with deaths more than doubling from 2022 to 2023 and continuing to rise in 2024. This could be attributed to several factors, including the emergence of a more virulent strain, reduced access to healthcare, or other socio-economic factors impacting the population's health. The data underscores the need for enhanced public health measures, increased surveillance, and possibly a review of treatment protocols to address this growing health challenge.

The data on hepatitis cases and deaths in mainland China from December 2014 to December 2024 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, the number of deaths remained relatively stable, with minor fluctuations from month to month. However, starting in 2023, there is a noticeable and significant increase in the number of deaths, despite the number of cases not showing a proportionate rise. For instance, in May 2023, there were 141,492 cases with 170 deaths, and by December 2023, the deaths had surged to 428 with 143,778 cases. This trend continues into 2024, with December reporting 632 deaths, the highest in the dataset, despite a similar number of cases compared to previous months.

This sharp increase in mortality rate could suggest several underlying issues. It may indicate a change in the virulence of the hepatitis virus, a decrease in the effectiveness of medical interventions, or potential issues in healthcare delivery systems. The data suggests that while the number of cases has not dramatically increased, the lethality of the disease has, which could warrant further investigation into the causes, such as potential new strains, changes in treatment protocols, or other socio-economic factors affecting healthcare access and quality.
