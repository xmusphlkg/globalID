# Infectious diarrhea

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Infectious diarrhea is a condition caused by viruses, bacteria, or parasites that inflame the gastrointestinal tract. Common pathogens include rotavirus, norovirus, Escherichia coli, and Salmonella. It often leads to symptoms such as frequent loose or watery stools, abdominal cramping, nausea, and sometimes fever. Transmission can occur through contaminated food or water, or person-to-person contact. Prevention strategies include proper sanitation, hygiene practices, and ensuring safe drinking water. While often self-limiting, severe cases, especially in children and the elderly, may lead to dehydration and require medical attention to manage fluid loss and prevent complications.

## Highlights

- **Increase in cases in 2024**: The number of infectious diarrhea cases in September 2024 (121,176) reflects a rising trend compared to the same month in previous years, with higher counts since March 2024.<br/>
- **Low mortality rates**: Despite the rise in cases, the death rate remains minimal, with only two deaths reported in 2024.<br/>
- **Seasonal patterns**: Historically, infectious diarrhea cases tend to peak in summer and early fall, as seen in the higher numbers during July-August across multiple years.<br/>
- **Impact of COVID-19**: A marked reduction in cases was observed during 2020, possibly due to pandemic-related public health measures.<br/>

The analysis of infectious diarrhea cases in mainland China from September 2014 to September 2024 highlights notable seasonal trends. Case numbers typically peak during winter months, particularly in December and January. For instance, January 2018 saw a sharp rise with 186,071 cases, possibly due to increased transmission in colder weather and the impact of seasonal pathogens like rotavirus.

Over time, fatalities have decreased, with low death rates from 2021 onwards. Even during months with high case counts, such as 196,347 in March 2024, only one death was reported. This suggests improvements in healthcare, diagnosis, and treatment. Additionally, the dip in cases during 2022 may reflect the impact of COVID-19-related hygiene measures, although a resurgence in 2023 highlights the ongoing need for vigilant public health efforts.

The analysis of infectious diarrhea mortality in the Chinese mainland from 2014 to 2024 reveals a low and stable death toll despite varying case numbers. In the early years, from 2014 to 2016, deaths ranged from zero to six per month, with no clear correlation between the number of cases and fatalities. For example, high case counts in December 2014 and 2015 resulted in only three and five deaths, while months with lower case numbers sometimes saw similar or fewer fatalities, indicating effective healthcare responses or milder outbreaks.

In subsequent years, particularly from 2021 onwards, a trend of decreasing deaths becomes apparent. Despite the resurgence of cases, the number of monthly deaths remained minimal, underscoring improved public health responses, access to healthcare, and sanitation. Notably, from February 2023 to August 2024, there were no reported deaths, which suggests effective early interventions and community health strategies. The isolated death spikes in March and August 2024, despite high case numbers, appear to be exceptions rather than indicative of broader health system issues.

Overall, the sustained low mortality amidst fluctuating case numbers suggests a positive public health trajectory, with advancements in outbreak management significantly reducing the impact of infectious diarrhea, even in the face of occasional case surges.
