# Infectious diarrhea

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Infectious diarrhea is a condition characterized by frequent, loose, or watery stools resulting from infections by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Common causes include pathogens like *Escherichia coli*, *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *Rotavirus*, and *Giardia*. Transmission often occurs via contaminated food, water, or contact with infected individuals. Symptoms typically include abdominal cramps, fever, nausea, and dehydration. Effective prevention measures include proper hand hygiene, safe food handling, and access to clean water. Treatment focuses on rehydration and, in some cases, antimicrobial therapy depending on the causative agent.

## Highlights

- **Rising Cases:** Incidence has increased from 2014 to 2024, peaking at 196,347 in March 2024. <br/>
- **Seasonal Trends:** Higher cases in summer months suggest environmental or behavioral influences. <br/>
- **Low Mortality:** Mortality rates are low, with most months reporting zero deaths, indicating effective medical interventions. <br/>
- **2024 Surge:** Significant increase in 2024 cases, reflecting potential emerging challenges or better reporting. <br/>

The analysis of infectious diarrhea cases in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals notable trends in both incidence and mortality. There was a generally increasing trend in reported cases over the years, with significant monthly fluctuations. For instance, peaks in cases were observed in summer months like July and August, suggesting possible seasonal factors influencing transmission, possibly linked to higher temperatures or changes in food consumption patterns. Notably, the highest number of cases recorded was in March 2024, with 196,347 cases, which is significantly above the monthly averages for previous years.

Mortality rates remained relatively low throughout the period, with the number of deaths typically ranging from zero to a few per month. The sporadic nature of these fatalities, with no clear pattern correlating with the peaks in case numbers, indicates effective management and treatment protocols in place, reducing the lethality of infectious diarrhea despite the fluctuations in case numbers. This trend suggests that while the disease remains widespread, improvements in healthcare responses have likely contributed to containing the fatality rate. The data also indicate a decrease in reported cases from late 2023 into early 2024, possibly reflecting improved public health measures or reporting changes.

The analysis of deaths due to infectious diarrhea in mainland China from June 2014 to June 2024 reveals several notable trends. The data indicates that the number of deaths remains remarkably low despite substantial fluctuations in the number of reported cases. For example, while cases peaked at 196,347 in March 2024, there was only a single death reported that month. Similarly, significant case counts in July 2017 and December 2017 (119,341 and 174,969, respectively) resulted in minimal deaths (1 and 5, respectively). This trend of low mortality in the face of high case numbers suggests effective public health interventions, including prompt treatment and possibly improved sanitation and awareness campaigns.

Over the ten-year period, there appears to be a general trend of decreasing death rates even as the number of cases varies significantly. For instance, from 2017 onwards, months with high case numbers often report zero deaths, such as July 2021 with 123,462 cases and no deaths. Additionally, the latter years (2021 to 2024) show a reduction in the number of months with reported deaths compared to earlier years. This could indicate improvements in healthcare responses and management of infectious diarrhea cases. Overall, while the incidence of infectious diarrhea remains variable, the mortality associated with it has been effectively minimized, reflecting successful public health strategies.
