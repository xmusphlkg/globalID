# Influenza

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

Influenza, or the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses that infect the nose, throat, and lungs. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, and fatigue. The virus spreads through droplets from coughs or sneezes of infected individuals. It poses significant health risks to young children, elderly people, and those with weakened immune systems. Prevention primarily involves annual vaccinations, which are the most effective measure against the flu. Additional preventive practices include regular handwashing and avoiding close contact with sick individuals. Influenza can lead to serious complications like pneumonia and hospitalization.

## Highlights

- **Peak in Winter Cases:** Influenza cases in China exhibit marked seasonal peaks during winter months, notably in late 2023 and early 2025, indicating increased transmission during colder periods. <br/>

- **Decline in Summer:** As of July 2025, cases have decreased to 78,790, demonstrating the typical downturn in summer compared to winter highs. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate:** Despite high monthly case counts, influenza-related deaths remain low, typically below double digits since 2019, illustrating effective disease management. <br/>

- **Case Fluctuations:** The number of cases varies significantly year-to-year, suggesting changes in influenza strains or public health responses may impact disease spread. <br/>

The influenza data from the Chinese mainland show variable trends in cases and deaths between 2015 and 2025. Initially, in 2015, cases ranged from about 9,000 to 23,000 monthly, with very low mortality. In 2016, there was a sharp rise in cases, particularly in March, reaching over 76,000, indicative of a seasonal flu spike. Despite increased cases, fatalities remained low, pointing to effective healthcare or strain-specific dynamics.

From 2017 to 2019, there was a steady increase in cases, with a dramatic spike in January 2019 when cases exceeded 600,000. December 2019 showed high cases leading into the COVID-19 era, suggesting overlapping respiratory syndromes. A downturn in 2020 aligns with pandemic interventions like mask-wearing and social distancing, possibly reducing influenza spread. However, from 2023 onwards, there is a surge in cases, notably in December 2023, but death rates stayed low, indicating effective treatments or vaccine impacts.

By mid-2025, fluctuating case numbers alongside low deaths suggest seasonal impacts but consistent healthcare response. Despite significant peaks and troughs in monthly cases, reduced mortality may reflect improved medical management, vaccine efficacy, or changes in viral strain characteristics. These trends highlight the need for ongoing adaptation in public health strategies to mitigate influenza's impacts, especially as patterns have shifted post-pandemic.

Influenza mortality data in the Chinese mainland shows notable seasonality, with spikes in cases and deaths typically occurring in winter months. For instance, 2019 experienced a severe season, peaking in January when over 600,000 cases were reported and 143 deaths occurred. This suggests particular challenges that year, potentially due to viral mutations, environmental factors, or healthcare system strains.

Recent data displays massive fluctuations with high case numbers but fewer deaths. March 2023 recorded over 3.7 million cases, yet deaths were limited to 38, indicating improvements in public health interventions or healthcare access. Similar trends in December 2023 and January 2024 reveal significantly high cases but low deaths, reflecting possible impacts of better vaccination coverage and improved treatment protocols.

Overall, the influenza landscape in China seems to be evolving, marked by high case counts but reduced deaths relative to the severity of outbreaks. This could be attributed to increased public awareness and preventive measures, better healthcare provisions, or potentially changes in viral patterns. The data underscores the need for ongoing vigilance and adaptations in public health strategies to manage what remains a complex and seasonally shifting threat. Despite fluctuations in case numbers, the trends suggest a positive trajectory towards minimizing mortality from influenza outbreaks in recent years.
