# Influenza

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses, primarily types A and B. It spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, and fatigue. The flu can lead to severe illness and complications, especially in young children, elderly adults, and individuals with chronic health conditions. Annual vaccination is the most effective prevention method. The virus undergoes frequent genetic changes, necessitating updated vaccines each year to match circulating strains.

## Highlights

- **Significant Increase**: Influenza cases in June 2024 reached 314,709, showing a marked rise compared to previous years. <br/>
- **Fluctuating Mortality**: Deaths remain low with only one in June 2024, though sporadic increases occur, peaking in high incidence months. <br/>
- **Seasonal Peaks**: Clear seasonal peaks in winter months highlight the cyclical pattern of influenza. <br/>
- **Recent Surge**: Early 2023 and 2024 saw dramatic increases, particularly in March 2023 with over 3.7 million cases, indicating potential new strains or reduced immunity. <br/>

The data on influenza cases and deaths in mainland China from mid-2014 to mid-2024 reveals significant temporal fluctuations in both the number of cases and fatalities. Analyzing the trends, we observe a pronounced increase in influenza cases starting in late 2018, peaking dramatically in early 2019 and again in early 2023. For instance, January 2019 saw over 600,000 cases, escalating further to over 1.1 million by December of the same year. This surge aligns with high fatalities, with notable peaks in January and February 2019, suggesting severe outbreaks during these periods.

Similarly, 2023 exhibited another substantial spike in cases, with March reporting over 3.7 million cases, followed by a sustained high incidence through the year, culminating in over 4.1 million cases in December. This period also recorded a significant number of deaths, though the case-fatality ratio remained relatively low compared to the number of cases. Notably, the seasonal pattern of influenza is evident, with higher cases during winter months and lower incidences during the summer. This cyclical nature underscores the importance of targeted public health interventions during peak seasons to mitigate the impact of influenza outbreaks.

The pattern continued with considerable variability, with notable increases in both cases and deaths observed at various intervals, such as in January and February of 2019 and again in December 2019.

From January 2020 onward, the data reflects a decrease in the number of deaths despite fluctuations in cases, likely due to improved healthcare responses and interventions. For instance, January 2020 saw 108 deaths amidst a high case count of 986,543, while subsequent months exhibited a lower death rate even when case numbers surged, such as in December 2023 with 4,113,326 cases but only six deaths.
