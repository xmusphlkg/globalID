# Japanese encephalitis

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Japanese encephalitis is a viral infection caused by the Japanese encephalitis virus (JEV), and it's a major cause of viral encephalitis in Asia. The disease is transmitted to humans through the bite of infected mosquitoes, primarily Culex species, which become carriers after feeding on infected birds or pigs. Most JEV infections are asymptomatic, but severe cases can lead to inflammation of the brain, causing fever, headaches, seizures, and, in severe situations, permanent neurological damage or death. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent the disease, complemented by mosquito control measures to reduce transmission risk.

## Highlights

- **Seasonal Fluctuations:** Japanese encephalitis cases typically peak between August and October, aligning with monsoon season when mosquito vectors are most active, followed by a sharp decline over winter months. <br/>
- **Decreasing Trend:** Over the years, the number of cases has shown a decreasing trend, with recent years (2022-2025) reporting fewer cases compared to earlier years (2015-2018). <br/>
- **Improved Mortality Rates:** Mortality rates have decreased significantly in recent years, demonstrating improved management and treatment protocols. <br/>
- **Current Situation (August 2025):** Although there is a small resurgence with 36 cases in August 2025, overall case numbers remain relatively low, indicating effective control measures. <br/>

The analysis of Japanese encephalitis cases in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals a clear seasonal pattern, with cases peaking in the summer months of July, August, and September. This coincides with the lifecycle of mosquitoes, the primary vectors for the virus. August is often the peak month, with the highest numbers recorded in 2016 (425 cases), 2017 (580 cases), 2018 (904 cases), and 2023 (80 cases). These spikes highlight the influence of environmental conditions on transmission rates, emphasizing the need for effective mosquito control in these months.

The data shows notable fluctuations in fatalities, with August 2018 experiencing 55 deaths alongside the peak in cases. However, despite varying case numbers, there is a general decline in fatalities over the years, pointing to improved healthcare, early diagnosis, and intervention strategies such as vaccination and public health campaigns to reduce mosquito populations. The reduction in both cases and deaths since 2019 suggests success in these measures, although periodic spikes signify ongoing challenges.

Overall, the trend underscores the importance of continuous monitoring and adapting public health strategies to deal with the disease effectively. Focusing on peak transmission months with proactive measures can mitigate the epidemic impact, ensuring healthcare systems are prepared to handle potential outbreaks efficiently.

The data from 2015 to 2025 shows Japanese encephalitis in mainland China follows a distinct seasonal pattern, with a higher incidence and mortality during warmer months, typically between June and September. Notably, August consistently records the highest cases and deaths, such as in 2018 when there were 904 cases and 55 deaths, indicating heightened mosquito activity during this period. Despite fluctuations in case numbers, the death rate does not consistently decline year-on-year. Some months exhibit significant mortality relative to the number of cases, such as in April 2019, with only six cases but 26 deaths. This discrepancy suggests challenges in healthcare access, diagnostic delays, or perhaps more virulent strains present during certain periods.

The data shows improvement or stability in managing the disease, with fewer deaths in recent years, though the occasional high mortality months highlight the need for ongoing vigilance. The seasonal surge necessitates targeted preventative measures and continuous surveillance, especially in peak transmission months. While the dataset suggests some advancements in monitoring and response, maintaining these efforts is crucial to reducing both the incidence and mortality rates further. Overall, the trends underscore the importance of focused interventions during the summer season to mitigate the impact of Japanese encephalitis in mainland China.
