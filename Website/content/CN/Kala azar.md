# Kala azar

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Kala azar, also known as visceral leishmaniasis, is a severe tropical disease caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Leishmania, transmitted through the bites of infected female phlebotomine sandflies. It mainly affects the spleen, liver, and bone marrow, leading to symptoms such as prolonged fever, weight loss, anemia, and swelling of the liver and spleen. Predominantly found in parts of Asia, East Africa, and South America, if untreated, kala azar can be fatal. Control measures include vector control, early diagnosis, and effective treatment, with amphotericin B and miltefosine being among the pharmaceuticals used.

## Highlights

- **Fluctuating Case Numbers:** Monthly cases fluctuate, with a downward trend from 2015 to 2018, then stabilization from 2019 to 2025. A rise in recent cases is noted from 2023 to 2025, especially in February to August. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rates:** Deaths remain low, with only a few each year since 2016. Notably, every year from 2021 to 2025 recorded at least one death. <br/>

- **Seasonal Patterns:** Cases peak in spring and late summer, suggesting a seasonal transmission or reporting pattern. <br/>

- **Recent Developments:** Increased case fluctuations from 2023 to 2025 require targeted interventions during noted high incidence periods. <br/>

The reported data for kala azar cases in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 reflects a fluctuating pattern with variations in the number of cases across different months and years. The initial months in 2015 saw a relatively higher number of cases, peaking at 123 in November, before decreasing over the subsequent months. The pattern over the following years shows a generally declining trend in the number of cases, with occasional spikes, such as in February 2019 and March 2020. The annual data suggest seasonal fluctuations, particularly with increases more commonly observed during the warmer months, possibly due to the environmental conditions that favor the habitat of the sandfly vector responsible for the disease transmission.

Notably, the mortality rate remains low, with only sporadic occurrences of deaths, such as those reported in February 2016, November 2020, February 2022, and assorted months in 2023 and 2025. This may indicate effective treatment protocols and healthcare interventions despite occasional rises in cases. The later years, particularly 2024, observe a resurgence in case numbers with a peak of 39 in February 2025, indicating potential challenges in controlling the vector or changes in socio-environmental factors. This dataset invites further examination of these fluctuations, with consideration for the role of preventive measures, vector control programs, and public health policies in mitigating kala azar's impact across China.
