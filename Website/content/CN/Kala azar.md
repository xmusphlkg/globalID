# Kala azar

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Kala azar, also known as visceral leishmaniasis, is a severe parasitic disease caused by the protozoa Leishmania donovani and transmitted by infected sandflies. Endemic to parts of India, East Africa, and Brazil, it primarily affects the spleen, liver, and bone marrow, leading to symptoms such as fever, weight loss, and anemia. Without timely treatment, it can be fatal due to complications like secondary infections or severe organ damage. Early diagnosis and effective treatments are essential for controlling the disease and reducing mortality rates in affected regions.

## Highlights

- Cases of Kala azar in Chinese mainland show fluctuations with peaks typically observed in spring and early summer. The highest case count recently was in January 2024 with 33 cases. <br/>
- The mortality rate remains low, with a total of four deaths reported from February 2023 to October 2023. No deaths were reported in the first half of 2024. <br/>
- Although there has been no consistent upward or downward trend, the case counts indicate sporadic increases, suggesting ongoing transmission and needing continued monitoring. <br/>
- Efforts should focus on identifying and addressing the environmental or behavioral factors contributing to these fluctuations to prevent future outbreaks. <br/>

The data on Kala azar cases in the Chinese mainland from July 2014 to July 2024 demonstrates a fluctuating pattern of incidence with notable seasonality and periodic peaks in the number of reported cases. Initially, there is a marked increase in cases from July 2014 to November 2015, where the highest monthly cases recorded are in November 2015 with 123 cases. Post this peak, the number of cases tends to decrease, with fluctuations observed year-on-year, particularly in the months leading up to and including December. This trend of cases peaking towards the end of the year and reducing in the first half of the following year is consistent, indicating potential seasonal factors influencing disease transmission or reporting.

Over the years, the number of deaths associated with Kala azar has been relatively low, with sporadic occurrences recorded in February 2016, November 2020, February 2022, February 2023, April 2023, August 2023, and October 2023. This pattern suggests that while the disease remains a public health concern, its fatality rate appears low, possibly reflecting improvements in healthcare interventions or the less virulent nature of the disease within this region. However, the sporadic deaths in recent years may indicate ongoing challenges in managing the disease, underscoring the need for sustained public health efforts.

Despite the steady incidence rate, the reported fatalities remained infrequent, with only sporadic deaths occurring throughout the years. Interestingly, there were isolated deaths reported in February 2016, November 2020, February 2022, and multiple instances in 2023, indicating no clear pattern or correlation to the number of cases detected.

The rarity of fatalities compared to the incidence rate highlights the relatively effective disease management or low virulence of the Kala-azar strains present in China.
