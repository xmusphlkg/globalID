# Kala azar

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Kala azar, also known as visceral leishmaniasis, is a severe parasitic disease caused by the Leishmania donovani complex. It is transmitted to humans through the bite of infected female phlebotomine sandflies. The disease is characterized by prolonged fever, weight loss, enlargement of the spleen and liver, and anemia. Kala azar primarily affects impoverished populations in parts of South Asia, East Africa, and South America. Without treatment, it can be fatal. Control measures include vector control, early diagnosis, and effective treatment with antileishmanial drugs. Public health efforts focus on reducing transmission and improving access to healthcare.

## Highlights

- **Fluctuating Case Numbers:** Kala azar cases in the Chinese mainland have fluctuated, with peaks in late 2015 and early 2023. The highest monthly cases were in November 2015 with 123 cases. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate:** Despite case fluctuations, the mortality rate is low, with only a few deaths reported sporadically, indicating effective management. <br/>

- **Recent Trends:** In 2024, cases have stabilized with a slight increase in August and September, followed by a drop in October. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations:** There is a seasonal pattern, with higher cases often in the latter half of the year, suggesting environmental or social factors influencing transmission. <br/>

The data on Kala azar cases in mainland China from December 2014 to December 2024 shows notable trends. In late 2015, there was a significant spike in cases, with October and November recording 113 and 123 cases, respectively. This peak suggests potential seasonal or environmental factors affecting disease transmission or an outbreak that was subsequently controlled. After this peak, the number of cases generally decreased, stabilizing at a lower level by 2019.

From 2020 onwards, the data indicates a relatively stable pattern with minor fluctuations in monthly cases. The number of deaths remains low throughout the period, with only a few months reporting fatalities. This suggests effective management of the disease in terms of mortality, likely due to improvements in medical treatment and public health interventions. However, sporadic deaths in recent years, such as in February 2023 and October 2023, highlight ongoing challenges in completely eradicating the disease or preventing all fatalities.

Overall, the data reflects a controlled but persistent presence of Kala azar in the region. Continuous public health efforts are necessary to monitor and manage the disease effectively. The consistent reporting of cases without significant increases in deaths underscores the importance of maintaining and enhancing current health strategies to further reduce the disease's impact.
