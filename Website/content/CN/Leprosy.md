# Leprosy

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, mucosal surfaces of the upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Leprosy is known for its long incubation period, often taking years to manifest symptoms. It is transmitted via droplets from the nose and mouth during close and frequent contact with untreated cases. Early diagnosis and treatment with multidrug therapy (MDT) can prevent disability and transmission. Despite its historical stigma, leprosy is now curable, and efforts continue to reduce its prevalence worldwide.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases:** From 2015 to 2025, there is a noticeable decline in leprosy cases in mainland China, with monthly cases dropping from a peak of 108 in March 2016 to 36 in February 2025. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remains extremely low, with only three deaths recorded over the entire period, indicating effective disease management and treatment. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations:** There are fluctuations in case numbers, with some months consistently showing higher cases, suggesting potential seasonal or reporting influences. <br/>

- **Recent Stability:** In the last few years, the number of cases has stabilized, with monthly cases generally remaining below 40, reflecting sustained control efforts. <br/>
