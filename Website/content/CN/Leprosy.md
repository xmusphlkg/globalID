## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansenâ€™s disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Leprosy is characterized by skin lesions, nerve damage, and progressive debilitation, with symptoms varying from mild to severe. It spreads through droplets from the nose and mouth of untreated patients. Despite its historical stigma, leprosy is curable with multidrug therapy (MDT), effectively halting transmission and preventing disabilities. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial for effective management and reducing the risk of complications.

## Highlights

- Leprosy cases in mainland China have significantly declined from 2015 to September 2025, dropping from 108 cases in March 2016 to as low as 10 in September 2024. <br/>
- A notable reduction is seen in 2024, with several months reporting cases below 20. <br/>
- While episodes like February 2023 (40 cases) occur, the overall trend indicates effective control measures, with no deaths since December 2021. <br/>
- The trend in 2025 remains steady at low levels, highlighting the culmination of consistent public health interventions and successful disease management strategies. <br/>

The data on leprosy cases in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 reveals a general trend of decreasing incidence over time, marked by fluctuations and occasional spikes. During the early years, particularly from 2015 to 2017, monthly cases showed volatility, with peaks such as March 2016's 108 cases. Despite these fluctuations, the trend began to shift toward a decrease, evident from 2018 as monthly cases mostly fell below 50, occasionally dipping below 30 by late 2023 and early 2024.

From 2022 onwards, the trend of reduced cases becomes more apparent, with numbers frequently dropping below 30, hitting a low of 10 in September 2024. This suggests improvements in control measures, public health interventions, or possibly changes in detection and reporting accuracy. The data presents only three deaths over the surveyed period, indicating effective disease management and minimal impact on mortality.

Overall, the decrease in case numbers and low fatality rate demonstrate progress in leprosy control in China. This reduction could be attributed to public health initiatives, awareness campaigns, and improvements in healthcare access and disease management strategies. The sustained downtrend in cases accentuates China's successful efforts in mitigating the impact of leprosy within its borders as part of wider public health goals.
