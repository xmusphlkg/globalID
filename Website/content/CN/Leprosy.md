# Leprosy

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's Disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Leprosy is characterized by lesions that may be lighter than the surrounding skin and may have decreased sensation. Though historically stigmatized, leprosy is not highly contagious and can be effectively treated with multi-drug therapy (MDT). Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to prevent disabilities and transmission. Despite being curable, leprosy remains a public health issue in several countries, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions.

## Highlights

- Leprosy cases in the Chinese mainland have shown a significant decline from 75 cases in May 2014 to 31 cases in May 2024. <br/>
- Deaths due to leprosy have remained extremely rare, with only 3 reported deaths from 2016 to 2021. <br/>
- Monthly new cases have displayed seasonal and yearly fluctuations but have generally decreased over the past decade. <br/>
- Recent data for 2024 indicates a slight increase in cases during March and April, but overall, the incidence remains low. <br/>

Over the given period, from May 2014 to May 2024, the incidence of leprosy in the Chinese mainland shows notable fluctuations, with a general declining trend in the number of cases over the years. Initially, monthly reports in mid-2014 to 2016 indicated relatively higher case counts, often exceeding 50 and even peaking at 108 cases in March 2016. Post-2016, there is a visible decrease, with a significant reduction in the monthly case numbers that stabilize to mostly below 50 from 2018 onwards. Notably, months such as February 2015, with just 26 cases, and several months in subsequent years consistently report lower incidences, particularly in 2022, where the numbers frequently drop below 30 cases per month.

The mortality data indicates minimal deaths reported from leprosy over this period, with only four deaths recorded: one each in January 2016, August 2019, and December 2021. This low mortality rate suggests commendable case management and healthcare interventions over the years. The decline in new cases, along with consistent zero or single-digit death figures over the last decade, hints at effective public health strategies and early disease detection and treatment protocols in place. However, the occasional spike in cases observed in specific months may warrant focused investigations to unravel possible causative factors such as seasonal variations, public health lapses, or inaccuracies in reporting.

An analysis of leprosy-related mortality data in Mainland China reveals that despite the steady occurrence of new cases over the years, mortality remains remarkably low. From May 2014 to May 2024, reported cases of leprosy varied monthly, ranging from a high of 108 cases in March 2016 to a low of 13 cases in October 2022. Despite fluctuations in reported cases, the total number of reported deaths is extremely limited, with only four deaths recorded throughout the entire duration of the dataset: one in January 2016, one in August 2019, and one in December 2021.

This remarkable disparity between the number of reported cases and the minimal death toll suggests successful management and treatment protocols in place for leprosy in Mainland China. Given the chronic nature of leprosy and its potential to cause significant morbidity, the ability to maintain near-zero mortality rates over a decade implies effective early diagnosis, continuous monitoring, and treatment adherence. The isolated fatalities could reflect either complications arising from delayed treatment initiation or the presence of comorbid conditions that exacerbated the patient's health status. Overall, these findings underscore the efficacy of existing public health strategies in controlling mortality associated with leprosy, emphasizing the importance of sustained efforts to maintain these outcomes.
