# Leprosy

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, mucosal surfaces, and eyes. Transmission generally occurs through prolonged close contact with untreated individuals, although it is not highly contagious. Symptoms include skin lesions, numbness, and muscle weakness, potentially leading to deformities if untreated. Effective multidrug therapy, available since the 1980s, can cure the disease and prevent disability. Leprosy is now rare in many parts of the world, but it remains a public health issue in certain regions, requiring ongoing monitoring and treatment efforts.

## Highlights

- **Case Reduction**: From 2014 to 2024, there is a discernible reduction in leprosy cases in the Chinese mainland, decreasing from monthly peaks of over 100 cases to just 10-30 cases in 2024. <br/>
  
- **Consistent Zero Deaths**: The data shows that deaths from leprosy remain virtually nonexistent, suggesting effective case management and treatment strategies. <br/>

- **Seasonal Trends**: Cases exhibit seasonal fluctuations with spikes typically observed early in the year, particularly March, observed across multiple years. <br/>

- **Recent Stability**: In the past five years, the number of cases has notably stabilized at lower numbers, indicating sustained control efforts. <br/>

The data on leprosy cases in mainland China from October 2014 to October 2024 indicates several trends and fluctuations over the years. Initially, there is a pattern of relatively high case numbers from late 2014 through 2015 and into early 2016, with a noticeable peak in March 2016 when cases reached 108. This period was followed by a general decline in case numbers, although fluctuations persist. For instance, March 2016's peak is followed by varying monthly case numbers that rarely surpass 70 in the subsequent years until the earlier part of 2018, demonstrating intermittent increases in March and May of 2018. 

From 2019 onwards, the data shows a gradual decline in reported cases with some exceptions, such as a brief surge in July 2020 and slight upticks in early months of 2021 and March 2024. Notably, the mortality statistics are low, with only a single death reported in January 2016, another in August 2019, and one more in December 2021, indicating effective management and treatment protocols. By 2022, case numbers consistently dropped, reaching the lowest levels recorded in October 2022 and subsequent years, with a notable decrease to just 10 cases by September 2024. This declining trend points towards successful public health interventions, increased awareness, and perhaps improved socio-economic conditions affecting disease prevalence.

The relative stability of case numbers does not translate into an increase in mortality, suggesting improved diagnosis, treatment protocols, and potentially the impact of nationwide health policies adapted to control the spread of this disease.

These sporadic deaths also suggest that while the number of cases may fluctuate month-to-month, the healthcare system is robust in ensuring fatalities remain exceptionally rare. The isolated instances of deaths might reflect individual factors like late diagnosis or complications from coexisting conditions, rather than systemic failures in the healthcare response to leprosy. This trend underscores effective medical management and possibly reflects China's commitment to international goals for disease control, as aligned with the World Health Organization's strategies to ultimately eliminate leprosy as a public health concern.
