## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansenâ€™s disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by *Mycobacterium leprae*. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Symptoms include skin lesions, numbness, muscle weakness, and deformities if untreated. The disease is spread through respiratory droplets, but is not highly contagious. Leprosy has a long incubation period, often taking years to show symptoms. Early diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics, such as dapsone, rifampicin, and clofazimine, can prevent disability and transmission. Although rare, leprosy remains a concern in certain tropical regions worldwide.

## Highlights

- **Stable Case Counts**: Leprosy cases in China have remained steady, with monthly counts generally below 100 in recent years. <br/>
- **No Recent Mortality**: Mortality has remained low, with only two deaths reported since 2016. <br/>
- **Seasonal Variation**: There is some seasonal fluctuation, with cases slightly higher in the first half of the year. <br/>
- **Recent Decline**: Leprosy cases have significantly decreased in late 2024, with only 10-14 cases reported in the last few months. <br/>

The reported data for leprosy in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals a consistent pattern of low case counts with no recorded deaths for the majority of the years. Notably, the frequency of new cases fluctuates seasonally, with periodic peaks observed, especially during the early months of each year. The highest recorded case number occurred in March 2016 (108 cases), while the lowest numbers appear in the winter months of 2022 and 2024, with counts dropping to as few as 10 cases in September 2024. This suggests that leprosy remains relatively rare and controlled in China, though sporadic case upticks are noted, especially in certain months.

Deaths from leprosy, however, are extremely rare in the dataset. Out of the entire 10-year span, there were only four recorded deaths: one each in January 2016, August 2019, and December 2021, with the final recorded death in December 2021. The absence of deaths for extended periods, despite the presence of active cases, could indicate effective management of the disease, possibly through timely diagnosis and treatment. This is further supported by the overall low case fatality rate, reinforcing that the public health infrastructure around leprosy in China likely provides adequate medical intervention to prevent fatal outcomes.
