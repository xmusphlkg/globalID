# Leprosy

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, mucosa of the upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Leprosy is known for its potential to cause disfigurement and disability due to progressive nerve damage. It spreads through respiratory droplets, although prolonged close contact is usually necessary for transmission. Early diagnosis and treatment with multidrug therapy (MDT) can cure the disease and prevent complications. While leprosy is rare in developed countries, it still poses a significant health challenge in some tropical and subtropical regions.

## Highlights

- **Declining Trend**: From 2015 to 2025, leprosy cases in China have generally declined. Notably, the monthly cases decreased from 55 in August 2015 to 23 in August 2025. <br/>

- **Deaths Remain Low**: Deaths attributed to leprosy have been sporadic, with only three reported deaths over the 10-year span, none occurring after December 2021. <br/>

- **Periodic Fluctuations**: While overall the trend is downward, some months, such as March 2016 and March 2017, showed spikes in cases. <br/>

- **Recent Stability**: From mid-2024 to August 2025, case numbers remained relatively low, with figures consistently under 40 per month, indicating possible stabilization of the diseaseâ€™s incidence. <br/>

Leprosy cases in mainland China from August 2015 to August 2025 show distinctive trends and variations over the years. Initially, from 2015 to 2016, there were relatively higher numbers of monthly cases, with a notable peak in March 2016 recording 108 cases. This period demonstrated fluctuations, yet there was a general trend of stabilization by the end of 2016, with routine monthly cases hovering around the 30s to 50s range. The data indicate a reduction in the number of deaths associated with leprosy, with only isolated instances in January 2016 and August 2019. This could be indicative of advancements in medical care or public health interventions aimed at managing leprosy.

From 2017 onwards, the data reveal a gradual decline in cases, particularly from mid-2018. The downward trend became more pronounced with the onset of 2022, reaching its lowest recorded cases in October 2022 with just 13 cases per month. Factors contributing to this decrease could include effective public health strategies, heightened awareness, improved medical treatments, and possibly successful early identification and management of the disease. The 2022 to 2025 period shows continued low figures, fluctuating mostly within the 10s to low 30s, suggesting stable control over leprosy's prevalence. Overall, the analysis implies a positive trajectory in leprosy management over the decade, underscoring effective healthcare frameworks and possibly indicating reduced transmission rates within the community.
