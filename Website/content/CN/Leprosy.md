# Leprosy

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, upper respiratory tract, and eyes. The disease has a long incubation period, typically ranging from 2 to 10 years. Transmission occurs through prolonged close contact with an untreated, infected person. Symptoms include skin lesions, numbness, muscle weakness, and eye problems. Early diagnosis and multi-drug therapy (MDT) are crucial for effective treatment and preventing disability. Despite the stigma, leprosy is curable, and efforts continue to eradicate the disease globally.

## Highlights

- Leprosy cases in mainland China have generally shown a declining trend from 2014 to 2024, with noticeable fluctuations in March, notably in 2016 and 2024. <br/>
- The number of leprosy-related deaths has remained extremely low, with only three reported fatalities over the entire period from 2014 to July 2024. <br/>
- Monthly cases dropped significantly from an average of around 50 in 2014 to less than 25 in the first half of 2024, suggesting effective control measures. <br/>
- The disease prevalence shows a seasonal pattern, with higher case counts observed in spring months such as March and April, suggesting potential environmental or behavioral influences. <br/>

However, in 2015 and 2016, there was significant variability, with a peak of 108 cases in March 2016, possibly indicating an outbreak or improved detection. Post-2016, a general decline is observed, with sporadic increases like in March 2018 (80 cases) and August 2019 (53 cases).

From 2020 to 2024, a consistent decline in cases is evident, averaging around 20 to 40 per month, with some months, like October 2022, recording as few as 13 cases. This trend may reflect successful transmission reduction, improved treatment compliance, or enhanced public health interventions. Notably, there were minimal deaths related to leprosy, with only three deaths reported over the entire period. This suggests effective healthcare management, likely through the use of multidrug therapies that prevent fatalities.

Overall, the data indicates significant efforts in disease control and potential to further reduce the leprosy burden in the region. Continued focus on early detection and medical support will be crucial for sustaining and enhancing these positive trends, helping to achieve further reductions in leprosy cases in Mainland China.
