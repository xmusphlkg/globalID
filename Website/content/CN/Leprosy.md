# Leprosy

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Leprosy, also known as Hansen's disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, mucosal surfaces of the upper respiratory tract, and eyes. Leprosy is known for its long incubation period, often taking years for symptoms to appear. It is transmitted via droplets from the nose and mouth during close and frequent contact with untreated cases. Early diagnosis and treatment with multidrug therapy (MDT) can prevent disability. Despite its historical stigma, leprosy is curable, and efforts continue to reduce its prevalence worldwide.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases:** There has been a noticeable decline in leprosy cases from 2015 to 2025, with cases dropping from a high of 108 in March 2016 to 33 in January 2025. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remains extremely low, with only three deaths recorded over the entire period, indicating effective disease management. <br/>
- **Seasonal Variations:** There are fluctuations in monthly case numbers, with some months consistently showing higher cases, suggesting possible seasonal influences. <br/>
- **Recent Stability:** In the last few years, particularly from 2022 onwards, the number of cases has stabilized at a lower level, reflecting successful public health interventions. <br/>
