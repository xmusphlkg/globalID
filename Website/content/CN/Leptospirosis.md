# Leptospirosis

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Leptospirosis is a widespread zoonotic infection caused by pathogenic bacteria of the genus Leptospira. It primarily affects animals, but humans can contract the disease through direct contact with contaminated water, soil, or animal tissues. The symptoms range from mild flu-like signs such as fever, headache, and muscle aches to severe manifestations like Weil's disease, characterized by jaundice, kidney failure, and hemorrhage. Leptospirosis is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in areas with poor sanitation and frequent flooding. Preventive measures include avoiding contaminated water, proper animal vaccination, and using protective clothing during exposure-prone activities.

## Highlights

- Seasonal Trend: Leptospirosis cases peak between July and October annually, likely due to climatic and environmental factors. <br/>

- Fluctuating Case Numbers: Although annual peaks are consistent, the number of cases during these peaks varies, with higher counts in 2021 and 2023. <br/>

- Low Mortality Rate: Deaths from Leptospirosis have generally been low, even when case numbers are high, indicating effective management and treatment strategies. <br/>

- Early 2024 Situation: As of July 2024, case numbers are moderate compared to past years. No deaths have been reported, suggesting ongoing effective disease management. <br/>

The leptospirosis data from mainland China highlights a consistent seasonal pattern in the number of cases, with notable increases during the late summer and early autumn months. This seasonal trend is evident across various years, notably in August and September. The peak occurrences commonly align with the warmer and rainier seasons, conditions favorable for leptospiral transmission due to increased human exposure to contaminated water sources. For instance, August 2016 saw 68 cases, closely followed by 65 in September. Similarly, the surges in 2021, with August reporting 81 cases and September 116 cases, underscore this seasonal pattern. 

Examining mortality figures alongside the case data reveals sporadic yet significant fatality occurrences. Notably, the lethality appears more pronounced in certain months across different years. September 2014, for instance, reported four deaths along with 55 cases, while September 2020 recorded three deaths corresponding to 110 cases, highlighting potential outbreak periods with increased severity. However, a lot of periods show decoupling between case numbers and deaths, suggesting potentially effective mitigation of risk factors or timely medical interventions reducing mortality but not necessarily affecting incident cases. This trend of higher cases with lower corresponding deaths in many months points to a probable improvement in healthcare response and disease management strategies over time.

Notably, there are sporadic instances of death, usually coinciding with higher case numbers, as seen in September 2014, December 2014, and other similar peaks. However, many months with relatively high case numbers, such as July 2023, show no associated deaths, suggesting potential improvements in the management and treatment of the disease over the years.

Particularly, the year 2020 shows a relatively higher death count (10 deaths) distributed across months with increased incidence, such as September and October. The data suggests that while the overall mortality from Leptospirosis in the Chinese mainland remains low, there are critical periods each year when the disease poses a greater risk of death, which are generally aligned with the late summer and early autumn months.
