# Leptospirosis

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Leptospirosis is a bacterial infection caused by Leptospira species, primarily affecting humans and animals. It is transmitted via direct contact with contaminated water or soil, often through cuts or abrasions on the skin. Symptoms vary from mild flu-like signs to severe illness, potentially leading to kidney damage, liver failure, or meningitis. Common in tropical and subtropical regions, leptospirosis is associated with exposure to water contaminated by animal urine, especially from rodents. Preventative measures include avoiding contact with contaminated water and ensuring proper sanitation. Early diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics are crucial for effective recovery.

## Highlights

- **Seasonal Pattern:** Leptospirosis cases display a strong seasonal trend, with peaks occurring between July and September each year. This pattern continues in 2024 with a significant increase in September. <br/>

- **Recent Increase:** In 2024, a notable spike is observed with cases reaching 128 in September, the highest in the dataset, indicating a possible outbreak or increased transmission. <br/>

- **Mortality Decline:** Although cases fluctuate, the number of deaths has significantly decreased, with zero deaths reported in 2024, suggesting improved treatment or lower disease severity. <br/>

- **Annual Variation:** Yearly case numbers show considerable variability, with 2021 and 2024 having the highest September cases, highlighting fluctuating transmission dynamics. <br/>

The leptospirosis data from mainland China between September 2014 and September 2024 reveals significant seasonality and fluctuation in case counts, with higher incidences generally recorded in late summer and early autumn months (July to October). These months consistently show elevated case numbers, peaking during specific years, such as December 2014 (207 cases) and September 2024 (128 cases). The seasonal trend suggests that environmental factors, likely related to rainfall and agricultural activities, may play a role in outbreaks, as leptospirosis is often linked to water and animal contact in rural areas.

The mortality rate is low overall, with the highest number of deaths in 2014, 2020, and 2021. Despite some spikes in cases during these years, fatalities remain sporadic and do not consistently correlate with the highest case counts, indicating possible improvements in clinical management or early detection. For example, September 2020 saw 110 cases and three deaths, while September 2021 had a higher case count (116) but only two deaths. This indicates variability in disease severity or response to treatment across different years and regions.

The data on leptospirosis in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveal several trends in mortality associated with the disease. Notably, deaths were recorded sporadically, with no fatalities reported between February 2015 and May 2016, as well as through much of 2017 and 2018. This lack of consistency may reflect both variances in public health responses or reporting practices. The data also shows cases spiking frequently in the latter months of each year, possibly due to seasonal factors that correlate with environmental conditions favorable to leptospirosis outbreaks.

Despite fluctuations in case numbers, mortality did not always align proportionally with case increases. Some months—such as September and October 2020—stood out with higher mortality rates, having three and four deaths respectively, likely indicating more severe outbreaks or potentially delayed healthcare access. A plateau in reporting occurs in 2017 and again in 2024 with no reported deaths, which may signify improved disease management or other mitigating factors. However, since 2022, cases have continued to rise without associated increases in mortality, suggesting progress in diagnostic and therapeutic interventions. Overall, this pattern suggests a complex interplay of factors underlying disease severity and outcomes, necessitating continuous surveillance and adaptation of public health strategies.
