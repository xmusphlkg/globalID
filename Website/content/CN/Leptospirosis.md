# Leptospirosis

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Leptospirosis is a bacterial infection caused by pathogenic spirochetes of the genus Leptospira. It is a zoonotic disease transmitted from animals to humans, often through contact with water contaminated by the urine of infected animals, such as rodents. Symptoms range from mild flu-like manifestations to severe illness, including Weil's disease, characterized by jaundice, renal failure, and hemorrhage. Leptospirosis is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions but can occur worldwide. Key preventive measures include avoiding contact with contaminated water and employing protective clothing. Early diagnosis and antibiotic treatment are crucial to managing the disease effectively.

## Highlights

- Leptospirosis cases exhibit seasonal variation, with peaks generally occurring in August to October each year, indicating potential environmental and behavioral risk factors associated with these months.  
<br/>
- Mortality rates from Leptospirosis are relatively low but sporadically observable, with variations occurring without consistent patterns across different months and years.  
<br/>
- There has been a decrease in the monthly number of cases and deaths since 2022, suggesting potential improvements in public health measures, awareness, or treatment protocols.  
<br/>
- The data for May 2024 shows an increase in reported cases compared to previous years, underscoring the need for continued vigilance and possibly revised preventive strategies.

The reported data on Leptospirosis in mainland China from 2014 to May 2024 reveal seasonal and annual fluctuations in both cases and deaths. A notable rise in cases is observed typically during the latter half of the year, particularly from June to December, with peak incidences frequently occurring in August and September across several years. For instance, in 2014, August recorded 61 cases, and in December 207 cases, showing significant monthly variability. Death counts, although generally low, peaked occasionally in specific months - notably in September 2014 (4 deaths), December 2014 (1 death), June 2015 (1 death), and September 2020 (3 deaths).

Analyzing longer-term trends, the data exhibit a gradual rise in reported cases and corresponding mortality from 2014 till 2021, followed by a stabilization or slight decline thereafter. For example, 2020 saw a dramatic spike with cases peaking at 110 in September and associated deaths, while post-2021 data, particularly 2022 and onwards, reflect a more tempered case count with relatively lower mortality rates. The highest deaths within a single month are recorded in October 2020 with 4 deaths. These fluctuations indicate possible outbreaks or seasonal climactic influences on the transmission dynamics of Leptospirosis. The data suggest the need for targeted public health interventions, especially during the identified high-risk months to mitigate the risk and manage the cases more effectively.
