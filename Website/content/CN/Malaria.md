# Malaria

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, transmitted to humans through the bites of infected Anopheles mosquitoes. The main symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and muscle pain, which can progress to severe complications like anemia, respiratory distress, and organ failure if untreated. Malaria predominantly affects tropical and subtropical regions, with the highest burden in Sub-Saharan Africa. Preventive measures include insecticide-treated mosquito nets, indoor spraying, and antimalarial drugs. Prompt diagnosis and treatment are crucial to reduce mortality and prevent the spread of the disease.

## Highlights

- Malaria cases in mainland China showed a significant decline from 2020 onwards, reaching their lowest in 2021, likely due to effective public health interventions. <br/>
- Since 2022, there has been a gradual increase in malaria cases, with a notable rise in 2023 and 2024, indicating potential resurgences or changes in transmission dynamics. <br/>
- Deaths have remained relatively low, with sporadic increases but no clear upward trend, suggesting improved case management and treatment. <br/>
- The peak of malaria cases typically occurs in the summer months (June to August), consistent with the seasonal transmission patterns of the disease. <br/>

The reported cases of malaria in the Chinese mainland from mid-2014 to mid-2024 demonstrate a significant overall decline, particularly notable in the years following 2020. The data reveals a consistent seasonal trend, with higher cases typically occurring from June to August each year, reflecting the peak transmission period. For instance, the highest number of cases within a year was frequently reported in June or July, such as 2015 (June, 386 cases), 2016 (May, 369 cases), and 2023 (July, 289 cases). The marked reduction in cases beginning in 2020, with numbers dropping from a high of 348 cases in January 2020 to as low as 26 cases in January 2022, likely indicates the impact of public health interventions, possibly influenced by broader health policy changes during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Mortality associated with malaria also shows a decreasing trend, although less pronounced. The number of deaths per month generally remained low, with occasional spikes. The years with the highest annual fatalities include 2015 and 2016, each with 26 deaths, but since then, the numbers have stabilized or decreased. The data from 2020 onwards shows fewer fluctuations in deaths, with several months reporting zero fatalities. The sustained decrease in both cases and deaths from 2021 to 2024 suggests successful long-term strategies in malaria control and possibly improved healthcare access and treatment protocols.

However, the overall trend in malaria cases and deaths shows a decline, particularly evident from 2020 onwards, which aligns with a significant reduction in malaria cases. The year 2020 marks a notable drop in both cases and deaths, likely influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic and its impact on public health measures and reporting systems.

Examining the later years, particularly from 2020 to 2024, the data indicates a generally lower incidence of deaths, even with periodic spikes in cases. The continuous decrease in both malaria cases and related deaths towards the end of the dataset, particularly in 2022 and beyond, underscores the potential success of sustained public health efforts in reducing the malaria burden in mainland China.
