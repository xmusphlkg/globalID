# Malaria

Version: September 2025

## Introduction

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, which are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected Anopheles mosquitoes. The disease is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in parts of Africa, Asia, and South America. Symptoms typically include fever, chills, headache, nausea, and vomiting, which can progress to severe illness and even death without treatment. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial, often involving antimalarial medications. Preventative measures include mosquito control strategies, such as insecticide-treated nets and indoor spraying, as well as prophylactic drug therapies for high-risk populations.

## Highlights

- Malaria cases showed significant reductions from 2020 to early 2022, with cases dropping to as low as 26 in January 2022, indicating effective control measures.<br/>
- A resurgence is noted from mid-2022 onward, peaking at 437 cases in June 2025, marking the highest monthly count in recent years.<br/>
- Deaths have remained relatively low, often below three per month, suggesting effective treatment efforts alongside preventive measures.<br/>
- The comparison of 2020 to 2025 data indicates an overall increase in malaria cases, highlighting a need for strengthened surveillance and intervention strategies.<br/>

The reported data from 2015 to 2025 indicates fluctuations in malaria cases in the Chinese mainland with notable variations. In 2015, the case numbers remain relatively stable until a peak of 333 cases in January 2016. Higher figures are noted in early 2016, especially in May and June with 369 and 356 cases, respectively. However, there's a downward trend from July 2016, with figures dropping to 195 by November. This pattern suggests potential seasonal impacts, possibly linked to climatic or socio-environmental factors.

From 2017 to 2019, a consistent decline in cases is evident, reaching its lowest in March 2019 with 167 cases. However, early 2020 witnesses a sharp decrease, with April registering only 54 cases, indicating potential interventions or reporting changes. This coincides with global disruptions from the COVID-19 pandemic, possibly affecting reporting or healthcare access. During 2020, cases remain low compared to previous years, bottoming out at 40 in May before climbing again.

Post-2021, cases gradually increase, peaking at 437 in June 2025, the highest recorded in the dataset. This upward trend raises concerns about vector control efficacy or possible resistance patterns among parasites. The death rates, usually low, occasionally spike, aligning with months of high cases or suggesting treatment lapses. Overall, the data reflects a complex interaction of health interventions, environmental factors, and socio-economic conditions impacting malaria prevalence.
