# Measles

Version: October 2025

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the measles virus, a member of the paramyxovirus family. It primarily affects children but can occur at any age. Transmission occurs through respiratory droplets from coughs or sneezes, with the virus remaining viable in the air or on surfaces for several hours. Symptoms typically appear 10-12 days after exposure and begin with fever, cough, runny nose, and conjunctivitis, followed by a characteristic rash. Complications can include pneumonia, encephalitis, and death. Vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective prevention method, offering lifelong immunity.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases**: Measles cases have significantly decreased from a peak in 2016, with consistent declines observed from 2019 onward, reaching low levels in recent years.<br/>
- **Zero Deaths**: Since January 2020, no measles-related deaths have been recorded, highlighting improvements in disease management and healthcare response.<br/>
- **Recent Uptick in Cases**: A moderate increase in cases was observed in early 2025 compared to preceding months, indicating potential vulnerabilities that may need monitoring.<br/>
- **Sustained Control**: The consistent decrease in cases over recent years suggests effective vaccination and public health measures in place to control the disease.<br/>

The data for measles cases in mainland China from October 2015 to October 2025 show significant trends and shifts. Initially, there is a sharp rise in cases from late 2015, peaking at 5,488 in April 2016, before steadily declining by the end of that year. This peak could be due to various factors, including seasonal changes or fluctuations in vaccination rates. Following this surge, the case numbers stabilize from 2017, remaining mostly below 1,000 per month, indicating improved control measures.
 
Starting in 2018, there is a noticeable decrease in cases, with zero deaths reported from 2019 onwards. The steady decline continues in 2020 and 2021, with cases dropping to double digits, potentially influenced by COVID-19 interventions such as social distancing, which might have inadvertently reduced measles transmission. This period signifies effective public health responses, perhaps due to enhanced vaccination coverage or better outbreak management strategies.

From 2022 to 2025, there is a maintenance of low case numbers, with occasional moderate increases, but still far below the 2016 peak. These patterns highlight a sustained control over measles in China, with no reported deaths in recent years, underscoring the effectiveness of ongoing public health measures and the importance of vaccinations in maintaining low transmission rates.
