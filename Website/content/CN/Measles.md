# Measles

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the measles virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. It primarily affects children but can occur at any age. The disease is characterized by symptoms such as high fever, cough, runny nose, and a classic red rash that typically starts on the face and spreads to the rest of the body. Measles spreads through respiratory droplets from coughs and sneezes of an infected person. Complications can include pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, particularly in malnourished children and individuals with weakened immune systems. Vaccination is highly effective in preventing the disease.

## Highlights

- **Sharp decline in cases:** Measles cases in the Chinese mainland have significantly decreased since the peak years of 2015, dropping from over 8,000 cases in April 2015 to just 111 cases in August 2024. <br/>
- **Sustained reduction in deaths:** Since February 2019, no measles-related deaths have been reported, indicating improved disease control and health outcomes. <br/>
- **Stable low incidence post-2020:** From 2020 onward, monthly case counts have remained under 200, showing sustained control of measles transmission. <br/>
- **Recent slight uptick:** In 2024, there has been a slight rise in cases compared to previous years, suggesting ongoing monitoring and vaccination efforts are crucial. <br/>

The analysis of measles cases in mainland China from August 2014 to August 2024 shows key trends in incidence. A sharp increase occurred from late 2014, peaking in April 2015 with 8,265 cases, possibly reflecting a lapse in vaccination coverage or the introduction of a new strain. After this peak, cases steadily declined, indicating successful public health interventions. Mortality remained low throughout, suggesting effective clinical management.

From 2017 onwards, measles cases continued to decline, stabilizing by 2019. A significant drop occurred in 2020, likely due to public health measures linked to COVID-19, such as reduced travel and increased vaccination efforts. Measles cases remained low into 2023 and 2024, showing the ongoing impact of sustained control measures. 

While there were fluctuations in monthly cases during these last two years, zero deaths were reported, suggesting effective disease control and healthcare responses. Overall, the trend points to improved vaccination coverage and strong public health efforts in maintaining low measles incidence in mainland China over the observed period.

The analysis of measles-related deaths in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals notable trends and shifts in mortality despite varying case numbers. In 2014 and 2015, the number of deaths fluctuated with spikes in January to April of 2015, where the highest mortality (7 deaths) was recorded in April despite high case numbers during this period. As measles cases rose significantly in early 2015, peaking in April with 8,265 cases, deaths also showed a corresponding increase. However, after 2015, there was a noticeable reduction in both cases and deaths, indicating potential improvements in disease management or increased immunity.

From 2016 onwards, there is a substantial decrease in the number of deaths, with only occasional fatalities reported up to 2017. After 2017, deaths become increasingly rare, and by 2018, there were no deaths reported, despite cases still being recorded. This pattern of zero fatalities continues through to 2024, even during minor fluctuations in the number of cases. This suggests the possibility of enhanced healthcare interventions, increased vaccine coverage, or better outbreak management, contributing to the sustained reduction in mortality. The data showcases a critical public health achievement, particularly in the successful elimination of measles-related deaths over the latter half of the reporting period.
