# Measles

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease primarily affecting children. Caused by the measles virus, it spreads through respiratory droplets from coughs and sneezes. Initial symptoms include high fever, cough, runny nose, and red eyes, followed by a characteristic red rash that usually starts on the face and spreads downward. Measles can lead to serious complications such as pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, particularly in malnourished children and those with weakened immune systems. Vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective way to prevent the disease and is essential for achieving herd immunity.

## Highlights

- **Measles incidence in decline:** Measles cases in mainland China have significantly decreased since a peak in 2015, from 8,265 cases in April 2015 to 118 cases by September 2024.<br/>
- **Zero deaths in recent years:** No measles-related deaths have been reported since December 2017, suggesting improvements in disease control and healthcare response.<br/>
- **Post-pandemic stability:** Since 2020, after a dramatic reduction in cases, measles incidence has remained low, averaging around 100-200 cases per month from 2020 to 2024.<br/>
- **Sporadic increases:** A small uptick in cases is seen in 2024, with a rise from 51 in January to 118 in September, but no associated deaths.<br/>

From the data on measles cases in mainland China between 2014 and 2024, there are distinct patterns of fluctuation over the years. A significant surge in cases is observed between late 2014 and early 2016, with peaks in April 2015 (8,265 cases) and March 2016 (5,437 cases). This period saw higher incidence rates, with some months reporting thousands of cases and corresponding fatalities, such as 2015 February to April, where both cases and deaths increased. However, from mid-2016 onwards, there was a gradual decline in both cases and deaths, reflecting effective public health interventions, vaccination programs, or natural epidemiological trends. 

By 2017, cases started to steadily decline, and the death count remained extremely low or absent. The number of cases per month significantly dropped by 2020, coinciding with the global pandemic and potentially the implementation of strict public health measures like lockdowns, which might have also impacted the spread of measles. From 2020 to 2024, the reported cases remained below 300 per month, with no deaths, suggesting a controlled epidemiological environment for measles transmission. The persistent low levels post-2020 indicate sustained improvements in measles control efforts.

The effectiveness of public health interventions, likely including widespread immunization campaigns and improvements in healthcare access and quality, is evident in this declining trend of measles mortality.
