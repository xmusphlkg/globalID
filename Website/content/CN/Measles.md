# Measles

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the measles virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. Symptoms typically appear 10 to 14 days after exposure and include high fever, cough, runny nose, and a characteristic red rash that usually starts on the face and spreads. Measles can lead to serious complications, such as pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, particularly in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent measles and its associated complications.

## Highlights

- **Dramatic Decline in Cases:** From 2015 to 2020, measles cases dropped significantly, from over 8,000 in April 2015 to just 58 in April 2020, indicating effective control measures. <br/>

- **Low Incidence Maintained:** Between 2020 and 2023, cases remained low, generally under 100 per month, showing sustained disease control. <br/>

- **Recent Increase Observed:** In 2024, cases rose, peaking at 201 in June, and continued to rise into January 2025 with 344 cases, suggesting a potential resurgence. <br/>

- **Zero Deaths Since 2020:** No deaths have been reported since 2020, reflecting effective treatment and management. <br/>

The data on measles cases in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals a significant decline in reported cases over the years, with a notable peak in 2015. In 2015, the number of cases was highest, with a peak in April at 8,265 cases, and a total of 37,639 cases for the year. This year also saw the highest number of deaths, totaling 34. The subsequent years show a marked decrease in both cases and deaths, with 2016 reporting 25,370 cases and 17 deaths, and 2017 further reducing to 7,068 cases and 4 deaths. This trend of declining cases continues through 2020, where the number of cases drops significantly to 1,164, with no reported deaths.

From 2021 onwards, the data indicates a stabilization in the number of cases, maintaining a low level with minor fluctuations. The years 2021 to 2024 show a consistent pattern of low case numbers, ranging from 38 to 344 cases per month, with no reported deaths. This suggests effective control measures and possibly high vaccination coverage, contributing to the containment of measles outbreaks. The data for 2025, although incomplete, shows an increase in cases in January, which could indicate a seasonal pattern or a temporary lapse in control measures. Overall, the data reflects successful public health interventions in reducing measles incidence in mainland China over the decade.
