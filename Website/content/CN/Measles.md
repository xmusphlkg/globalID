# Measles

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the measles virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. It primarily affects children but can occur at any age. The infection spreads through respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing. Early symptoms include high fever, cough, runny nose, and red eyes, followed by a characteristic red rash that begins on the face and spreads downward. Complications can be severe, leading to pneumonia, encephalitis, and even death. Vaccination is the most effective prevention method, with the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine providing robust immunity and helping to reduce outbreaks.

## Highlights

- Measles cases in China have significantly declined from thousands monthly in 2015 to just 135 in July 2025, showcasing effective control measures. <br/>
- Measles-related deaths were eliminated from 2018 onwards, with no deaths recorded from 2018 through July 2025, indicating successful management strategies. <br/>
- 2025 saw a temporary increase in cases, peaking in January and February with over 300 cases before declining mid-year. This suggests possible seasonal variability or effective outbreak responses. <br/>
- Overall, China has maintained low measles incidence through sustained prevention and vaccination efforts, highlighting improvement in public health strategies over the decade. <br/>

The data for Measles cases in the Chinese mainland over a span of several years, starting from mid-2015, reveals notable trends and fluctuations. Initially, in 2015, the number of reported Measles cases was substantially high, with a peak in March 2016, reaching 5,488 cases. This high incidence period was marked by some mortality, though deaths remained relatively low compared to the number of cases. Following this peak, there was a steady decline in cases throughout the latter half of 2016, 2017, and the following years. By 2018, cases significantly decreased to below 1,000 per month, indicating a successful control phase that extended into 2019.

The period from 2020 onwards shows a dramatic reduction in Measles cases, extensively mirroring the global public health interventions and the possible impact of COVID-19-related measures, which likely altered patterns of disease transmission. By 2021 and 2022, monthly cases had fallen to double-digits, suggesting an effective containment phase. Remarkably, throughout 2023 and early 2024, the cases remained low, punctuated with sporadic increases. However, by late 2024 and early 2025, a resurgence is observed with increasing monthly case numbers, indicating the need for renewed focus on Measles surveillance and vaccination programs to prevent a potential resurgence of outbreaks. This gradual resurgence highlights the ever-present challenge of maintaining high vaccination coverage and monitoring disease dynamics to prevent larger outbreaks.
