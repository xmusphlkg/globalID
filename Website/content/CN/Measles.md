# Measles

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the measles virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. It primarily affects children but can occur in unvaccinated adults. The disease spreads through respiratory droplets from coughing and sneezing. Initial symptoms include high fever, cough, runny nose, and inflamed eyes, followed by a characteristic red rash that usually begins on the face and spreads to the rest of the body. Complications can include pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, particularly in young children and immunocompromised individuals. Vaccination with the MMR (measles, mumps, and rubella) vaccine is highly effective in preventing measles.

## Highlights

- **Significant Decline**: From 2015 to 2025, measles cases in the Chinese mainland have drastically decreased, from over 2000 cases in some months of 2015 to below 100 cases in August 2025. <br/>

- **Mortality Rate**: The number of deaths associated with measles has been effectively reduced to zero since 2020, indicating successful interventions and disease management. <br/>

- **Recent Trends**: Slight increases in case numbers were observed in early 2025, but they remain significantly lower than previous years, with a general downward trend in subsequent months. <br/>

- **Control Measures Effective**: Consistent reductions highlight successful vaccination programs and public health measures in controlling measles transmission. <br/>

The Measles case data from the Chinese mainland showcases a significant fluctuation in reported cases from 2015 to 2025. Analyzing the data, a notable high was observed in early 2016, especially between March and May where the cases peaked above 5,000. This surge could be attributed to increased transmission during that period, potentially attributed to factors such as seasonal patterns, vaccination coverage gaps, or reporting errors. Mortality in the data remains relatively low, even during peak periods, which might suggest effective clinical management and recovery strategies for measles-infected individuals during these episodes.

Post-2016, a significant decline in cases is apparent, with the number eventually reaching a more controlled level from 2019 onwards. This decline could indicate improvements in public health initiatives, possibly through aggressive vaccination campaigns or strengthened surveillance and outbreak response measures. The years following 2020 show a sustained low incidence, likely reflecting effective containment and control measures in the population. Notably, the data presents a persistent absence of fatalities from 2019 onwards, underscoring enhancements in healthcare or the potential success of immunization in preventing severe disease developments. Despite minor rises in 2024 and 2025, the overall trend indicates a strong containment of measles, emphasizing the importance of continued surveillance and immunization efforts to sustain these gains.

Analyzing the data for reported measles cases and associated deaths in mainland China reveals a notable decline in measles-related deaths over the years from 2015 to 2025. The initial period from August 2015 to December 2016 saw fluctuations in both cases and deaths, with the number of cases peaking in March and April 2016, at over 5,000 cases per month, and deaths reaching a maximum of five deaths in both November 2015 and March 2016. However, the incidence of deaths appears to have sharply decreased after this period, correlating with a significant drop in the number of confirmed measles cases.

From 2017 onward, the data shows a consistent absence of reported deaths, despite the fluctuating number of cases reported each month. It suggests a possible successful implementation of healthcare interventions, such as vaccination campaigns, that have effectively reduced the mortality risk associated with measles. The notable decline in both the number of cases and mortality over the years highlights the success of public health strategies in controlling and minimizing the impact of measles in China. This trend illustrates an essential public health victory, as no deaths were recorded from 2017 to 2025 while the reported number of cases also declined substantially compared to earlier years.
