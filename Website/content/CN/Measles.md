# Measles

Version: June 2025

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the measles virus, a paramyxovirus of the genus Morbillivirus. It primarily affects children but can occur in unvaccinated individuals of any age. Transmission occurs through respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing. Initial symptoms include high fever, cough, runny nose, and conjunctivitis, followed by a characteristic red rash. Complications can include pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, especially in malnourished or immunocompromised individuals. Vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective way to prevent infection, significantly reducing the incidence of measles worldwide.

## Highlights

- **Significant Decline in Cases**: From a peak of over 5000 monthly cases in 2015, measles cases in China have steadily declined, reaching under 156 cases by June 2025. <br/>
  
- **Zero Deaths in Recent Years**: Since 2020, there have been zero reported deaths due to measles, showcasing significant improvements in disease control. <br/>

- **Periodic Fluctuations in Case Numbers**: While overall cases have declined, periodic increases are noted, such as the surge to 344 cases in January 2025, indicating ongoing monitoring is necessary. <br/>

- **Successful Control Measures**: Continuous reduction in cases demonstrates effective vaccination and public health strategies, enhancing measles control efforts across mainland China. <br/>

The measles cases in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to mid-2025 display a fluctuating but clear downward trend, particularly evident after 2016. Initially, in 2015, the number of cases was significantly high, peaking in June at over 5,000. This high number was coupled with varying monthly death counts. However, by the end of 2016, there was a notable reduction in both cases and fatalities. The following year, 2017, marked the beginning of sustained lower case numbers, typically remaining below 1,000 per month with only sporadic deaths recorded. The trend of reduced cases continued into 2019, indicating effective public health measures potentially contributing to curbing the spread of measles.

The onset of the COVID-19 pandemic in early 2020 coincided with a noticeable drop in measles cases to fewer than 100 per month, with no recorded deaths. This trend persisted throughout 2021 and 2022, likely reflecting increased public health interventions such as vaccination campaigns, improved healthcare infrastructure, and heightened general health precautions. Post-pandemic years, specifically from 2023 onward, show a slow increase in cases, yet they remain relatively low compared to pre-2019 levels. This consistent low case count with no deaths suggests effective control measures have been largely maintained. These observations indicate that despite brief fluctuations, robust health systems and proactive policies have crucially minimized the impact and spread of measles in the region.

The analysis of measles-related deaths in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to mid-2025 highlights a notable trend of decreasing mortality over time despite fluctuations in reported cases. In 2015 and 2016, a total of 24 deaths were recorded, primarily during months of higher case counts, such as March and November 2015, which exhibited a slightly higher mortality. This initial phase demonstrates the burden of measles mortality aligning closely with seasonal peaks typical of infectious diseases. However, as time progressed, the number of deaths associated with measles significantly declined, reaching zero deaths from mid-2018 onwards, despite ongoing low-level transmission, as evidenced by the persistent reporting of cases.

Several factors potentially contribute to this decline in deaths despite ongoing cases. Continued efforts in vaccination, improved public health responses, and the strengthening of healthcare systems likely played pivotal roles in mitigating mortality risks associated with measles. Additionally, the increasing public health awareness and potentially better access to healthcare services for complications arising from measles may have contributed to improved survival outcomes. While the dataset indicates a sustained presence of measles cases, the elimination of associated deaths underscores a significant achievement in public health, reflecting enhanced disease control measures over the decade.
