# Measles

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the measles virus. It primarily affects children and is characterized by symptoms such as high fever, cough, runny nose, and a distinctive red rash. The virus spreads through respiratory droplets from coughs and sneezes. Complications can include pneumonia, encephalitis, and death, especially in young children and those with weakened immune systems. Vaccination with the MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine is highly effective in preventing the disease and has significantly reduced measles incidence worldwide.

## Highlights

- **Significant Decline in Cases:** From a peak of 8,265 cases in April 2015, the number of measles cases has significantly decreased to 201 cases in June 2024.<br/>
- **Zero Deaths Since 2020:** There have been no reported deaths due to measles in the Chinese mainland since December 2019.<br/>
- **Low Incidence in Recent Years:** Monthly cases have remained under 100 since February 2020, with a slight increase observed in recent months, peaking at 201 cases in June 2024.<br/>
- **Sporadic Increases:** Occasional spikes in case numbers, such as in April and May 2024, indicate the need for continued surveillance and vaccination efforts.<br/>

The data for measles cases in mainland China from June 2014 to June 2024 reveals significant trends in the incidence and control of the disease over this period. Initially, the number of reported cases was high, peaking in mid-2015 with the highest monthly count in April 2015 at 8,265 cases. However, following this peak, there was a marked decline in the number of cases, suggesting successful public health interventions and vaccination campaigns. By late 2016, the number of monthly cases had decreased significantly, consistently remaining below 1,000 and often below 500 cases per month.

From 2017 onwards, the downward trend continued, with monthly cases rarely exceeding 500 and deaths becoming almost non-existent, indicating improved disease management and possibly higher vaccination coverage. By 2020, the impact of COVID-19 and related public health measures might have further influenced the sharp decline in reported measles cases, with monthly cases dropping to double digits. This reduction persisted through 2021 and beyond, with the number of cases remaining very low up to 2024, where even the highest monthly case count (in April 2024) was only 201, with zero reported deaths since 2018. This trend underscores the effectiveness of sustained vaccination programs and robust public health measures in controlling and nearly eliminating measles outbreaks in mainland China.

Throughout the subsequent years, particularly from 2016 onwards, a substantial reduction in both cases and deaths was observed. This consistent decline suggests effective public health interventions and increased immunization coverage.
