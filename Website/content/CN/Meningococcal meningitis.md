# Meningococcal meningitis

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Meningococcal meningitis is a severe bacterial infection caused by *Neisseria meningitidis*. It inflames the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. Transmission occurs through respiratory droplets or close contact. Symptoms include sudden fever, headache, neck stiffness, nausea, and altered mental status. Prompt antibiotic treatment is crucial to prevent severe complications or death. Vaccination is the primary preventive measure, recommended for high-risk groups and during outbreaks. Early diagnosis and public health interventions are essential to control the spread of this potentially fatal disease.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing Trend in Cases:** Overall, there has been a general decline in meningococcal meningitis cases from 2014 to 2024, with notable fluctuations. <br/>
- **Seasonal Variation:** The disease exhibits some seasonal variation, with higher cases in winter and early spring months. <br/>
- **Mortality Rates:** The number of deaths has remained relatively low, with occasional spikes, suggesting improved management or treatment. <br/>
- **Recent Stability:** In recent years, particularly from 2020 to 2024, the number of cases and deaths has stabilized, with fewer severe outbreaks. <br/>
