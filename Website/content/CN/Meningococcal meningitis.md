# Meningococcal meningitis

Version: June 2025

## Introduction

Meningococcal meningitis is a severe bacterial infection caused by Neisseria meningitidis, leading to inflammation of the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It's a major public health concern due to its rapid progression, high mortality rate, and potential for outbreaks, particularly in the "meningitis belt" of sub-Saharan Africa. Initial symptoms include sudden fever, headache, and neck stiffness, which can escalate to more severe effects like altered consciousness and shock. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial; antibiotics and supportive care are key components of management. Vaccination remains the best preventative strategy against meningococcal meningitis.

## Highlights

- Between 2015 and mid-2025, the incidence of Meningococcal meningitis in mainland China demonstrated fluctuating trends, with peaks observed in early months of 2025 and 2019. <br/>
- The highest number of cases was recorded in January 2025 with 26 cases and 2 deaths, indicating a recent surge in incidence. <br/>
- The fatality rate remained relatively low throughout the period, with sporadic increases, such as 6 deaths in December 2017 and 4 deaths in March 2016. <br/>
- Since 2020, the general trend has been a decline in cases, though there are occasional rises, stressing the importance of ongoing monitoring and preventive measures. <br/>

The data for meningococcal meningitis in mainland China from mid-2015 to mid-2025 shows seasonal patterns, with higher case counts typically observed in winter months, especially December through March. Notable spikes occurred in December 2015 (20 cases), December 2017 (24 cases), and December 2019 (15 cases), which may point to environmental or social factors like colder temperatures or increased indoor congregation facilitating transmission. These seasonal surges suggest cyclical trends possibly linked to climate or public behavior patterns during colder periods.

Mortality figures have remained relatively low despite fluctuations in case numbers, with occasional increases during peak months, such as March 2016 and December 2017, when clusters likely occurred, or when more virulent strains might have circulated. The pandemic year of 2020 marked a significant decline in case reports, likely due to COVID-19 mitigation measures like social distancing and heightened hygiene practices reducing the spread of infectious diseases generally.

However, the post-2020 period shows a resurgence, with 2025 data indicating another significant rise in cases, suggesting a return to pre-pandemic conditions and behaviors. This trend underscores the need for continued vigilance in monitoring meningococcal meningitis patterns, particularly during high-risk winter months, to anticipate and manage potential outbreaks effectively.
