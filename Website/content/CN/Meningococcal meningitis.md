# Meningococcal meningitis

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Meningococcal meningitis is a serious bacterial infection caused by Neisseria meningitidis, affecting the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It often results in symptoms like sudden fever, headache, and a stiff neck. The disease can progress rapidly, leading to severe complications such as brain damage, hearing loss, or death if not treated promptly. Transmission occurs through respiratory droplets, and it is more common in settings where people are in close contact. Vaccination is a key preventive measure. Early diagnosis and antibiotic treatment are crucial for improving outcomes.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing Incidence Post-2016**: There's a notable decline in yearly cases after 2016, from 24 in May 2016 to consistently fewer cases in subsequent years.<br/>
- **Lowest Monthly Cases in May 2023**: Only 2 cases were recorded in May 2023, marking the lowest incidence in the dataset.<br/>
- **Fluctuating Fatalities**: Deaths have varied, with occasional spikes in some months, including a high of 4 deaths in January 2015 and 6 in December 2017.<br/>
- **Stabilized Cases in 2024**: As of May 2024, the number of cases is 12, with no reported deaths in the first five months of the year, signaling effective disease control efforts.<br/>

Analyzing the reported data for Meningococcal meningitis in mainland China from May 2014 to May 2024 reveals variable monthly case numbers with seasonal trends. Winters show spikes, such as the 24 cases in December 2017, while summers commonly report fewer cases, like 2 in August 2021. Higher incidence does not consistently result in increased mortality; March 2016 saw 16 cases with 4 fatalities, while December 2019 saw 15 cases and only 1 death. This implies improved medical responses or fluctuating pathogenic virulence could be contributing to these discrepancies. The data likely reflects the complexity of epidemic dynamics driven by various environmental, biological, and healthcare factors in the region.
