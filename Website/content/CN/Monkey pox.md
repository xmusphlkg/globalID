# Monkey pox

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus. First identified in 1958 in laboratory monkeys, the disease primarily occurs in Central and West African rainforests but has seen sporadic cases elsewhere due to international travel and animal trade. Transmission to humans can occur through direct contact with infected animals, human-to-human through respiratory droplets, or contaminated materials. Symptoms typically manifest within 5-21 days, featuring fever, headache, muscle aches, and a characteristic rash. Though similar to smallpox, monkeypox is generally milder, and smallpox vaccination provides some cross-protection.

## Highlights

- **Initial Surge and Decline:**
  The number of Monkeypox cases initially surged in September 2023 with 80 cases and peaked in October with 127 cases. A subsequent decline is observed, with August 2024 reporting 48 cases. <br/>

- **Stable Mortality Rate:**
  Across the observed period, there were zero reported deaths, indicating a stable mortality rate despite fluctuating case numbers. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variability:**
  The data suggests potential seasonal patterns, with lower cases recorded in early 2024 (March and April) before experiencing minor fluctuations in the following months. <br/>

- **Overall Downward Trend:**
  Despite some fluctuations, an overall downward trend in case numbers is evident from late 2023 through August 2024. <br/>

A slight decrease occurred in November (80 cases), with another rise in December (102 cases). From January to August 2024, the data shows a steady decline, with April recording the lowest number of cases at 39. Small fluctuations occurred in May (49 cases) and June (51 cases), but the numbers remained stable.

Despite the variation in case numbers, no deaths were reported during this period, indicating effective disease control and clinical management. This suggests that public health interventions might have helped in reducing the severity of the infections. The data on Monkeypox in the Chinese mainland from September 2023 to August 2024 reveals a consistent pattern of cases being reported without any associated deaths. Over this period, the monthly reported cases fluctuated between a high of 127 cases in October 2023 and a low of 39 cases in both April and July 2024. Despite the fluctuations in case numbers, the notable constant is the absence of reported fatalities, suggesting that either the cases were mild, successfully managed, or less severe strains of the virus may be in circulation.

This lack of reported deaths can be indicative of several factors: effective public health response, access to medical care, and perhaps the virulence of the Monkeypox strain circulating during this time. The data suggests that while the infection is present, its impact in terms of mortality is negligible on the Chinese mainland. This could provide an opportunity for public health authorities to focus more on preventive strategies, case management, and monitoring potential changes in the virus's behavior or transmission dynamics.
