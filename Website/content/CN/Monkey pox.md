# Monkey pox

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Monkeypox is a zoonotic viral disease, first identified in 1958 in monkeys and in humans in 1970 in the Democratic Republic of Congo. It primarily occurs in Central and West Africa. The monkeypox virus, part of the Orthopoxvirus genus, spreads from animals like rodents and primates to humans. Human-to-human transmission happens via respiratory droplets, direct contact with body fluids, or contaminated objects. Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle aches, and a characteristic rash. While related to the smallpox virus, monkeypox is less severe; however, the smallpox vaccine provides some protection. Early detection and isolation are crucial to controlling outbreaks.

## Highlights

- Initial rise and stabilization: Monkeypox cases saw an initial increase, peaking in October 2023 and then experiencing gradual stabilization throughout 2024 with fluctuations between 16 and 70 cases per month. <br/>

- Resurgence: Starting in early 2025, cases began to rise again significantly, peaking at 135 cases in July 2025, indicating a resurgence of the disease. <br/>

- Mortality: Across the data span, there have been zero reported deaths, demonstrating the non-lethal nature of monkeypox in the Chinese mainland context. <br/>

- Current status: As of August 2025, the situation shows a decrease from the July peak, with cases dropping to 81, suggesting possible mitigation efforts or natural decline. <br/>

During the analyzed period, monkeypox cases in the Chinese mainland show a dynamic pattern. Starting at 80 cases in September 2023, the incidence peaked in October 2023 with 127 cases before fluctuating with a declining trend through late 2023 and early 2024. By April 2024, cases reached a low of 39, indicating temporary containment or effective mitigation. Despite slight increases in May and June, 2024 overall shows a decrease in cases, suggesting successful intervention measures or seasonal influences.

Entering 2025, there's a noticeable rise in cases: starting with 40 in January and peaking at 135 in July, followed by a decrease in August with 81 cases. This resurgence might reflect relaxed prevention efforts, increased transmission, or improved detection and reporting due to heightened awareness. Importantly, no deaths were reported throughout the period, highlighting effective patient management, possibly due to less virulent virus strains or successful vaccination and early intervention. 

These trends offer insights for public health strategies in resource allocation and prevention, underscoring the need for preparedness in managing future outbreaks. Continuous monitoring and adaptive response measures remain crucial in understanding and controlling the disease dynamics efficiently, particularly given the fluctuations observed across the months analyzed.
