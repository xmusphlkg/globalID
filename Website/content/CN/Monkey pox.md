# Monkey pox

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus. It was first discovered in 1958 in monkeys kept for research, with the first human case recorded in 1970 in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The disease is endemic to Central and West Africa, with occasional outbreaks elsewhere. Symptoms resemble those of smallpox, including fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes, but are generally milder. Transmission occurs through contact with infected animals, humans, or contaminated materials. Vaccination against smallpox offers some protection against monkeypox.

## Highlights

- **Initial Surge and Decline**: The data shows an initial surge in cases from September to December 2023, peaking in October with 127 cases, followed by a gradual decline throughout 2024. <br/>

- **Consistent Zero Mortality**: Despite the number of cases, there have been no reported deaths, indicating effective management and treatment of the disease. <br/>

- **Stabilization in 2024**: After a significant drop in early 2024, the number of cases stabilized, fluctuating between 16 and 51 cases per month, suggesting control measures are effective. <br/>

- **Recent Increase**: A slight increase in cases is observed in December 2024, with 27 cases, which may require monitoring to prevent a potential resurgence. <br/>

The reported data for Monkeypox cases in mainland China from September 2023 to December 2024 shows a fluctuating trend with an initial increase in cases followed by a gradual decline. In the latter part of 2023, the number of cases peaked in October with 127 reported cases, which was the highest during the observed period. This peak was followed by a decrease in November and December, with cases dropping to 80 and 102, respectively. The trend continued into 2024, with a noticeable decline in the number of cases, reaching a low of 16 in November 2024. This suggests that the outbreak was more severe in late 2023, with a subsequent reduction in cases over the following year.

Throughout the entire period, there were no reported deaths, indicating that while the disease was present, it was not fatal in the reported cases. The consistent absence of deaths could suggest effective management and treatment strategies, or that the cases were generally mild. The decline in cases over time might be attributed to increased public health measures, improved awareness, or natural disease dynamics. The data indicates a successful containment and management of the outbreak, with the number of cases significantly decreasing by the end of 2024.
