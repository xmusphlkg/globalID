# Monkey pox

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus. It is primarily found in Central and West Africa but has spread to other regions. The disease manifests with fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes, and it can lead to a range of medical complications. Human-to-human transmission occurs through close contact with lesions, body fluids, respiratory droplets, and contaminated materials. Although related to smallpox, monkeypox is generally less severe. Vaccines and antiviral treatments used for smallpox may also be effective for monkeypox.

## Highlights

1. **Steady Decline in Cases**: There has been a noticeable decline in monkeypox cases from January 2024 (97 cases) to April 2024 (39 cases), indicating effective control measures. <br/>

2. **Fluctuating Case Numbers**: The number of cases has fluctuated, with spikes in October 2023 (127 cases) and December 2023 (102 cases), suggesting occasional outbreaks despite the overall downward trend. <br/>

3. **Zero Deaths**: No deaths have been reported throughout the period, reflecting potentially effective treatment protocols and low mortality associated with the current outbreak. <br/>

4. **Stabilization in Recent Months**: From May to June 2024, case numbers have stabilized around 50 cases per month, indicating a potential plateau in the outbreak. <br/>

The analysis of Monkeypox cases in the Chinese mainland from September 2023 to June 2024 reveals several notable trends. The number of cases fluctuated significantly over the ten-month period, with a peak of 127 cases reported in October 2023 and a gradual decline thereafter. Following the October peak, the cases decreased in the subsequent months, reaching a low of 39 cases in April 2024. There was a slight increase in May and June 2024, with both months reporting 49 and 51 cases respectively. Notably, there were no reported deaths during this period, indicating a potentially effective healthcare response in managing the disease's severity.

The observed trends suggest a successful containment and management effort post the October 2023 peak. The continuous decline in cases after October could be attributed to enhanced public health measures, increased awareness, and possibly the implementation of vaccination campaigns. The slight rise in May and June 2024 could indicate periodic fluctuations, which might warrant a closer investigation into potential seasonal or environmental factors influencing transmission rates. Overall, the data reflects a controlled outbreak with significant reductions in case numbers over time.
