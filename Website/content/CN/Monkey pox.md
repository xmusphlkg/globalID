# Monkey pox

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Monkeypox is a contagious zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, part of the Orthopoxvirus genus. First identified in laboratory monkeys in 1958, and in humans in 1970 in the Democratic Republic of Congo, it primarily occurs in Central and West Africa. It spreads through direct contact with infected animals, humans, or contaminated materials. Human symptoms mirror smallpox but are milder, including fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes. Recent outbreaks have raised global concerns, emphasizing the importance of monitoring and preventing cross-species transmission. Vaccination and public health measures are key strategies in controlling its spread.

## Highlights

- **Declining Trend in Cases:** From 127 cases in October 2023 to 38 in October 2024, Monkeypox cases in Chinese mainland have shown a consistent decline, highlighting effective control measures. <br/>
- **Zero Mortality Rate:** No deaths were attributed to Monkeypox, suggesting successful case management and the mild nature of recent outbreaks in the region. <br/>
- **Fluctuating Monthly Cases:** Despite an overall decline, months like December 2023 and June 2024 saw slight increases, indicating the need for continued vigilance. <br/>
- **Stable Low-Level Incidence:** Since March 2024, monthly cases have remained below 50, pointing to a controlled situation with ongoing public health efforts sustaining low transmission levels. <br/>

The data on monkeypox cases in mainland China from September 2023 to October 2024 demonstrate a notable trend in disease progression and containment efforts. Initially, the number of cases was relatively high, with 80 cases reported in September 2023 and a peak in October 2023 with 127 cases. This peak could indicate a surge potentially attributed to increased transmission rates or identification and reporting of cases. However, despite this increase in cases during the initial months, the absence of reported deaths suggests effective medical management and potentially less virulent strains or effective early interventions.

As the timeline progresses, a general decline in monthly reported cases is observed, dropping to a low of 38 cases by October 2024. This decline over the months might reflect successful public health interventions, increased public awareness, and behavioral adjustments that mitigated the spread of the virus. The consistency in the absence of deaths throughout the period reinforces the effectiveness of healthcare responses to monkeypox in this region. However, fluctuations such as the slight increase in cases during May and June 2024 highlight the continued need for vigilance in monitoring and controlling the disease spread to prevent potential resurgences. Overall, the data suggests successful management and a decreasing trend in monkeypox cases in mainland China over the observed period.
