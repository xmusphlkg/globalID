# Mumps

Version: December 2025

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral disease caused by the mumps virus, primarily affecting the salivary glands, especially the parotid glands. It is transmitted through respiratory droplets or direct contact with an infected person's saliva. Common symptoms include fever, headache, muscle aches, fatigue, and painful swelling of one or both parotid glands. Mumps mostly affects children but can occur in adults. Complications may include orchitis, meningitis, and, rarely, deafness. Vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective preventive measure. Incidence has decreased in countries with widespread immunization.

## Highlights

- Mumps cases in Chinese mainland have shown a substantial decline since the peak years (2017–2019), with monthly cases dropping from over 30,000 to under 10,000 in late 2025.<br/>
- Deaths from mumps remain exceptionally low throughout the entire period, with only rare occurrences (mainly before 2022), and no deaths reported since June 2021.<br/>
- Transmission now displays a moderate, stable trend with minor seasonal fluctuations, suggesting effective ongoing surveillance and control measures.<br/>
- The current situation as of December 2025 is characterized by low incidence and zero mortality, reflecting successful public health interventions and maintenance of low disease burden.<br/>

From 2015 to 2019, the Chinese mainland experienced marked seasonal fluctuations in mumps cases, with pronounced peaks occurring between April and July each year, followed by gradual declines toward the winter months. Annual case counts increased through this period, reaching a high in 2019, where monthly cases exceeded 36,000 in May and 37,000 in June. Mortality remained exceedingly low throughout the years, with only sporadic deaths reported—a total of nine across the entire dataset, suggesting the clinical burden was largely non-lethal during these years. These patterns reflect both the cyclical nature typical of mumps epidemiology and possibly stable levels of population immunity.

A dramatic decline in reported mumps cases occurred in 2020, coinciding with extensive COVID-19 containment measures, such as school closures, lockdowns, and heightened hygiene practices. Monthly counts dropped by more than half compared to previous years, never exceeding 17,000 cases, and remaining subdued through subsequent years. From 2021 onwards, case numbers stabilized at a lower baseline, suggesting lasting effects of behavioral changes and possible ongoing impacts from pandemic-era interventions. By 2023–2025, monthly incidence settled closer to 6,000–11,000 cases, exhibiting much-reduced seasonality and persistently low mortality, pointing to shifts in transmission dynamics and potential improvements in surveillance, diagnosis, or vaccination efforts.
