# Mumps

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral infection characterized by swelling of the parotid glands, which are one of the salivary glands located near the ears. It is caused by the mumps virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. The disease spreads through respiratory droplets or direct contact with an infected person. Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle aches, and swollen glands. While it is generally mild in children, mumps can cause serious complications in adults, such as orchitis in males and oophoritis in females. Vaccination is the primary method of prevention.

## Highlights

- **Decreasing Trend in Cases**: Mumps cases in China have shown a general decreasing trend since peaking in 2019, with significant declines from 2020 onward due to potential public health interventions and changes in disease reporting. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate**: The mortality rate from mumps remains very low, with sporadic deaths recorded and no deaths reported in recent months. <br/>
- **Seasonal Variations**: Case numbers show seasonal peaks, typically higher in early summer (June) and lower in winter (February), indicating possible seasonal transmission patterns. <br/>
- **Recent Stability**: The latest data from June 2024 indicates a relatively stable situation with 9,939 cases, continuing the trend of lower incidence compared to pre-2020 levels. <br/>

From 2014 to 2024, the reported cases of mumps in mainland China exhibit significant fluctuations, with notable seasonal peaks typically occurring between April and August. The highest number of cases was recorded in June 2019, with 37,913 cases. Conversely, the lowest reported cases were in February 2024, with 3,344 cases. There is a clear pattern of increased cases during warmer months and decreased cases during colder months, suggesting a potential seasonal influence on mumps transmission. 

Despite the variations in the number of reported cases, the number of deaths remains remarkably low, with a total of 11 deaths over the entire period. The highest number of deaths occurred in January 2020, coinciding with a significant outbreak. The data suggests effective management and treatment protocols for mumps, given the low mortality rate despite occasional spikes in case numbers. This low mortality rate is consistent with mumps being a generally mild disease, though severe complications can occur in some cases.
