# Mumps

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral infection caused by the mumps virus, typified by painful swelling of the salivary glands, particularly the parotid glands located near the ears. It primarily affects children but can occur at any age. Mumps spreads through respiratory droplets from coughs or sneezes or direct contact with an infected person. Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle aches, and fatigue, emerging typically 16-18 days after exposure. Mumps can lead to complications such as meningitis, orchitis, and hearing loss. Vaccination is the most effective prevention, typically administered as part of the MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine.

## Highlights

- Mumps incidence in the Chinese mainland has declined since the 2019 peak, dropping from over 37,000 cases in June 2019 to about 6,000 in August 2025. <br/>
- Mortality rates are very low, with sporadic deaths observed up to 2021, indicating effective disease management. <br/>
- A marked reduction in cases from 2020 onwards may reflect enhanced public health measures and vaccination efforts amid the COVID-19 pandemic. <br/>
- The disease demonstrates a seasonal pattern, with higher case counts during late spring and early summer, decreasing by yearâ€™s end. <br/>

The mumps data from Mainland China between August 2015 and August 2025 showcases noticeable fluctuations in infection patterns. Initially, from 2015 to 2019, cases exhibit a seasonal pattern, with peaks typically occurring from May to July, when numbers would rise before receding towards year-end. The highest monthly figure in this span was June 2019, with 37,913 cases. Only a few deaths were recorded during this time, indicating low mortality linked to the mumps virus despite high case counts.

Starting in early 2020, a distinct decrease in reported mumps cases is evident, coinciding with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. The implementation of public health measures, such as lockdowns and social distancing, likely curtailed mumps transmission. Throughout 2020, cases remained significantly lower than in previous years, with June reporting only 11,995 cases. This downward trend persisted in the following years, with 2025 showing the lowest monthly figures in the dataset. The sustained decline in cases may be attributed to continuous public health interventions or changes in population immunity.

Despite these fluctuations, reported mumps-related deaths remained minimal across the decade, underscoring the low lethality of mumps in the region. Overall, these data highlight the interplay between infectious diseases and public health measures, emphasizing the importance of adaptive strategies in response to shifting epidemiological patterns.
