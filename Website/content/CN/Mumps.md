# Mumps

Version: May 2025

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral disease caused by the mumps virus, primarily affecting the salivary glands, particularly the parotid glands. It spreads through respiratory droplets or direct contact with an infected person's saliva. Symptoms typically include swollen, painful salivary glands, fever, headache, muscle aches, and fatigue. While mumps is usually mild in children, it can cause complications such as orchitis, oophoritis, meningitis, or hearing loss, especially in adolescents and adults. Vaccination with the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective way to prevent infection and control outbreaks in the population.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases**: Mumps cases in the Chinese mainland have shown a marked decline since the peak in 2019, with cases in May 2025 being substantially lower than those in May 2019. <br/>

- **Seasonal Trends**: There is a notable seasonal pattern, with higher case numbers in spring and early summer months, although the peak levels have diminished over the past years. <br/>

- **Mortality Rate**: The disease continues to have a low mortality rate, with no deaths reported from 2023 onwards, indicating effective management and care. <br/>

- **Recent Stability**: The number of cases in 2025 remains low and stable, suggesting potential control measures' effectiveness and possibly improved vaccination coverage. <br/>

An analysis of the mumps cases in the Chinese mainland from May 2015 to May 2025 reveals several trends and patterns. The data shows a recurring seasonal pattern where the number of cases tends to increase in early summer and decreases towards the winter months. This trend is consistent over the years, with peaks observed typically in May or June and a notable decline during the coldest months, particularly January and February. The highest recorded number of cases was in June 2019, with 37,913 cases, indicating a particularly high transmission year, possibly due to specific environmental, social, or epidemiological factors at that time.

From 2020 onwards, there is a marked decline in the number of reported cases, likely influenced by the impact of public health measures and behavioral changes during the COVID-19 pandemic, such as increased hygiene practices and reduced social interaction. Mortality due to mumps within the given period remains exceptionally low, with very few deaths—only seven reported over the span of the dataset, indicating a generally non-life-threatening nature of the mumps virus within this population. The data suggests that while the mumps virus impacts a significant number of individuals annually, the resulting fatalities are rare, and the decline in cases since the pandemic could be attributed to enhanced public health practices.
