# Mumps

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral disease caused by the paramyxovirus, primarily affecting the salivary glands near the ears. Symptoms include swollen, painful glands, fever, headache, and muscle aches. It spreads through respiratory droplets and direct contact with an infected person's saliva. Complications, though rare, can include meningitis, encephalitis, and hearing loss. Vaccination, particularly the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine, is the most effective prevention method. Due to widespread vaccination efforts, mumps, once a common childhood disease, has become much less prevalent, although outbreaks still occur, particularly in areas with low vaccination coverage.

## Highlights

- There has been a significant decline in mumps cases from 2019 to 2025, with cases peaking at 37,913 in June 2019 and dropping to 6,652 by March 2025. <br/>

- The mumps mortality rate remains low, with few sporadic deaths recorded throughout the years, suggesting effective management and containment strategies in place. <br/>

- Seasonal trends are evident with peaks in cases typically observed from May to July, aligning with historical patterns, but with decreasing intensity in recent years. <br/>

- Overall, the data indicates improved control over mumps transmission, contributing to reduced public health burden in Chinese mainland by 2025. <br/>

The reported mumps cases in Mainland China from March 2015 to February 2025 show significant seasonal and annual variations. Cases typically peak in warmer months, particularly in May, June, and July, and decrease during colder months. For example, in 2017, cases surged from April to July, with May recording 31,840 cases and June peaking at 33,458. Similarly, in 2019, May and June had high counts (36,669 and 37,913 cases, respectively).

Over the decade, only ten deaths were reported, with January 2020 having the highest monthly death count of four. The onset of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 coincided with a notable decline in mumps cases from March onwards, likely due to public health interventions reducing the transmission of communicable diseases. Subsequent years, 2021 and 2022, showed relatively lower case numbers, possibly due to ongoing hygiene and social distancing measures. However, from 2023 to early 2025, a gradual increase in cases was noted, approaching pre-pandemic levels and indicating a return to typical seasonal trends.

An analysis of the reported mumps cases and deaths in mainland China from March 2015 to February 2025 reveals a relatively low mortality rate despite fluctuating case numbers over the decade. Across this period, a total of nine mumps-related deaths were recorded, indicating a low fatality rate when juxtaposed with the high volume of reported cases, which peaked several times, most notably in June 2019 with 37,913 cases. The sporadic nature of the deaths, with some years seeing none while others reporting occasional fatalities, suggests that while mumps remains a prominent infectious concern, the fatal outcomes are relatively rare.

The data shows a consistent pattern of seasonal fluctuations, with cases often rising during the middle of the year and tapering off towards the end or start. Notable exceptions where deaths were recorded include August 2016, July 2017, February 2018, January and February 2020, and June 2021. The highest annual deaths occurred in 2020, with a temporal alignment to the COVID-19 pandemic, possibly indicating compounding vulnerabilities or reporting irregularities. The steady decrease in the number of mumps cases from early 2020 onwards could be attributed to social distancing measures, heightened healthcare vigilance during the pandemic, and improved vaccination coverage. Overall, the epidemiological outlook suggests that while mumps remains widespread, its lethality is notably low in the given timeframe on the Chinese mainland.
