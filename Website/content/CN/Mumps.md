# Mumps

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral disease caused by the mumps virus, characterized by swollen salivary glands, fever, headache, muscle aches, fatigue, and loss of appetite. The virus spreads through respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing and direct contact with infected surfaces. Complications, though rare, can include orchitis, oophoritis, meningitis, and hearing loss. Vaccination with the MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine is the most effective prevention method. While mumps primarily affects children, outbreaks can occur in unvaccinated populations, highlighting the importance of maintaining high vaccination coverage.

## Highlights

- **Declining Trend**: Mumps cases have shown a general declining trend from 2019 onwards, with a significant drop in 2022 and a gradual stabilization through mid-2024. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variation**: Historically, higher cases are observed in the summer months, peaking often in June and July. However, the trend appears to be less pronounced in recent years. <br/>

- **Minimal Mortality**: Deaths have been rare throughout the period, with sporadic cases in previous years, and no recorded fatalities from 2021 to July 2024. <br/>

- **Stabilization**: While mumps cases continue to fluctuate, the numbers have notably decreased since the peak in 2019, indicating potential disease control and prevention effectiveness. <br/>

The data for mumps in the Chinese mainland from July 2014 to July 2024 shows a clear seasonal pattern, with cases peaking typically between May and June each year before declining. Between 2014 and 2019, there was a gradual increase in cases, reaching a peak of 37,913 in June 2019. However, this trend was interrupted in 2020, coinciding with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, when cases dropped sharply. This decline likely reflects the impact of public health measures like social distancing and mask-wearing, which reduced the transmission of not only COVID-19 but also other communicable diseases such as mumps.

Deaths from mumps were rare throughout the period, with occasional spikes, such as four deaths in January 2020, but overall the disease remained low in lethality. After 2020, mumps cases did not return to pre-pandemic levels, remaining relatively low in 2021 and beyond. This continued reduction could be due to lasting changes in public behavior and healthcare practices post-pandemic. Overall, the data indicates that mumps had a predictable seasonal pattern disrupted by the pandemic, leading to a sustained decline in cases in the following years.
