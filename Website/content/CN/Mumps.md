# Mumps

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral infection, primarily affecting children, characterized by swollen salivary glands, particularly the parotid glands located near the ears. It is caused by the mumps virus, part of the Paramyxoviridae family. Symptoms typically include fever, headache, muscle aches, fatigue, and loss of appetite, followed by the hallmark swelling. Transmission is through respiratory droplets or direct contact with infected saliva. While usually self-limiting, mumps can lead to complications such as meningitis, orchitis, and hearing loss. Vaccination with the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine is the most effective prevention strategy.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases**: Mumps cases in the Chinese mainland have steadily decreased, from 37,913 in June 2019 to 6,692 in August 2024. <br/>
- **Seasonal Pattern**: Cases typically peaked between April and August, but this seasonality has become less prominent recently. <br/>
- **Low Mortality**: The mortality rate remains extremely low, with no deaths since October 2020. <br/>
- **Recent Stability**: Since 2023, mumps cases have stabilized, indicating better control and possibly improved vaccination efforts. <br/>
