# Mumps

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral infection primarily affecting the salivary glands, particularly the parotid glands located near the ears. It is caused by the mumps virus, a member of the Paramyxoviridae family. The disease is characterized by symptoms such as fever, headache, muscle aches, fatigue, and swelling of the salivary glands, leading to puffy cheeks and a tender, swollen jaw. Mumps is transmitted through respiratory droplets or direct contact with an infected person. Vaccination with the MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine is the most effective way to prevent mumps and its complications.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases:** Mumps cases in the Chinese mainland have shown a significant decline from a peak in 2019, with 38,000 cases in June 2019 to just over 3,800 cases in February 2025. <br/>
- **Seasonal Trends:** Historically, mumps cases tend to peak in the spring and early summer months, with noticeable declines during the winter months. <br/>
- **Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remains extremely low, with only a few deaths reported over the years, indicating effective management and treatment of the disease. <br/>
- **Recent Stability:** Since 2023, the number of cases has stabilized at lower levels, suggesting improved control measures and possibly increased vaccination coverage. <br/>

The data on mumps cases in mainland China from 2015 to 2025 reveals several trends and patterns. Initially, there is a noticeable seasonal fluctuation in the number of cases, with peaks typically occurring in the late spring and early summer months, particularly from April to June. This pattern is consistent with the known epidemiology of mumps, which often sees increased transmission in school settings and during times of increased social interaction. The highest number of cases was recorded in June 2019, with 37,913 cases, indicating a significant outbreak during that period. 

From 2020 onwards, there is a marked decline in the number of reported cases, which could be attributed to the public health measures implemented during the COVID-19 pandemic, such as social distancing and school closures, which inadvertently reduced the transmission of other infectious diseases like mumps. The data also shows a few instances of deaths, with a total of eight deaths reported over the entire period, indicating that while mumps can lead to severe outcomes, the mortality rate remains low. The overall trend from 2021 to 2025 shows a gradual decrease in cases, suggesting improved control measures or changes in population immunity.
