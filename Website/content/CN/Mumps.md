# Mumps

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Mumps is a contagious viral infection caused by the mumps virus, primarily affecting the salivary glands. It presents with symptoms such as swelling of the parotid glands, fever, headache, and muscle aches. Transmission occurs through respiratory droplets or direct contact with infected saliva. Although preventable through vaccination, mumps can lead to complications like meningitis, encephalitis, and orchitis. The MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine is the primary preventive measure. Mumps is more common in children but can occur at any age. Annual outbreaks highlight the need for sustained immunization coverage.

## Highlights

- **Significant Reduction**: Mumps cases have dramatically decreased since peaking in 2019. As of May 2024, there are 10,197 cases, a significant drop from previous highs. <br/>
- **Low Mortality**: Deaths due to mumps have been low, with a peak of four deaths in January 2020. No deaths have been reported in recent months. <br/>
- **Seasonal Trends**: Cases tend to rise in summer and fall in winter. Recent peaks are lower, indicating better control. <br/>
- **Overall Decline**: There is a clear downward trend in both mumps cases and deaths, showing improved disease management in the Chinese mainland. <br/>

An analysis of Mumps data in Chinese mainland from May 2014 to May 2024 reveals notable seasonal and yearly trends. Peaks typically occur from late spring to early summer (May to July), with lower incidence rates in winter months, especially January and February. The highest case count was in June 2019, with 37,913 cases, surpassing other high-incidence periods like May 2019 and June 2017. Yearly February data consistently show a drop in cases, likely due to school holidays and colder weather reducing interactions.

Mortality is minimal, with only sporadic deaths recorded. The highest mortality was in January 2020, with four deaths. The case fatality rate remains very low, suggesting effective mitigation and treatment measures. A significant drop in cases from 2020 to 2021 corresponds with the COVID-19 pandemic's public health interventions, such as lockdowns and increased hygiene, which likely reduced mumps transmission. By 2022 and 2023, cases began to rebound but remained lower than pre-pandemic peaks.

Overall, the data highlight the seasonal nature of mumps and the impact of public health measures on disease transmission. While mumps remains highly infectious, the low fatality rate indicates effective disease management and possibly broad vaccination coverage.
