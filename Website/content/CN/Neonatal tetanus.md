# Neonatal tetanus

Version: April 2025

## Introduction

Neonatal tetanus is a severe, often fatal disease in newborns caused by the bacterium *Clostridium tetani*, typically introduced through non-sterile delivery practices or contaminated instruments used to cut the umbilical cord. It is characterized by rigidity, muscle spasms, and difficulty feeding, usually appearing within the first two weeks of life. The disease predominantly affects infants born in unsanitary conditions and to mothers lacking immunity. Prevention relies heavily on clean delivery services and maternal immunization with tetanus toxoid. Despite being preventable, neonatal tetanus remains a public health challenge in some low-resource settings.

## Highlights

- There has been a dramatic decline in neonatal tetanus cases in mainland China, from monthly highs of over 30 in 2015 to just 1 reported case in April 2025. <br/>
- Mortality from neonatal tetanus has also decreased markedly, with no deaths recorded since August 2022, underscoring improved early detection and care. <br/>
- Since 2021, monthly case counts have mostly remained below 3, indicating sustained control and possible elimination in several regions. <br/>
- The disease trend reflects the effectiveness of immunization programs, clean delivery practices, and strengthened maternal and neonatal health services. <br/>

The data on neonatal tetanus in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 indicates a significant decline in both the number of cases and fatalities over time. In 2015, the monthly numbers of reported cases ranged from 17 to 33, with a total of 245 reported cases and 12 deaths throughout the year. This contrasts sharply with 2024 and the early months of 2025, where cases have significantly reduced to single digits, with some months reporting zero cases and deaths consistently remaining at zero, reflecting successful control measures. The peak of cases and deaths was noted in early 2015, with cases peaking around mid-year and then showing a gradual decline.

The decrease in cases and fatalities over the years aligns with intensified public health efforts, such as increased maternal immunization and improved healthcare practices in neonatal care across the country. From 2018 onwards, the monthly fluctuations in case numbers became less pronounced, with long stretches of months reporting minimal to no cases, and deaths occurring sporadically. The data suggest that ongoing public health strategies have effectively minimized the incidence of neonatal tetanus, reflecting better prenatal care and community health education. However, a continuation of surveillance and prevention measures is essential to maintain these gains, ensuring complete eradication and preventing potential resurgence.

From April 2015 through April 2025, the reported deaths from neonatal tetanus in mainland China show a marked decline both in frequency and absolute numbers. In the early part of the dataset, particularly from 2015 to 2016, neonatal tetanus was associated with relatively higher mortality—21 deaths in those two years alone—with a peak in October 2015 (3 deaths). Starting in 2017, both the number of cases and deaths began to decrease significantly, coinciding with wider implementation of preventive programs like maternal immunization and sterile birth practices. Sporadic deaths still occurred in individual months, such as November 2017 (2 deaths), but overall, the frequency was diminishing.

From 2020 onward, the mortality rate continued to fall, with several consecutive months—sometimes years—reporting zero fatalities. Only isolated incidents appear beyond 2020, such as a single death in March 2021 and August 2022, but no deaths were recorded at all between September 2022 and April 2025. This pattern points to improved control and likely near-elimination of neonatal tetanus deaths across the country, aligning with global goals for disease reduction. The sustained drop in fatalities alongside reduced case numbers emphasizes the long-term impact of continued public health intervention and surveillance.
