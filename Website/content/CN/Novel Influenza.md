# Novel Influenza

Version: September 2025

## Introduction

Novel Influenza refers to new or re-emergent strains of the influenza virus that cause significant health concerns. Unlike seasonal flu viruses, these strains arise from genetic shifts, often originating from animal reservoirs like birds or pigs before adapting to humans. Such viruses can trigger pandemics due to limited population immunity and efficient human-to-human transmission. Novel Influenza is closely monitored because of its potential to cause widespread illness and mortality. Surveillance, vaccination, and preparedness are crucial in managing outbreaks, aiming to mitigate impacts while promoting health and safety globally.

## Highlights

- The number of Novel Influenza cases in Chinese mainland for September 2025 is notably low, with only 2 reported cases, suggesting effective control measures or low transmission at this time. <br/>

- There have been no deaths reported in September 2025 related to Novel Influenza, indicating perhaps a milder strain or effective health interventions for those infected. <br/>

- Comparing this data to previous months or years would be crucial to understand trends, but based solely on this snapshot, the epidemiological situation appears stable with minimal spread and impact. <br/>

- Continuous surveillance and public health efforts are essential to prevent future outbreaks, especially as flu season approaches. <br/>
