# Other hepatitis

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Other hepatitis refers to liver inflammation caused by factors other than the common hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, and E). This includes autoimmune hepatitis, where the body's immune system attacks liver cells; alcoholic hepatitis, resulting from excessive alcohol consumption; and drug-induced hepatitis, caused by certain medications or toxins. Additionally, metabolic disorders, such as non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), can lead to hepatitis-like symptoms. Understanding these forms is crucial for effective diagnosis and treatment, as they may require different management strategies compared to viral hepatitis.

## Highlights

- **Declining Trend in Cases**: From 2015 to 2025, hepatitis cases decreased significantly, from over 2,500 in early 2015 to 749 in January 2025, indicating effective public health measures. <br/>

- **Low Mortality Rate**: Deaths remain consistently low, often zero or one per month, suggesting the disease is not highly lethal or is well-managed. <br/>

- **Recent Increase in Cases**: A slight rise in cases in January 2025 compared to late 2024 may require further investigation to prevent resurgence. <br/>

- **Stable Seasonal Patterns**: The data shows no strong seasonal variations, implying other factors like healthcare access might influence case numbers. <br/>

The data on Other hepatitis cases in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 reveals significant fluctuations in reported cases and deaths over the years. Initially, in 2015, the number of cases was notably high, peaking at 2,715 in March. However, the mortality rate remained low, with only a few deaths reported throughout the year. As the years progressed, there was a general decline in the number of cases, particularly evident from 2018 onwards, where the monthly cases often fell below 1,500. This trend suggests a potential improvement in public health measures or changes in the epidemiology of hepatitis in the region.

In terms of mortality, the data indicates that deaths remained relatively low compared to the number of cases, with sporadic increases in certain months. For instance, in 2018, there were two deaths reported in June, which was an anomaly in a year characterized by lower case counts. The years 2020 to 2025 show a continued decline in both cases and deaths, with the highest number of cases recorded in January 2025 at 749. This ongoing trend may reflect effective interventions, increased awareness, and better healthcare access, contributing to the overall management of hepatitis in the Chinese mainland.
