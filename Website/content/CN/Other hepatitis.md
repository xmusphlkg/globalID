# Other hepatitis

Version: July 2025

## Introduction

"Other hepatitis" refers to less common types of viral hepatitis that are not caused by the typical hepatitis A, B, or C viruses. This category includes hepatitis D and E, among others. Hepatitis D requires the presence of hepatitis B to replicate and can exacerbate the severity of hepatitis B infections. Hepatitis E is transmitted through contaminated water and is prevalent in regions with poor sanitation. Both types can lead to liver inflammation, causing symptoms like jaundice, fatigue, and abdominal pain. Understanding these variants is crucial for effective global health management and prevention strategies.

## Highlights

- **Sharp Decline in Cases**: From July 2015 to July 2025, Other hepatitis cases dropped from over 2,500 to under 600 per month, reflecting successful disease control efforts. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate**: Deaths have consistently remained low, often at zero, indicating effective treatment and management strategies. <br/>
- **Stabilization in Recent Years**: Since mid-2023, cases have stabilized at around 500 to 700 per month, suggesting effective and consistent public health interventions. <br/>
- **Continued Vigilance Required**: Ongoing surveillance and prevention measures are essential to maintain and improve these positive trends, despite the significant reduction in cases and low mortality rates. <br/>
