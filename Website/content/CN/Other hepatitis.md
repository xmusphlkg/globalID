# Other hepatitis

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Other hepatitis refers to liver inflammation not caused by the well-known hepatitis viruses A, B, C, D, or E. It can result from various factors such as autoimmunity (autoimmune hepatitis), genetic disorders (e.g., Wilsonâ€™s disease), metabolic syndromes (e.g., non-alcoholic fatty liver disease), toxins, alcohol abuse, or certain medications. This condition leads to liver cell damage, fibrosis, and potentially cirrhosis if untreated. Symptoms often include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and abnormal liver function tests. Diagnosis typically involves a combination of clinical evaluation, blood tests, imaging, and sometimes liver biopsy.

## Highlights

1. **Notable Decline in Case Numbers:** There has been a significant reduction in reported hepatitis cases from 2014 to 2024, from 2,924 cases in May 2014 to 604 cases in May 2024. <br/>
2. **Persistent Mortality Stability:** Deaths due to hepatitis have remained consistently low with sporadic occurrences over the years, peaking at 2 deaths in certain months but often being 0. <br/>
3. **Recent Stability in Cases:** In the last two years, case numbers have stabilized around 600-700 cases per month, indicating a plateau in new infections. <br/>
4. **Potential Seasonal Variation:** A slight increase in cases during the summer months (May-August) across various years suggests some seasonal pattern. <br/>

Analysis of the cases of "Other hepatitis" in the Chinese mainland from 2014 to May 2024 reveals a declining trend over this decade. In the initial period (2014-2016), the number of cases per month fluctuated around 2,000 to 3,000, peaking in the summer months, particularly between May to September. Deaths during this period were sporadic, ranging from 0 to 2 deaths per month. Notably, 2014 experienced the highest number of cases, with a maximum monthly case count of 3,041 in August and a total of six deaths recorded throughout the year.

From 2017 onward, the data shows a gradual but consistent decline in the number of cases. By 2020, around the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, there is a sharp drop in cases, with numbers consistently below 1,000 per month post-February 2020. This period also saw reduced deaths, typically zero to one per month. The most recent years, 2021 to 2024, continued this trend, maintaining lower case counts rarely exceeding 900 per month. Death incidence in these latter years remained infrequent, further underscoring the overall decline. This data indicates not only significant fluctuations influenced potentially by seasonal and environmental factors but also an effective ongoing public health response that has managed to lower both the incidence and the mortality of "Other hepatitis" across the Chinese mainland.
