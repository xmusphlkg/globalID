# Other hepatitis

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Other hepatitis refers to types of liver inflammation not caused by the primary hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, E). These can be due to autoimmune conditions, metabolic disorders, medications, toxins, or other infections like cytomegalovirus and Epstein-Barr virus. Autoimmune hepatitis, for example, occurs when the body's immune system attacks liver cells. Drug-induced hepatitis can result from various medications or toxins, leading to liver damage. Metabolic conditions like Wilsonâ€™s disease, which involves copper accumulation, also cause liver inflammation. Proper diagnosis and treatment depend on identifying the underlying cause.

## Highlights

- **Steady Decline in Cases:** "Other hepatitis" cases in mainland China have decreased from over 3,000 per month in 2014 to around 500-700 per month by mid-2024. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rates:** Deaths remain low, typically 0-2 per month, with no significant increase over the years. <br/>
- **Recent Trends:** Cases have stabilized, with a slight decline in the first half of 2024 compared to previous years. <br/>
- **Effective Control:** The declining trend in cases suggests effective epidemiological control measures. <br/>
