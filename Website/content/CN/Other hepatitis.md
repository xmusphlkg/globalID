# Other hepatitis

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Other hepatitis refers to liver inflammation caused by factors other than the well-known hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, and E). This category includes autoimmune hepatitis, where the immune system attacks liver cells, and alcoholic hepatitis, resulting from excessive alcohol consumption. Non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) is another form, linked to obesity and metabolic syndrome. Drug-induced hepatitis can occur due to adverse reactions to medications. Symptoms often include jaundice, fatigue, and abdominal pain. Diagnosis typically involves blood tests, imaging, and sometimes liver biopsy. Treatment varies based on the underlying cause, focusing on managing symptoms and preventing further liver damage.

However, from 2016 onwards, there is a noticeable decline, with cases dropping below 2,000 by early 2017 and continuing to decrease steadily. By 2020, the monthly cases had fallen to below 1,000, and this trend persisted through to 2024, with some months reporting fewer than 500 cases.

The number of deaths associated with "Other hepatitis" remains relatively low throughout the period, with most months reporting zero deaths. There are sporadic instances of one or two deaths in certain months, but no significant increase in mortality is observed.
