# Other hepatitis

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Other hepatitis refers to liver inflammation caused by infections that are not related to the well-known hepatitis viruses A, B, C, D, and E. This category can include infection with other viruses such as Epstein-Barr virus, cytomegalovirus, or yellow fever virus. It can also be a consequence of non-viral factors including alcohol, medications, toxins, or autoimmune diseases. Symptoms often resemble those of viral hepatitis and may include jaundice, fatigue, and abdominal pain. Diagnosis usually involves blood tests and liver function tests. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms and addressing the underlying cause to prevent liver damage.

## Highlights

- The incidence of Other hepatitis in the Chinese mainland has generally declined from 2715 cases in March 2015 to 745 cases in March 2025, showing a significant reduction in cases over a ten-year span. <br/>
- Mortality rates remain low, with sporadic deaths reported monthly, totaling approximately 1-2 deaths per month over the given period. <br/>
- A notable decline in case numbers occurred in 2020, likely influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic measures, with cases reaching a low of 404 in February 2020. <br/>
- Despite the reduction in cases, recent months in 2025 have shown a slight uptick, requiring ongoing monitoring to ensure continued disease control. <br/>

The reported data on Other hepatitis cases in the Chinese mainland from March 2015 through March 2025 illustrates several noticeable trends and patterns. Initially, the number of cases fluctuated but generally trended downward from a high of 2715 cases in March 2015 to below 700 cases per month consistently by 2021. This overall decline is interspersed with minor monthly increases, reflecting a non-linear reduction pattern. Furthermore, following 2020, there are persistent reductions with cases ranging from 404 to 679 per month, indicating an apparent impact of external public health measures or interventions post-2020.

In terms of mortality, the recorded deaths are notably sporadic and relatively low considering the number of cases. After an initial few months of 2015 with no deaths, sporadic instances of deaths began to appear, averaging about 1-2 deaths in some months and frequently none in others. This data signifies a relatively low mortality rate for Other hepatitis during the specified period. The consistent low death rate despite variations in case numbers suggests effectiveness in healthcare management of the disease, possibly indicating improvements in clinical treatment or healthcare infrastructure effectiveness over time. This analysis of case count reduction and low consistent mortality rate highlights substantial progress in addressing Other hepatitis in China.
