# Pertussis

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Pertussis, also known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium *Bordetella pertussis*. It spreads through respiratory droplets and is characterized by severe coughing fits followed by a high-pitched "whoop" sound. Pertussis can affect individuals of all ages but is particularly dangerous for infants. Symptoms typically start with cold-like signs, progressing to intense coughing spells that can last for weeks. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent pertussis, with the DTaP vaccine recommended for children and the Tdap booster for adolescents and adults.

## Highlights

1. **Rising Case Numbers:** Pertussis cases have dramatically increased from 315 in June 2014 to 85,817 in June 2024, showing a significant upward trend in incidence. <br/>
2. **Recent Surge:** There has been an exponential surge in cases starting from early 2024, peaking at 97,669 in May 2024. <br/>
3. **Mortality Trends:** Although deaths remain relatively low compared to the number of cases, there was a notable increase in fatalities in early 2024, with a peak of 8 deaths in February 2024. <br/>
4. **Seasonal Variations:** There are noticeable seasonal fluctuations in pertussis cases, with peaks typically occurring during summer months. <br/>

From mid-2014 to mid-2024, pertussis cases in mainland China exhibited significant fluctuations, with notable increases in certain periods. Initial data from mid-2014 to late 2016 shows a relatively stable pattern with modest increases. For instance, cases rose from 315 in June 2014 to a peak of 999 in August 2015. However, this period also witnessed several months with fewer than 500 cases, indicating variability without a consistent upward or downward trend. Deaths during this time were sporadic, with one or none reported in most months.

A marked increase in pertussis cases began in 2017, with a steady rise each year until 2019, when numbers peaked at 4,388 cases in August 2019. This upward trend was briefly interrupted in 2020, likely due to public health measures related to the COVID-19 pandemic, as cases dropped significantly. However, starting from 2021, the number of cases surged dramatically, reaching unprecedented levels in 2024, with April 2024 recording an extraordinarily high number of 91,272 cases. This recent surge also saw an increase in deaths, peaking in February 2024 with eight reported fatalities. This data underscores the cyclic nature of pertussis outbreaks and highlights recent significant epidemiological shifts that could necessitate further public health interventions.

However, as the years progressed, especially post-2017, the case numbers surged substantially, reaching a zenith of 91,272 cases in April 2024. Despite these high numbers, the death toll remained low compared to the case count, with the highest number of deaths reported in February 2024 (8 deaths).
