# Pertussis

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Pertussis, commonly known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. It primarily affects infants and young children, leading to severe coughing fits that can result in difficulty breathing. The disease is characterized by a distinctive "whoop" sound during inhalation following a coughing episode. Vaccination is the most effective prevention method, with the DTaP vaccine recommended for children. Despite vaccination efforts, outbreaks still occur, highlighting the importance of maintaining high immunization rates and awareness of the disease's symptoms for early diagnosis and treatment.

## Highlights

- **Significant Increase in Cases**: Pertussis cases in the Chinese mainland surged dramatically from 2023 to January 2025, peaking at 91,272 in April 2024, indicating a concerning upward trend in incidence. <br/>

- **Mortality Rates**: Although the overall mortality rate remains low, there were 5 deaths reported in January 2024, highlighting the potential severity of outbreaks. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variability**: The data shows seasonal peaks, particularly in the summer months, suggesting a possible correlation with environmental factors or vaccination coverage. <br/>

- **Current Situation**: As of January 2025, there were 5,611 reported cases, with 1 death, indicating a continued high burden of disease despite a slight decrease from the previous year's peak. <br/>

The data on Pertussis cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 shows several trends. Initially, from 2015 to 2017, there was a gradual increase in cases, peaking in the summer months, typical for respiratory infections. In 2018, cases rose significantly, with August reaching over 4,000 cases. This upward trend continued into 2019, with August again showing the highest number of cases at 4,388. The data suggests a seasonal pattern, with higher incidence rates during warmer months, possibly due to increased social interactions and environmental factors conducive to disease spread.

From 2020, there was a decline in cases, likely influenced by COVID-19 public health measures like social distancing and mask-wearing, which also reduce the transmission of other respiratory diseases. However, starting in 2021, cases began to rise again, with a significant surge in 2022 and 2023. In 2024, an unprecedented spike occurred, with cases reaching over 90,000 in April. This dramatic increase could be due to changes in reporting practices, vaccine coverage, or more transmissible strains. The data also shows a slight increase in deaths, particularly in 2024, reflecting higher case numbers or changes in disease severity. Overall, the data highlights the need for continuous monitoring and vaccination efforts to control Pertussis outbreaks.

However, starting in 2018, there were sporadic deaths reported, with a notable increase in both cases and fatalities. As we move into 2024, the data indicates a dramatic rise in both cases and deaths, particularly in the latter half of the year. The increase in deaths, particularly in the context of rising case numbers, raises questions about the adequacy of vaccination coverage, healthcare access, and the overall response to Pertussis outbreaks.
