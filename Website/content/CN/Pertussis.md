# Pertussis

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Pertussis, commonly known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. It primarily affects infants and young children but can infect individuals of all ages. The disease is characterized by severe coughing fits followed by a "whooping" sound during inhalation. Pertussis is spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. Complications can include pneumonia, seizures, and even death, particularly in unvaccinated infants. Vaccination is the most effective prevention strategy, with the DTaP and Tdap vaccines providing protection for children and adults respectively.

## Highlights

- **Rapid Increase in Cases:** Pertussis cases in Chinese mainland have shown a dramatic increase, from 4,793 in August 2023 to 91,272 in April 2024, indicating a significant outbreak. <br/>

- **Fluctuating Mortality:** Although deaths have remained relatively low compared to cases, they increased to a peak of 8 in February 2024, demonstrating a potential strain on healthcare resources. <br/>

- **Recent Decline in Cases:** From a high in April 2024, cases have declined steadily to 43,216 in August 2024, suggesting measures might be stabilizing the situation. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations:** The data reveals seasonal trends, with higher incidence in warmer months, which can assist in future resource allocation and prevention strategies. <br/>

The data on Pertussis cases in mainland China from August 2014 to August 2024 shows notable trends in disease incidence. Between 2014 and 2016, there was a relatively low and stable number of cases, fluctuating between 275 to 999 per month. However, starting in 2017, a marked increase in cases can be observed, peaking in August 2018 with 4,134 cases. This upward trend persisted, with sporadic fluctuations, reaching a peak of 4,388 cases in August 2019. After a temporary decline in 2020, likely influenced by COVID-19-related public health interventions, cases began to rise again, showing a significant surge from mid-2021 onward, peaking at 17,105 cases in February 2024 and then rising sharply to 91,272 cases in April 2024.

Deaths associated with Pertussis remained relatively low throughout the observed period, with only occasional fatalities reported. The mortality rate showed some spikes, particularly in early 2024, when higher death counts (5â€“8 deaths per month) were recorded alongside the surging case numbers. From 2014 to 2017, there was generally one death or none reported each year, despite moderate increases in case numbers during this period, particularly in 2017 when cases reached over 1,600 in August without any reported deaths. Deaths were sporadic and infrequent, with only a few months each year reporting fatalities.

However, starting from 2023, there was a noticeable rise in both the number of cases and deaths. By the end of 2023 and into early 2024, significant surges in cases were recorded, particularly in January and February of 2024, where deaths also spiked to eight in February. This trend continued, with the highest number of reported deaths occurring in April 2024, corresponding with a sharp increase in cases that month. The data suggests that the rise in case numbers may have contributed to the elevated death toll during these periods, particularly in 2024, although the mortality rate remains relatively low compared to the total number of cases.
