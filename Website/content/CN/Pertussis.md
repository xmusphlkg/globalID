# Pertussis

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Pertussis, commonly known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. It is characterized by severe coughing fits that can last for weeks, often ending with a high-pitched "whoop" sound during the subsequent breath. Pertussis spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It poses a significant risk to infants and young children, who may experience complications such as pneumonia, seizures, or even death. Vaccination is the most effective prevention method, with booster shots recommended for adolescents and adults to maintain immunity.

## Highlights



- **Rising Trend in Cases**: Pertussis cases in mainland China have increased significantly from 2015 to 2025, peaking in early 2024 with over 91,000 cases in April. <br/>

- **Fluctuating Mortality**: Deaths remained low compared to cases, but there was a noticeable increase in early 2024, peaking at 8 deaths in February. <br/>

- **Recent Decline**: After the 2024 peak, both cases and deaths have declined, with February 2025 showing 4,298 cases and no deaths, indicating potential stabilization. <br/>

- **Seasonal Patterns**: The data suggests a seasonal pattern, with higher cases in summer months, aligning with global pertussis trends. <br/>

The data on pertussis cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals several notable trends. Initially, from 2015 to 2017, there was a gradual increase in reported cases, peaking in the summer months, which is typical for pertussis due to its seasonal nature. The number of cases rose significantly in 2018, with a marked increase in August, suggesting a possible outbreak or increased transmission during that period. This trend continued into 2019, with the highest number of cases reported in August, indicating a sustained high transmission rate.

In 2020, there was a significant decline in reported cases, likely influenced by public health measures related to the COVID-19 pandemic, such as social distancing and mask-wearing, which also reduce the spread of other respiratory infections. However, from 2021 onwards, the number of cases began to rise again, with a sharp increase observed in 2022 and 2023. The data for 2024 shows an unprecedented surge in cases, particularly in the first half of the year, which could be attributed to changes in public health policies, vaccine coverage, or pathogen evolution. The increase in deaths, although relatively low compared to the number of cases, suggests a need for enhanced surveillance and vaccination efforts to control the spread of pertussis.
