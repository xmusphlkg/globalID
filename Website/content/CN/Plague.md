# Plague

Version: April 2025

## Introduction

Plague is a severe infectious disease caused by the bacterium *Yersinia pestis*. It primarily affects rodents and is transmitted to humans through the bites of infected fleas. There are three main forms: bubonic, septicemic, and pneumonic, each with distinct symptoms and transmission routes. Historically, plague has caused major pandemics, including the Black Death in the 14th century, which killed millions. Today, it remains a rare but potentially deadly disease, treatable with antibiotics. Effective public health measures, surveillance, and rapid treatment help control outbreaks and prevent widespread transmission.

## Highlights

- Plague incidence in mainland China has remained sporadic and rare, with most months from 2015 to April 2025 reporting zero cases and zero deaths. <br/>
- Notable peaks occurred in November 2019 (4 cases), August 2020 (2 cases, 2 deaths), and August 2023 (4 cases, 1 death), suggesting potential seasonal or regional clusters. <br/>
- Mortality remains low, with only 6 deaths across the 10-year period, indicating effective case management and healthcare response. <br/>
- The absence of reported cases from September 2024 to April 2025 indicates no current outbreak, signaling stable disease control as of April 2025. <br/>

Plague cases in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 demonstrate low transmission with sporadic occurrences, consistent with the plague's enzootic presence in certain areas. The period from 2015 to mid-2016 saw no cases until a single, non-fatal case was reported in June 2016. This is followed by another isolated case in December 2017, resulting in one death, and a slight uptick in late 2019, with five cases and one death in September. Subsequent containment efforts appeared effective, as no further cases were reported for several months.

From 2020 to 2024, outbreaks remained sporadic and limited, with minor peaks typically occurring in mid-to-late summer. Notably, 2020 saw three cases, including two deaths, across July to September, suggesting potential seasonal interactions with plague reservoirs. This pattern continued with one non-fatal case in August 2021, another single case in July 2022, and a fatal case in September 2022. In August 2023, the number of cases rose briefly to four, with one resulting in death, indicating occasional localized outbreaks.

Overall, fatalities are uncommon, with seven deaths across 19 cases from 2017 to 2024, underscoring the potential severity of untreated or late-diagnosed infections. The year 2024 again saw two cases with one fatality in August, maintaining the seasonal trend of cases appearing in summer months.
