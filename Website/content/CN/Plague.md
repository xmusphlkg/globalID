# Plague

## Introduction

Plague is a severe infectious disease caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis. Primarily affecting wild rodents, it spreads to humans via flea bites, direct contact with infected animals, or inhalation of respiratory droplets. Historically infamous for causing pandemics like the Black Death in the 14th century, plague manifests in three forms: bubonic, septicemic, and pneumonic, distinguished by symptoms such as swollen lymph nodes, fever, and respiratory issues. Despite its past impact, modern antibiotics effectively treat plague, reducing mortality rates. However, it remains a concern in some regions due to its potential for outbreaks.

## Highlights

- Plague cases on the Chinese mainland have been sporadic, with notable occurrences in August 2020, August 2023, and August 2024, indicating a seasonal pattern in late summer. <br/>
- Deaths have been relatively low compared to cases, suggesting improvements in disease management or detection, though occasional fatalities highlight ongoing challenges in completely controlling the disease. <br/>
- A significant surge in cases was observed in November 2019, contributing to heightened awareness and possibly policy changes in plague management. <br/>
- Recent data from 2025 shows no cases or deaths, suggesting a stable and controlled situation; however, continued vigilance is necessary due to past sporadic outbreaks. <br/>

The data on plague cases in mainland China from June 2015 to June 2025 shows intermittent occurrences, highlighting isolated outbreaks. There are long periods without cases, such as from June 2015 through May 2016. In June 2016, a single non-fatal case was reported, indicating sporadic infection. The data reveals a fatal case in December 2017 and another in September 2019. Such sporadic activity suggests occasional re-emergence of the plague, possibly driven by temporary environmental or biological factors.

In November 2019, a noticeable spike occurred with four cases, though no deaths were recorded, suggesting effective medical response. Sporadic cases occurred throughout 2020, with fatalities in August, underlining occasional outbreaks. The period from 2021 to 2024 is marked by sparse incidents, indicating that interventions have been effective overall, preventing consistent escalation. The year 2023 saw a notable increase in August with four cases and one death, pointing to possible seasonal or ecological factors influencing these occurrences.

These trends emphasize the importance of maintaining vigilant monitoring and rapid containment strategies. The sporadic nature of plague cases and occasional spikes suggest localized sources, possibly involving wildlife-human interactions during certain times of the year. Continued responsive healthcare measures are crucial to minimizing fatalities and containing outbreaks swiftly, preventing widespread transmission despite the periodic rise in cases.
