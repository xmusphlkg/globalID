# Plague

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Plague is a severe infectious disease caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis. Historically notorious for causing pandemics, such as the Black Death in the 14th century, it primarily infects rodents and is transmitted to humans through flea bites or contact with contaminated fluids or tissues. Plague manifests in three forms: bubonic, septicemic, and pneumonic. Bubonic plague, the most common, is characterized by swollen lymph nodes, fever, and malaise. While modern antibiotics are effective treatments, early diagnosis is crucial. The disease remains endemic in parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, necessitating ongoing public health vigilance.

## Highlights

- Plague cases in mainland China have been sporadic, with an overall low incidence, as evidenced by periods of zero cases interspersed with occasional outbreaks. <br/>
- The most notable recent outbreaks occurred in November 2019, August 2023, and August 2024, with the highest number of cases reported in August 2023. <br/>
- There is an observed mortality among cases, with deaths reported sporadically alongside some of the outbreaks, particularly in 2020, 2022, 2023, and 2024. <br/>
- The recent trend shows a cluster of cases during late summer, notably in August, indicating a potential seasonal pattern or environmental exposure during this time. <br/>

The data from 2014 to 2024 on Plague in the Chinese mainland shows sporadic cases with varying fatalities. In 2014, two cases were reported in October, both resulting in death. There were no further cases until June 2016, when one non-fatal case occurred. The pattern remained quiet until December 2017, with one fatal case reported. November 2019 saw a notable rise, with four cases but no deaths, followed by another increase in August 2020, with two of the three cases being fatal. These instances mark significant moments of higher incidence within the dataset.

After 2020, cases remained minimal and infrequent. There were isolated occurrences such as in August 2021, and July and September 2022, generally resulting in non-fatal outcomes, except a fatality in September 2022. A peak came in August 2023 with four cases and one reported death, indicating a potential resurgence risk. In 2024, August saw two new cases, one of which was fatal, while other months reported none, reflecting a return to lower incidence rates. 

Overall, the decade-long data reveals the Plague's intermittent presence on the Chinese mainland, with specific years showing spikes in cases and deaths. Recognizing these trends is crucial for guiding public health responses and preparedness for future outbreaks.
