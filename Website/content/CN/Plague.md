# Plague

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Plague is an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis. It primarily affects rodents, but can be transmitted to humans through the bite of infected fleas or contact with contaminated animals or materials. There are three main forms: bubonic, septicemic, and pneumonic, each affecting the body differently. Bubonic plague, the most common, is characterized by swollen lymph nodes, fever, and weakness. Historically known for causing pandemics such as the Black Death in the 14th century, plague is now rare and treatable with antibiotics. However, it remains a concern in some regions with favorable environments for rodent hosts.

## Highlights

- From 2014 to August 2024, plague cases have been sporadic, with 24 cases and 9 deaths reported over the period. <br/>
- Peaks in cases were observed in 2019, 2020, and 2023, with smaller outbreaks in other years. <br/>
- Deaths have occurred in about 37.5% of cases, indicating a significant fatality risk associated with the disease. <br/>
- The latest reported outbreak in August 2024 includes two cases and one death, suggesting continued public health vigilance is necessary. <br/>

The data for plague cases in mainland China from August 2014 to August 2024 shows intermittent occurrences of the disease, with certain months reporting cases and deaths while many months have no reported cases. Notably, in October 2014, there were two cases and two deaths, indicating a fatal outbreak during that period. Similar instances of fatal cases are seen in December 2017, August 2020, and September 2022, where cases resulted in deaths. More recent spikes include August 2023, with four cases and one death, and August 2024 with two cases and one death.

The data suggests a sporadic pattern of plague outbreaks, with relatively low case numbers but a significant mortality rate when cases do occur. Certain years, such as 2015 and 2018, had no reported cases, reflecting potential effective control measures or a lower prevalence of the disease. However, isolated incidents, such as the spike in 2019 and 2020, emphasize the persistent nature of the plague in this region. This periodic reemergence of the plague, particularly in the latter months of the year, may warrant further investigation into environmental or seasonal factors that could influence outbreaks.
