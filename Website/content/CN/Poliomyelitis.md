# Poliomyelitis

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, or polio, is a highly infectious viral disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects young children and spreads through person-to-person contact, often via the fecal-oral route. The virus multiplies in the intestine and can invade the nervous system, potentially leading to paralysis. While most infections are asymptomatic or cause mild symptoms, a small percentage result in permanent muscle weakness or death. There is no cure for polio, but it is preventable through effective vaccination. Global immunization efforts have significantly reduced polio incidence, bringing the world closer to eradication.

## Highlights

- Poliomyelitis has reported zero cases and zero deaths consistently from March 2015 to March 2025. <br/>
- There are no spikes or fluctuations in data, indicating successful control and prevention measures. <br/>
- The continuous absence of Poliomyelitis cases suggests high vaccination coverage and effective public health strategies. <br/>
- The data highlights China's achievement in maintaining a polio-free status for over a decade. <br/>

China's achievement in maintaining a poliomyelitis-free status suggests not only robust vaccine infrastructure but also effective surveillance to promptly detect and respond to any potential outbreak. This is crucial for ensuring that the disease does not re-emerge, especially considering global travel and the risks posed by poliovirus outbreaks in other parts of the world.
