# Poliomyelitis

Version: August 2025

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is an infectious disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects children under the age of five, spreading through person-to-person contact, contaminated water, and food. The virus multiplies in the intestine and can invade the nervous system, potentially leading to paralysis. While most infections are asymptomatic, about 1 in 200 infections result in irreversible paralysis, predominantly affecting the legs. Vaccination has significantly reduced polio cases globally, yet areas with low immunization rates remain at risk. Eradication efforts continue, aiming to eliminate the disease worldwide through enhanced immunization and surveillance measures.

## Highlights

- **Zero Incidence**: From August 2015 through August 2025, there have been no reported cases of Poliomyelitis in mainland China, indicating sustained polio-free status. <br/>
- **No Mortality**: Along with zero cases, there have also been zero deaths due to poliomyelitis throughout this period, underscoring successful prevention efforts. <br/>
- **Vaccine Success**: The absence of cases suggests effective vaccination campaigns and robust public health strategies for maintaining immunity in the population. <br/>
- **Continued Vigilance**: While the data reflects success in maintaining polio-free status, continuous monitoring and vaccination efforts remain crucial to prevent potential re-emergence. <br/>

The data for poliomyelitis in the Chinese mainland from August 2015 through to August 2025 indicate a complete absence of reported cases and deaths over this period. This consistent record suggests that there have been no new infections or resulting fatalities reported during the timeframe. Such an outcome aligns with China's longstanding polio-free status, as declared by the World Health Organization in 2000. The absence of reported cases reflects the effectiveness of ongoing public health policies focused on surveillance and immunization, which involve extensive vaccination campaigns and the maintenance of a high level of herd immunity across the population.

The data's portrayal of zero cases for over a decade emphasizes the strength of China's healthcare infrastructure and its ability to control and eradicate infectious diseases effectively. The sustained zero reporting could be attributed to systematic vaccination coverage, efficient public health communication, and rapid response strategies to any potential outbreaks. It also reflects the global progress in polio eradication, highlighting regional efforts in maintaining vigilance against any re-emergence, particularly considering the challenges posed by polio-endemic countries elsewhere. This record enhances understanding of successful disease control strategies and serves as an exemplar for other countries aiming to eliminate poliovirus transmission.
