# Poliomyelitis

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a viral infectious disease caused by the poliovirus. It predominantly affects children under five years old and can lead to irreversible paralysis, particularly in the legs. The virus spreads primarily through the fecal-oral route, often via contaminated water or food. While most infections are asymptomatic, a small percentage result in severe neurological complications. With the development of effective vaccines, polio has been nearly eradicated worldwide, though it remains endemic in a few regions. Ongoing global vaccination efforts aim to completely eliminate the disease.

## Highlights

- **No reported cases since 2014:** The data shows no new poliomyelitis cases or deaths from September 2014 to September 2024. <br/>
- **Sustained disease elimination:** China has maintained a polio-free status for a decade, indicating strong vaccination coverage and effective public health measures. <br/>
- **Absence of resurgence:** Despite the global circulation of poliovirus in some regions, there has been no re-emergence in the Chinese mainland, emphasizing successful containment efforts. <br/>
- **Ongoing vigilance needed:** Continued monitoring and immunization efforts are crucial to prevent future outbreaks and maintain eradication success. <br/>
