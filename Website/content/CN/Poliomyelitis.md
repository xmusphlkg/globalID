# Poliomyelitis

Version: February 2025

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a highly infectious viral disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects children under five years of age, spreading through contaminated water and food or contact with an infected person. The virus multiplies in the intestine, from where it can invade the nervous system, potentially leading to paralysis. While many infections are asymptomatic, a small percentage result in irreversible paralysis, and among those, some cases can be fatal. Vaccination has significantly reduced polio incidence worldwide, with ongoing efforts to eradicate the disease completely.

## Highlights

- **Zero Cases Reported:** From February 2015 to February 2025, there have been no reported cases of poliomyelitis in mainland China, indicating effective control and prevention measures. <br/>
- **No Deaths Recorded:** Consistently, there have been no deaths attributed to poliomyelitis during this period, reflecting successful public health interventions. <br/>
- **Sustained Eradication Efforts:** The absence of cases over a decade suggests that eradication efforts, including vaccination campaigns, have been highly effective. <br/>
- **Continued Vigilance Required:** Despite the success, ongoing surveillance and vaccination programs are essential to prevent re-emergence and maintain polio-free status. <br/>
