# Poliomyelitis

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is an infectious disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects young children and can lead to permanent paralysis. The virus spreads through contaminated water and food or contact with an infected person. Most infections are asymptomatic, but in a small percentage of cases, the virus can invade the nervous system, causing muscle weakness and paralysis. Vaccination has significantly reduced polio incidence worldwide, and ongoing global efforts aim to eradicate the disease completely. Polio remains endemic in a few countries despite extensive immunization campaigns.

## Highlights

1. **Consistent Zero Cases**: Since June 2014, there have been zero reported cases of poliomyelitis in the Chinese mainland, indicating effective control and prevention measures.<br/>
2. **No Reported Deaths**: Throughout the entire period from June 2014 to June 2024, there have been no deaths attributed to poliomyelitis.<br/>
3. **Sustained Disease Elimination**: The data reflects sustained elimination of poliomyelitis over a decade, showcasing a robust public health infrastructure and vaccination programs.<br/>
4. **Public Health Success**: The absence of new cases for such an extended period underscores a significant public health success in maintaining polio-free status.<br/>
