# Poliomyelitis

Version: May 2024

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a highly infectious viral disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects children under the age of five, leading to symptoms ranging from mild flu-like signs to severe neurological complications. The virus spreads through person-to-person contact, contaminated water, and food. Although most infections cause no or mild symptoms, about 1 in 200 infections result in irreversible paralysis. Polio can be prevented through vaccination, and global efforts have significantly reduced its prevalence, edging closer to eradication. Despite these advancements, vigilance and continued immunization are crucial to prevent outbreaks.

## Highlights

- No reported cases or deaths from poliomyelitis in Chinese mainland from May 2014 to May 2024. <br/>
- Consistent zero incidence indicates effective polio vaccination and surveillance programs. <br/>
- The sustained absence of poliomyelitis suggests continued poliovirus transmission interruption. <br/>
- Maintaining high immunization coverage and vigilant monitoring remains crucial to prevent reintroduction. <br/>
