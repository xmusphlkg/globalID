# Poliomyelitis

Version: December 2025

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a highly infectious viral disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects young children and is transmitted through the fecal-oral route. The virus multiplies in the intestine and can invade the nervous system, leading to paralysis in severe cases. Most infections are asymptomatic, but some patients develop flu-like symptoms, and a small proportion experience irreversible paralysis, which may be fatal. Global vaccination efforts have drastically reduced polio incidence, but outbreaks persist in areas with low immunization coverage.

## Highlights

- Policymelitis has reported zero cases and zero deaths each month in the Chinese mainland from December 2015 through December 2025, indicating sustained interruption of transmission.<br/>
- This decade-long absence of cases underscores the effectiveness of ongoing immunization campaigns and robust surveillance systems.<br/>
- The consistent lack of disease re-emergence suggests high population immunity and minimal risk of local outbreaks as of December 2025.<br/>
- No evidence of importation or vaccine-derived poliovirus circulation was detected, confirming the maintenance of polio-free status in the region.<br/>

The data reveals a remarkable and sustained absence of reported poliomyelitis cases and deaths in mainland China from December 2015 through at least December 2025. This extended period of zero incidence is highly significant against the historical backdrop of polio as a major public health concern, indicating the effective interruption of indigenous transmission. The consistency across nearly 120 consecutive monthly reports demonstrates not only robust surveillance but also ongoing immunization coverage and the maintenance of public health infrastructure sufficient to prevent both wild-type and vaccine-derived poliovirus outbreaks.

Such an unbroken record points to China’s continued success in upholding polio-free status, following certification for the Western Pacific region in 2000. The data further suggests there are no detectable lapses in case detection or reporting, nor have there been isolated importations resulting in locally transmitted cases. The absence of both cases and deaths underscores a potent combination of rigorous border control measures, high population immunity, and effective response protocols. Sustained vigilance will remain crucial given global concerns around polio resurgence and circulation elsewhere; nonetheless, this dataset attests to a decade of exemplary public health achievement regarding poliomyelitis in China.
