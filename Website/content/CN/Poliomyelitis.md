# Poliomyelitis

Version: October 2025

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a contagious viral infection caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects children under the age of five and can lead to permanent paralysis. The virus is transmitted through person-to-person contact, often via the oral-fecal route, and can infect the central nervous system. Symptoms range from mild flu-like signs to severe muscle weakness and paralysis. Polio has no cure, but vaccination has significantly reduced its prevalence worldwide. Efforts by the Global Polio Eradication Initiative aim to completely eradicate the disease, with ongoing immunization campaigns crucial to preventing outbreaks.

## Highlights

- Sustained Zero Cases: The Chinese mainland has consistently reported zero cases of poliomyelitis from October 2015 through October 2025, indicating successful public health efforts in maintaining eradication. <br/>  
- No Reported Deaths: There have been no deaths from poliomyelitis, further emphasizing effective control measures and the absence of the disease. <br/>  
- Strong Surveillance System: This continued absence of cases reflects a robust surveillance and vaccination system, which has been effective in detecting and preventing any outbreaks. <br/>  
- Vigilance Required: Despite this success, ongoing vigilance and vaccination efforts remain crucial to prevent any resurgence of the disease, especially from external sources. <br/>

The reported data for poliomyelitis in mainland China over an extensive period from October 2015 to October 2025 reveals a remarkable consistency with zero reported cases and deaths throughout. This continuous absence of poliomyelitis cases indicates sustained success in maintaining the disease-free status that China achieved after years of concerted public health efforts. Notably, these efforts have followed the Global Polio Eradication Initiative guidelines, which emphasize immunization, surveillance, and outbreak response. China's robust healthcare infrastructure, widespread vaccination campaigns, and effective monitoring systems have likely been instrumental in achieving this prolonged absence of poliomyelitis cases.

Moreover, the zero-case streak reflects the impact of China's vaccination strategy, particularly the implementation of the Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) and Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV). These vaccines have been pivotal in building population immunity against polio. Additionally, China's strict surveillance system likely plays a crucial role in ensuring that any potential polio resurgence is immediately identified and managed. This data not only manifests successful public health practices but also underlines the importance of continued vigilance and investment in immunization programs to ensure the sustenance of this remarkable polio-free status amid global eradication efforts.
