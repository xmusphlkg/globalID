# Poliomyelitis

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, or polio, is a contagious viral disease primarily affecting children. Caused by the poliovirus, it spreads through person-to-person contact and contaminated food or water. While most infections are mild, about 1 in 200 cases can lead to irreversible paralysis. Symptoms range from fever and fatigue to severe muscle weakness and paralysis. The virus attacks the nervous system, and in severe cases, it can be fatal. Vaccination remains the most effective prevention strategy, significantly reducing global incidence and pushing towards eradication, supported by initiatives like the World Health Organization and the Global Polio Eradication Initiative.

## Highlights

- **Continued Absence of Cases and Deaths:** From October 2014 to October 2024, there have been no reported cases or deaths from poliomyelitis in mainland China, indicating sustained success in eradication efforts. <br/>
- **Effective Immunization Program:** The consistent zero-case trend reflects a robust and effective immunization program, suggesting high vaccination coverage and good public health strategy. <br/>
- **Strong Surveillance Systems:** The absence of cases over the past decade underscores the effectiveness of surveillance and rapid response systems designed to detect and prevent outbreaks. <br/>
- **Global Eradication Alignment:** Mainland China's performance aligns with global poliovirus eradication goals, contributing to regional stability and reduced risk of cross-border transmission. <br/>
