# Poliomyelitis

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Poliomyelitis, commonly known as polio, is a highly infectious viral disease caused by the poliovirus. It primarily affects children under five years of age. The virus spreads through person-to-person contact, often via contaminated water or food. Polio can invade the nervous system, potentially leading to paralysis, and in severe cases, it can be fatal. While there is no cure, polio can be prevented through vaccination. Global vaccination efforts have significantly reduced polio cases, with the disease now endemic in only a few countries. Continued immunization is crucial to achieving complete eradication.

## Highlights

- **Zero Cases and Deaths:** From December 2014 to December 2024, there have been no reported cases or deaths due to poliomyelitis in mainland China. This indicates a successful control and prevention strategy. <br/>
- **Sustained Eradication:** The data reflects a decade-long period of zero incidence, suggesting that poliomyelitis has been effectively eradicated in the region. <br/>
- **Effective Public Health Measures:** The absence of cases over such an extended period highlights the effectiveness of vaccination programs and public health policies in maintaining polio-free status. <br/>
- **Continued Vigilance Required:** Despite the success, ongoing surveillance and vaccination efforts are crucial to prevent re-emergence, especially with global travel and potential importation risks. <br/>
