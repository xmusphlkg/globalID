# Rabies

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Rabies is a viral zoonotic disease predominantly transmitted through the bite of an infected animal, commonly dogs. The rabies virus affects the central nervous system, leading to encephalitis and, if untreated, is almost always fatal. Early symptoms include fever, headache, and generalized weakness, progressing to more severe neurological manifestations such as agitation, hallucinations, and hydrophobia. Preventive measures include vaccination of animals, post-exposure prophylaxis for humans, and public awareness campaigns. Effective post-exposure treatment, administered promptly, can prevent the onset of symptoms and subsequent death.

## Highlights

- **Decline in Cases and Deaths**: Rabies cases and deaths in mainland China have significantly declined from 2014 to 2024, reflecting successful public health interventions.<br/>
- **Seasonal Fluctuations**: Higher numbers of cases and deaths typically occurred during the summer months (July-September), indicating possible seasonal factors influencing rabies transmission.<br/>
- **Stabilization in Recent Years**: From 2022 onwards, the number of cases and deaths has stabilized at low levels, with monthly figures rarely exceeding 20 cases.<br/>
- **Sustained Low Incidence in 2024**: As of July 2024, rabies incidence remains low, with only 16 cases and 11 deaths, continuing the trend of controlled rabies spread.<br/>

The reported cases of rabies in mainland China from July 2014 to July 2024 reveal a significant overall decline in both cases and deaths. In mid-2014, monthly cases and deaths were relatively high, with numbers often exceeding 80 cases and deaths. However, starting from 2015, a noticeable downward trend is observed. By 2018, the number of cases and deaths begins to stabilize at a lower level, generally fluctuating between 30 to 50 cases and deaths per month. This decreasing trend continues into subsequent years, with some minor fluctuations, reaching a low in early 2024, where the numbers further dip below 20 cases and deaths per month.

This decline may suggest effective rabies control measures, such as increased public awareness, better vaccination coverage, and improved post-exposure prophylaxis. However, periodic spikes in cases, such as those seen in late 2015, late 2016, and early 2021, indicate that localized outbreaks or challenges in maintaining consistent control efforts may still exist. The lower and more stable rates from 2018 onwards could reflect sustained public health interventions and monitoring, though the persistence of any cases and deaths highlights the need for continued vigilance and improvement in rabies prevention strategies.

The analysis of rabies deaths in mainland China from mid-2014 to mid-2024 reveals a general downward trend in the number of deaths over the years, though there are fluctuations. In 2014 and 2015, the number of deaths remained relatively high, with some months, such as September 2014 and January 2015, reporting close to or more than 90 deaths. However, starting from 2016, there was a notable decline in the number of reported deaths, with more stable but lower monthly counts generally hovering between 20 to 50 deaths until around 2018. By 2019 and onwards, the trend further declines with monthly deaths often falling below 30, except for occasional spikes.

The period from 2020 to 2024 shows a marked decrease in both cases and deaths, indicating improvements in rabies control measures and possibly better access to post-exposure prophylaxis or public awareness. Despite some months showing relatively higher numbers (e.g., December 2020 and January 2021 with 29 and 25 deaths, respectively), the overall pattern suggests a decline in rabies mortality, reflecting successful public health interventions. However, the data still show sporadic spikes in deaths even in the later years, suggesting that while control measures may be effective overall, there may still be gaps in coverage or challenges in managing rabies cases in specific regions or populations.
