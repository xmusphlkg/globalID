## Introduction

Rabies is a viral disease that affects the central nervous system of mammals, including humans. It is primarily transmitted through the bite of an infected animal, most commonly dogs. The rabies virus travels from the site of the bite to the brain, causing inflammation and leading to symptoms such as fever, headache, excess salivation, muscle spasms, paralysis, and mental confusion. Once clinical symptoms appear, rabies is almost always fatal. However, it is preventable through prompt post-exposure prophylaxis, which includes wound cleaning and vaccination. Rabies remains a significant public health concern in many parts of the world.

## Highlights



- **Decline in Cases and Deaths:** From 2015 to 2025, rabies cases and deaths in the Chinese mainland have significantly decreased, indicating improved control measures. <br/>

- **Fluctuations in Monthly Data:** Despite the overall decline, there are fluctuations, with occasional spikes, such as in August 2024, suggesting seasonal or regional variations. <br/>

- **Recent Low Numbers:** As of February 2025, cases and deaths are at a low, with only 4 cases and 7 deaths, reflecting effective public health interventions. <br/>

- **Sustained Control Efforts Needed:** Continued efforts in vaccination, public education, and surveillance are essential to maintain and further reduce rabies incidence. <br/>

The data on rabies cases and deaths in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals several trends and fluctuations. Initially, in 2015, there was a relatively high number of cases and deaths, with a peak in August 2015, where 86 cases and 78 deaths were reported. This period was marked by a high case-fatality ratio, indicating the severe nature of rabies and the challenges in timely medical intervention or vaccination post-exposure. The subsequent years show a general decline in both cases and deaths, with some fluctuations. For instance, 2016 saw a decrease in cases and deaths compared to 2015, but there were still months with significant numbers, such as September and October.

From 2017 onwards, there is a noticeable downward trend in both cases and deaths, with some months showing a significant reduction. By 2020, the numbers had decreased considerably, with some months reporting as few as 12 cases and 9 deaths. This trend continues into 2021 and 2022, with the lowest numbers recorded in February 2022, with only 7 cases and 4 deaths. However, there is a slight increase in cases and deaths towards the end of 2024 and into 2025, suggesting a potential resurgence or reporting anomaly. This data highlights the importance of sustained rabies control measures, including vaccination of domestic animals, public awareness, and access to post-exposure prophylaxis to maintain low incidence rates.

The data on rabies in mainland China from 2015 to early 2025 reveals a fluctuating pattern in both reported cases and deaths, with a general trend of decline over the years. In 2015, the number of cases and deaths was relatively high, with peaks in the summer months, such as August, where 86 cases and 78 deaths were reported. This pattern of higher incidence during warmer months is consistent with rabies transmission dynamics, as increased outdoor activities and interactions with animals can lead to more exposure.

From 2016 onwards, there is a noticeable decrease in both cases and deaths, with some fluctuations. By 2020, the numbers have significantly reduced, with monthly cases often below 20 and deaths following a similar trend. This decline could be attributed to improved public health measures, increased awareness, and possibly better access to post-exposure prophylaxis. However, the data also shows occasional spikes, such as in December 2020 and December 2022, indicating that while overall control has improved, rabies remains a public health concern that requires continuous monitoring and intervention efforts.
