# Rabies

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Rabies is a viral disease affecting mammals, including humans, transmitted through the bite of an infected animal. The virus impacts the central nervous system, causing brain inflammation and is almost always fatal if untreated. Initial symptoms include fever, headache, and weakness, progressing to severe neurological issues such as paralysis, hallucinations, and agitation. Rabies can be prevented through immediate wound cleaning and post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) with the rabies vaccine and immune globulin. Public health strategies focus on vaccinating domestic animals and wildlife management to control the spread of the virus.

## Highlights

- Rabies cases and deaths in mainland China have declined markedly from 2015 to 2025, with monthly cases peaking at 86 in August 2015 and dropping to under 20 in most months since 2021. <br/>
- Since 2022, both incidence and mortality have remained low and stable, with monthly deaths rarely exceeding 20, indicating improved control measures and healthcare access. <br/>
- Seasonal patterns persist, with slightly higher case numbers during summer and autumn months (Julyâ€“October), consistent with increased animal-human contact. <br/>
- As of March 2025, the disease remains under control, with only 13 cases and 9 deaths reported, suggesting continued effectiveness of rabies surveillance and prevention strategies. <br/>

The data for rabies cases in the Chinese mainland from March 2015 to March 2025 shows significant variations over the years with both fluctuations and trends becoming evident. Starting with 2015, the number of cases generally increased until December, peaking in the last two months, where both cases and deaths reached particularly high values. Moving into 2016, the numbers began to show signs of stabilization and then a general decrease throughout the year, notably March and April recorded the lowest numbers in both cases and deaths. However, there was a significant jump again toward the end of 2016 in terms of cases, while deaths fluctuated throughout.

From 2017 onwards, the patterns indicate a slow but steady decline in rabies cases across the years, though occasional spikes were observable, such as in January 2018 and November 2019. The data indicates a noticeable decline in the number of reported cases and deaths through 2020 to mid-2021, possibly reflecting better management strategies or external factors influencing rabies incidence. Nonetheless, the fluctuations persist, as the numbers slightly rise again and maintain variability in the subsequent years until 2025, where sporadic trends of increases and decreases mark the latter half of the period. This analysis suggests an overall progressive decline in rabies cases and deaths in the Chinese mainland, notwithstanding intermittent increases that underline the continuing need for targeted public health interventions.

Throughout 2019 to 2023, the data illustrates a further reduction with case numbers mostly below 20 and deaths infrequently reaching previous highs, such as the consistently low death rate recorded from early 2020 up to 2021. Overall, the trends suggest effective public health measures over the years leading to a reduction in rabies fatalities across the timeframe, notwithstanding occasional rises which warrant further examination to elucidate causative factors.
