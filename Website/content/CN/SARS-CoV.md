# SARS-CoV

Version: September 2024

## Introduction

Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (SARS-CoV) is a virus responsible for an outbreak that began in Asia in 2002. It belongs to the coronavirus family, characterized by their crown-like appearance under a microscope. SARS-CoV primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and causes symptoms like fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. The outbreak resulted in significant morbidity and mortality before being effectively contained in 2003 through public health interventions, including isolation and quarantine measures. Advances in understanding coronaviruses have since informed responses to other outbreaks, including the COVID-19 pandemic caused by a different but related coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2.

## Highlights

- No new cases or deaths from SARS-CoV have been reported in mainland China from September 2014 through September 2024. <br/>
- The data shows a consistent absence of SARS-CoV transmission, indicating effective long-term control or elimination of the virus in the region. <br/>
- The stable epidemiological trend over the past decade suggests that preventive measures and surveillance have been highly effective in preventing any resurgence of SARS-CoV. <br/>
- The situation as of September 2024 demonstrates the continued success of public health efforts in maintaining a SARS-free status across the mainland. <br/>

The provided data indicates that there were no reported cases or deaths due to SARS-CoV (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus) in mainland China from September 2014 through September 2024. This consistent lack of recorded cases suggests a successful containment or eradication of the virus in the region over the course of a decade. Given that SARS-CoV first emerged in 2002-2003, causing a significant global outbreak, the subsequent absence of cases aligns with the global control measures implemented after the initial epidemic, including heightened surveillance, public health interventions, and international cooperation to prevent the virus from re-emerging.

The zero-case trend across 10 years also highlights the long-term effectiveness of public health measures such as quarantines, improved sanitation, and enhanced monitoring systems in China. Despite sporadic concerns over SARS-CoV in the years following the initial outbreak, the data shows that there have been no resurgences of the virus, contributing to an extended period of public health stability concerning SARS. This outcome is particularly noteworthy considering the ongoing monitoring efforts for other coronaviruses during this period, including the emergence of SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) in late 2019.
