# SARS-CoV

Version: May 2025

## Introduction

Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (SARS-CoV) is a zoonotic virus responsible for the SARS outbreak in 2002–2003. It belongs to the Betacoronavirus genus and is believed to have originated in bats, with civet cats as intermediate hosts. SARS-CoV primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and causes flu-like symptoms, potentially progressing to severe pneumonia and acute respiratory distress syndrome. The outbreak affected over 8,000 people across 29 countries, resulting in nearly 800 deaths. Control measures, including isolation and quarantine, were effective in containing its transmission. SARS-CoV is genetically distinct from SARS-CoV-2, which causes COVID-19.

## Highlights

- Since May 2015, reported cases of SARS-CoV in mainland China have consistently remained at zero, indicating effective control and no recent outbreaks. <br/>
- The absence of deaths associated with SARS-CoV demonstrates a continued successful management of the disease, preventing any loss of life. <br/>
- January 2015 through May 2025 shows no epidemiological change, suggesting robust surveillance and preventive measures are maintaining stability. <br/>
- With no new cases or deaths, focus on holding preparedness and response systems allows for efficient handling if the virus re-emerges in the future. <br/>

The dataset from May 2015 to May 2025 for SARS-CoV in mainland China shows zero reported cases and deaths throughout the entire period. This consistent absence suggests that SARS-CoV has remained fully contained in the region since the end of the 2003 outbreak. The stability in the data implies no community transmission or reintroduction of the virus, reflecting effective long-term public health controls.

Such control likely results from sustained disease surveillance, strict protocols for emerging respiratory illnesses, and enhanced infection prevention practices. The data also suggests the integration of SARS-CoV monitoring within broader epidemic detection systems, which may have deterred unnoticed viral spread. Preventive actions—including rapid isolation measures and international health cooperation—appear to have minimized any resurgence risk. This case profile demonstrates a successful model of long-term eradication through coordinated effort, with no signs of re-emergence over the examined decade.
