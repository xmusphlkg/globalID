# SARS-CoV

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

SARS-CoV, or Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus, is a virus identified in 2003 as the causative agent of an outbreak of respiratory illness. Originating in southern China in late 2002, the virus quickly spread to over two dozen countries before being contained. SARS-CoV primarily transmitted through respiratory droplets, affecting the human respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as fever, cough, and pneumonia. It is believed to have zoonotic origins, likely transmitted from bats to civet cats, and then to humans. SARS-CoV heightened global awareness of coronaviruses and laid the groundwork for managing future coronavirus outbreaks.

## Highlights

- **No Reported Cases or Deaths:** From July 2014 to July 2024, there have been no reported cases or deaths due to SARS-CoV in the Chinese mainland. <br/>
- **Stable Epidemiological Situation:** The data indicates a stable epidemiological situation with no resurgence of SARS-CoV infections during this 10-year period. <br/>
- **Effective Control Measures:** The absence of cases suggests that existing public health measures and surveillance systems have been effective in preventing the re-emergence of the virus. <br/>

The data for SARS-CoV cases on the Chinese mainland from July 2014 to July 2024 indicates that there were no reported cases or deaths during this period. This extended period of zero reporting suggests the absence of new SARS-CoV outbreaks or cases during these years. Considering the infectious nature of SARS-CoV, significant efforts in public health measures, surveillance, and control protocols are likely to have contributed to this sustained period of no new cases. These measures could include rigorous infection control, monitoring potential sources of the virus, and rapid response systems to address any emerging threats.

This lack of reported cases over the years may reflect the effectiveness of interventions implemented since the original SARS outbreak in the early 2000s, which had a profound impact on public health policies globally. The absence of new cases could also speak to the global reduction in SARS-CoV cases due to drastic international travel and trade restrictions, improved laboratory capacity for pathogen detection, and awareness among healthcare workers. Despite the potential for zoonotic spillover events, enhanced preparedness and response mechanisms may have mitigated the emergence or re-emergence of SARS-CoV in the Chinese mainland during the period analyzed.
