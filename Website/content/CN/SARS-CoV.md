# SARS-CoV

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

SARS-CoV, or Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus, is a virus that emerged in 2002, causing the first known outbreak of SARS. It is a member of the Coronaviridae family and is zoonotic, primarily transmitted from animals to humans, with civet cats identified as a key reservoir. The virus spreads through respiratory droplets and can lead to severe respiratory illness, characterized by fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. The outbreak resulted in over 8,000 cases and 774 deaths worldwide, prompting global health responses and increased research into coronaviruses, ultimately influencing the understanding of related viruses like SARS-CoV-2.

## Highlights

- **No reported cases or deaths:** From January 2015 to January 2025, there have been no reported cases or deaths due to SARS-CoV in the Chinese mainland. This indicates effective control and prevention measures. <br/>

- **Sustained containment:** The absence of cases over a decade suggests that containment strategies have been consistently effective, preventing any outbreaks. <br/>

- **Public health success:** The data reflects a significant public health achievement, maintaining zero incidence of SARS-CoV over an extended period. <br/>

- **Continued vigilance required:** Despite the success, ongoing surveillance and preparedness are essential to prevent potential future outbreaks. <br/>
