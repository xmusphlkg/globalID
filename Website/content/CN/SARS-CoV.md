# SARS-CoV

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (SARS-CoV) is a contagious virus that emerged in 2002 in Guangdong, China. It is a zoonotic virus that belongs to the coronavirus family, closely linked to viruses found in bats. SARS-CoV causes SARS, a respiratory illness characterized by fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. The virus spreads via respiratory droplets and has a notable ability to transmit from person to person, leading to a global outbreak by 2003, with over 8,000 cases and nearly 800 deaths before being effectively contained. Its emergence underscored the need for vigilant global health surveillance and rapid response strategies.

## Highlights

- No reported cases of SARS-CoV have been documented in the Chinese mainland from August 2014 to August 2024.<br/>
- The absence of new cases indicates successful containment or lack of transmission of SARS-CoV during this period.<br/>
- There have been zero deaths linked to SARS-CoV in the Chinese mainland during the entire time frame.<br/>
- Continued monitoring and surveillance efforts are important to maintain this trend of zero cases and ensure early detection of any potential outbreaks.<br/>

The data on SARS-CoV cases in mainland China from August 2014 to August 2024 shows an absence of reported cases and deaths throughout the entire period. This consistent pattern indicates no resurgence of the virus during these ten years. Following the initial SARS outbreak in 2002â€“2003, which caused a significant global health crisis, the containment and control measures seem to have effectively prevented any further cases in subsequent years.

The lack of new cases from 2014 onward may reflect the success of public health interventions such as improved surveillance, rapid response capabilities, and infection control practices. This pattern also suggests that SARS-CoV has not re-emerged as a public health threat in mainland China, despite the ongoing monitoring efforts. The data underscores the importance of vigilance in preventing future outbreaks of novel coronaviruses, given that other coronaviruses, like SARS-CoV-2, have had significant global impacts since 2020.
