# Scarlet fever

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Scarlet fever, also known as scarlatina, is an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Streptococcus pyogenes, or group A streptococcus. It primarily affects children between the ages of 5 and 15. The disease is characterized by a distinctive red rash, high fever, and a "strawberry" tongue. Transmission occurs via respiratory droplets from coughs and sneezes. Historically, scarlet fever was more severe, but today it can be effectively treated with antibiotics. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to prevent potential complications, such as rheumatic fever and kidney disease. Good hygiene practices can help reduce its spread.

## Highlights

- Scarlet fever in China shows seasonal peaks, particularly from May to July, with 6,309 cases in July 2024, reflecting an increase from previous months. <br/>
- Cases dropped significantly during the COVID-19 pandemic but have been rising again since early 2023, with a noticeable resurgence in 2024. <br/>
- Mortality remains extremely low, with only two deaths reported since 2015, indicating the disease is generally not fatal. <br/>
- The situation in July 2024 suggests ongoing transmission, with rising case numbers compared to recent years, highlighting the need for continued public health monitoring. <br/>
