# Scarlet fever

Version: March 2025

## Introduction

Scarlet fever is an infectious disease resulting from Group A Streptococcus bacteria, often affecting children aged 5-15. It is characterized by a red rash, sore throat, and high fever. The rash typically manifests as tiny red bumps, lending the skin a sandpaper-like texture. Additionally, the tongue may appear red and swollen, known as "strawberry tongue." Transmission occurs via respiratory droplets or contact with infected surfaces. While once feared for its complications, scarlet fever is now treatable with antibiotics, reducing severity and spread. Early detection and treatment are crucial for preventing complications such as rheumatic fever.

## Highlights

- Scarlet fever cases in China have shown seasonal fluctuations with peaks typically occurring in late spring to early summer. <br/>
- There was a notable increase in cases from March 2020 to September 2022, followed by a significant drop until early 2023. <br/>
- Monthly case counts in early 2025 show a gradual rise, indicating a possible resurgence in spring. <br/>
- Mortality rates have remained very low, with only three deaths recorded over the entire dataset period, highlighting effective management and control measures. <br/>

The reported data for Scarlet fever in mainland China displays notable trends over the observed period. From 2015 to 2019, there is a clear seasonality in case incidence, with peaks around May and June, and lower counts during late summer and early autumn. This cyclical pattern suggests influences from climatic or social factors that promote transmission at specific times of the year. Deaths remain exceedingly rare, highlighting the disease's low mortality despite high incidence.

In 2020, the pattern shifts dramatically, coinciding with the COVID-19 pandemic's impact on reporting and transmission. Case numbers plummet in early 2020, with March and April showing the lowest figures, potentially due to stringent public health measures and reduced interactions. Post-2020, there is a gradual rise in cases but they remain below pre-2020 levels, suggesting lasting pandemic effects on transmission or reporting.

In recent years, up to 2025, case numbers indicate a steady increase, hinting at a resurgence as conditions normalize or changes in public health strategies take place. This illustrates the complex interplay between environmental factors, social behavior, and public health interventions on Scarlet fever's prevalence in China. Overall, while the disease affects significant numbers, the low mortality rate reflects effective management or inherent disease characteristics limiting fatalities.
