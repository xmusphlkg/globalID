# Scarlet fever

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Scarlet fever is an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Streptococcus pyogenes, commonly known as group A Streptococcus. It primarily affects children and is characterized by a distinctive red rash, usually accompanied by a sore throat and high fever. The rash typically begins on the chest and stomach before spreading to other parts of the body. Other symptoms may include a "strawberry" tongue, flushed face, and swollen glands. Scarlet fever is spread through respiratory droplets and direct contact with an infected person. With the advent of antibiotics, the disease is effectively treatable, significantly reducing complications and transmission.

## Highlights

- Scarlet fever cases in the Chinese mainland have surged significantly in 2024 compared to the previous year, with peaks in May and June exceeding 10,000 cases.<br/>

- The disease exhibits a seasonal pattern, with increased incidences typically seen in late spring and early summer, indicating potential environmental or societal influences on outbreaks.<br/>

- Despite fluctuating case numbers, mortality rates are remarkably low, with only a few deaths recorded from 2014 to 2024, highlighting effective healthcare and treatment measures.<br/>

- Recent months in 2024 show declining cases after mid-year peaks, but continued monitoring is essential to prevent future surges.<br/>

The data on Scarlet fever in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 shows fluctuating seasonal patterns, with notable peaks and decreases in reported cases. Between 2014 and 2019, Scarlet fever generally displayed a pattern of higher incidence in late spring and early summer, peaking notably in May and June each year. This pattern was interrupted in 2020 when a significant drop in cases was observed across all months, likely due to public health measures and behavioral changes in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the Scarlet fever case patterns reverted to the original trend post-2020, with cases gradually increasing over the years toward the historical month-specific highs.

Mortality related to Scarlet fever remained extremely low throughout the years, with just two recorded deaths between 2014 and 2024. The consistent detection and reporting even at low incident months indicate robust monitoring and reporting mechanisms by Chinese public health authorities. Additionally, the data may suggest an effective control and management system for Scarlet fever, which is reflected in a low case-fatality ratio. The variations and often cyclical nature in the number of cases highlight the potential for Scarlet fever outbreaks, particularly in the absence of interventions, underscoring the importance of continuous public health surveillance and targeted interventions during peak seasons.
