# Syphilis

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum. It presents in distinct stages, starting with a painless sore at the infection site, known as a chancre, during the primary stage. If untreated, it progresses to secondary syphilis, characterized by skin rashes and mucous membrane lesions. The latent stage follows, which can last for years without symptoms, before potentially advancing to tertiary syphilis, causing severe health complications affecting the heart, brain, and other organs. Syphilis is typically transmitted through sexual contact, but can also be passed from mother to child during pregnancy, leading to congenital syphilis.

## Highlights

- **Rising Cases:** Syphilis cases in mainland China have steadily increased, peaking at 64,161 in March 2024, indicating a growing public health concern. <br/>
- **Low Deaths:** Deaths remain low, fluctuating between 1 and 25 per month, with no clear pattern, suggesting varying outbreak severity. <br/>
- **Seasonal Patterns:** Monthly case fluctuations suggest possible seasonal patterns, with higher numbers seen in early to mid-year. <br/>
- **Recent Decline:** August 2024 shows a decrease to 58,717 cases, down from the March peak, indicating potential stabilization or early success in control measures. <br/>
