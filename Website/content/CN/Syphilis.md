# Syphilis

Version: October 2024

## Introduction

Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum. It progresses through four stages: primary, secondary, latent, and tertiary. The primary stage features a sore called a chancre; the secondary includes skin rashes. If untreated, it enters a latent phase, possibly advancing to the tertiary stage with severe complications like neurological and cardiovascular problems. Transmission mainly occurs through sexual contact, but can also pass from mother to child during pregnancy, leading to congenital syphilis. Early detection and antibiotic treatment, usually with penicillin, are effective in managing the disease.

## Highlights

- Syphilis cases in mainland China have shown a significant increase from early to mid-2024, maintaining high levels throughout the summer months, peaking in March at 64,161 cases. <br/>
- Since 2014, fluctuations have been observed with a general upward trend in cases, particularly noticeable from 2019 onwards, which suggests ongoing transmission and possibly improved detection. <br/>
- Deaths related to syphilis are relatively low compared to the number of cases, indicating better treatment or management; in 2024, monthly deaths range from 2 to 7. <br/>
- The consistent rise in syphilis cases highlights the need for intensified public health interventions focused on prevention, education, and early treatment to mitigate further spread. <br/>

Initially, from 2014 to 2017, monthly cases ranged from about 40,000 to 48,000. Post-2017, there was a frequent climb above 50,000, with a peak over 61,000 in 2023, suggesting potential shifts in disease dynamics or surveillance enhancements.

Despite the increasing number of cases, monthly deaths remained low relative to case numbers, underscoring the chronic but manageable nature of syphilis with effective treatment. Most months recorded single-digit deaths, although there were occasional peaks, like in January 2020, with 25 deaths. These peaks might reflect external stressors affecting healthcare delivery or temporary increases in disease severity. The low mortality amidst high case numbers may highlight advancements in healthcare response capabilities or the effectiveness of existing treatment protocols. The sporadic nature of death peaks suggests that health systems generally contained the disease's impact effectively, although unforeseen challenges probably emerged during certain periods, possibly due to broader socio-economic or health-related crises.
