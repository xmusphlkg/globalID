# Syphilis

Version: January 2025

## Introduction

Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum. It progresses through distinct stages: primary, secondary, latent, and tertiary. The primary stage is marked by a painless sore at the infection site, while the secondary stage involves skin rashes and mucous membrane lesions. If untreated, the infection can progress to the latent stage, potentially leading to severe complications in the tertiary stage, affecting organs like the heart and brain. Syphilis is treatable with antibiotics, particularly penicillin, but early detection is crucial to prevent long-term health issues.

## Highlights

- **Rising Trend in Cases**: From 2015 to 2025, syphilis cases have increased significantly, peaking in March 2024 with 64,161 cases, indicating a growing public health challenge. <br/>

- **Fluctuating Death Rates**: Deaths remain low compared to cases, with occasional spikes, such as 25 in January 2020, suggesting effective treatment but a need for ongoing monitoring. <br/>

- **Seasonal Variations**: Higher cases are typically observed in the first half of the year, indicating potential socio-environmental factors affecting transmission. <br/>

- **Recent Decline**: As of January 2025, there is a slight decline in cases from previous months, suggesting improvements in control measures. <br/>

The data on syphilis cases in the Chinese mainland from 2015 to 2025 reveals a concerning trend in the incidence of this sexually transmitted infection. Over the years, there has been a noticeable fluctuation in the number of reported cases, with peaks occurring in certain months. For instance, March 2018 recorded the highest number of cases at 49,546, while January 2015 also saw a significant spike with 36,339 cases. This variability suggests potential seasonal patterns or the impact of public health interventions, awareness campaigns, or changes in testing practices that could influence reporting rates.

In terms of mortality, the number of deaths associated with syphilis remains relatively low compared to the number of cases, indicating that while syphilis is a significant public health issue, it is often manageable with appropriate treatment. The data shows that deaths peaked in January 2020 with 25 reported deaths, which may correlate with broader healthcare challenges during that period, possibly exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic. Overall, the analysis of this data underscores the need for ongoing surveillance, targeted prevention strategies, and public health education to address the rising incidence of syphilis in the region.
