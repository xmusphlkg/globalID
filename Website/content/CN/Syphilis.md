# Syphilis

Version: July 2024

## Introduction

Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium *Treponema pallidum*. It progresses through distinct stages: primary, secondary, latent, and tertiary. The primary stage typically presents with a painless sore at the infection site, while the secondary stage may include skin rashes, mucous membrane lesions, and flu-like symptoms. If untreated, syphilis can progress to the latent stage without symptoms and potentially advance to the tertiary stage, causing severe health issues such as neurological and cardiovascular complications. Diagnosis is through blood tests, and it is effectively treated with antibiotics, commonly penicillin.

## Highlights

- **Steady Increase in Cases**: Syphilis cases in mainland China have shown a consistent upward trend, reaching 62,430 in July 2024, the highest recorded in the data set. <br/>
- **Low Mortality Rate**: Despite the rise in cases, the number of deaths has remained relatively low, with only 5 deaths reported in July 2024, indicating effective management of the disease. <br/>
- **Seasonal Variations**: There is a noticeable fluctuation in case numbers, with peaks often occurring in the summer months, suggesting possible seasonal factors influencing transmission. <br/>
- **Recent Surge**: The period from early 2023 to mid-2024 saw significant growth in monthly case numbers, highlighting an ongoing public health concern. <br/>

The reported syphilis cases in mainland China from mid-2014 to mid-2024 exhibit notable fluctuations with an overall upward trend over the years. In the earlier years documented, from 2014 to 2016, monthly syphilis cases typically ranged from the mid-30,000s to low 40,000s, with sporadic months witnessing slightly lower counts such as February 2015, which registered 25,771 cases. Cases exhibited some seasonality or periodic fluctuations, with a tendency toward higher numbers in the middle of each year. Moving towards 2017 and beyond, this trend of incremental increases continues, culminating in monthly case counts consistently surpassing 50,000 by the early 2020s.

From 2020 onwards, despite the influence of COVID-19 on healthcare systems, syphilis cases showed resilience, continuing to rise and peaking dramatically around early 2024 with some of the highest monthly figures recorded, notably 64,161 in March 2024. Although there is an evident rise in the number of cases over the decade, the death count remained relatively controlled, rarely exceeding single digits monthly. This suggests improvements or sustained efficacy in public health measures, treatment availability, or both, which have effectively kept mortality low despite increasing infections. Overall, the gradual incline in reported cases underscores the need for continued public health interventions to address syphilis transmission in China.

Noteworthy peaks in syphilis deaths include August 2017 with 19 deaths and January 2020 with 25 deaths, which also coincide with lower reported cases compared to other high-case periods, suggesting possible reporting anomalies or specific socio-economic circumstances impacting mortality.

Over the observed period, the incidence of syphilis cases shows an overall increasing trend. However, mortality does not follow the same pattern, generally remaining low relative to the number of cases. The mortality data reveals no clear seasonal pattern, different from the number of cases which appear to peak sporadically throughout the years. From 2021 onward, there is a noticeable reduction in deaths despite an upward trend in cases, indicating potential improvements in healthcare management and treatment of syphilis, leading to reduced fatalities even as the infection rate rises. These findings highlight the continual need for public health interventions focused on early detection and treatment to manage syphilis efficiently and maintain low mortality rates.
