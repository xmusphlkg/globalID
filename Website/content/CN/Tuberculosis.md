# Tuberculosis

Version: November 2024

## Introduction

Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection caused by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, primarily affecting the lungs but can also impact other parts of the body. It spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or speaks. Symptoms include persistent cough, chest pain, weight loss, fever, and night sweats. TB can be latent or active, with latent TB not showing symptoms but still contagious under certain conditions. Treatment typically involves a long course of antibiotics. Drug-resistant TB is a growing concern, requiring specialized treatment regimens.

## Highlights

1. **Declining trends in TB**: TB cases and deaths have decreased significantly from 2015-2017 peaks. In November 2024, there were 51,790 cases and 238 deaths. <br/>
2. **Seasonal variation**: TB cases tend to increase during the colder months, particularly from January to March. <br/>
3. **COVID-19 effects**: The COVID-19 pandemic led to a temporary drop in TB cases in 2020, possibly due to reduced diagnostic access and public health disruptions. <br/>
4. **Ongoing burden**: While cases have decreased, TB remains a significant public health concern in China, with notable mortality, emphasizing the need for continued control efforts. <br/>

The data on tuberculosis (TB) cases and deaths in mainland China from 2014 to 2024 reveals notable fluctuations in both incidence and mortality. The number of TB cases peaked in 2016 but gradually declined in the following years, with a sharp drop in early 2020, likely due to COVID-19-related disruptions in healthcare services. Despite the reduction in cases, TB-related deaths remained a significant concern, with mortality fluctuating more than incidence. In particular, there were notable spikes in deaths in early 2022, with monthly death counts exceeding 300, and similar increases in 2023 and 2024. These fluctuations may reflect the strain on the healthcare system during the pandemic, delays in diagnosis, or challenges in TB management.

By 2023 and 2024, the overall number of TB cases continued to decrease, though the death toll remained relatively high, particularly in the latter months of 2024. This suggests that while case numbers may be declining, the mortality rate is still a pressing issue, possibly due to late-stage diagnoses or insufficient treatment access. The data highlights the need for focused efforts on improving TB control, with particular attention to early diagnosis, treatment adherence, and addressing healthcare disruptions. Additionally, regional disparities in TB incidence warrant targeted interventions to further reduce the disease burden.

The mortality trends for tuberculosis (TB) in the Chinese mainland show variability, with notable seasonal spikes, especially toward the end of the year. December often sees higher death counts, such as in 2022 and 2023, where deaths rose to 316 and 416, respectively. This seasonal increase may be linked to factors like healthcare strain, delayed diagnoses, or complications from colder weather. 

Between 2020 and 2024, there is a noticeable decline in TB-related deaths despite relatively stable case numbers. In particular, the death toll decreased over time, with 2024 showing a reduction in mortality, with deaths dropping to 238 in November despite substantial case loads. This trend may reflect improvements in TB treatment and care, as well as enhanced preventative measures. However, fluctuations remain, particularly in regions where healthcare resources are more strained. The case fatality ratio seems to have stabilized, suggesting progress in TB management, but ongoing challenges persist, especially with TB's seasonal and regional variability. 

While overall deaths have decreased, the data highlights the continued need for targeted interventions, particularly in high-risk periods and regions, to further reduce mortality and improve control efforts.
