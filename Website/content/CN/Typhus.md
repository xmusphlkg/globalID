# Typhus

Version: December 2024

## Introduction

Typhus is a group of infectious diseases caused by Rickettsia bacteria, transmitted to humans through lice, fleas, or mites. The most common forms include epidemic typhus, spread by body lice, and murine typhus, spread by fleas. Symptoms typically appear 1-2 weeks after exposure and include high fever, headache, rash, and muscle pain. Historically, typhus has been associated with crowded and unsanitary conditions, such as those found in war zones or refugee camps. Treatment involves antibiotics like doxycycline, and prevention focuses on improving hygiene and controlling vector populations.

## Highlights

- **Rising Case Numbers**: From 2014 to 2024, Typhus cases have increased, with significant peaks during summer months, especially from 2022 onwards. <br/>

- **Seasonal Trends**: Higher case numbers consistently occur in warmer months (May to October), suggesting environmental influences. <br/>

- **Minimal Mortality**: Despite rising cases, mortality remains low, with only two deaths reported, indicating effective treatment. <br/>

- **Recent Increase**: A notable surge in cases was observed in 2023, peaking in September and continuing into 2024, emphasizing the need for continued public health efforts. <br/>

The data on Typhus cases in mainland China from December 2014 to December 2024 shows fluctuating monthly cases with an overall upward trend. Initially, in 2015, cases increased from January to October, peaking at 189. This pattern of rising cases towards the year's end is consistent in subsequent years, suggesting a seasonal trend or cyclical factors influencing Typhus incidence.

From 2016 onwards, higher cases are consistently reported from May to October, with peaks often in September or October. This could be due to environmental or social factors prevalent during these months, such as increased human activity or favorable conditions for Typhus vectors. Despite the rising number of cases, deaths remain extremely low, with only two reported over the entire period, indicating effective disease management and treatment.

In recent years, particularly from 2020 to 2024, there is a marked increase in cases, with 2023 and 2024 showing some of the highest monthly counts, peaking at 227 in September 2023. This trend could reflect improved reporting, changes in public health policies, or an actual increase in transmission. The data underscores the need for continued surveillance and possibly enhanced public health interventions to manage and mitigate Typhus spread in the region.
