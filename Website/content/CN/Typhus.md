# Typhus

Version: June 2024

## Introduction

Typhus is a group of infectious diseases caused by Rickettsia bacteria. There are three main types: epidemic typhus, transmitted by lice; murine typhus, spread by fleas; and scrub typhus, caused by chiggers. Symptoms typically include fever, headache, rash, and muscle aches. Severe cases can lead to serious complications like pneumonia, kidney failure, or central nervous system involvement. Diagnosis often involves serological tests, and treatment usually includes antibiotics like doxycycline. Prevention focuses on controlling vector populations and maintaining good hygiene practices to avoid infestations.

## Highlights

- **Rising Trends:** Typhus cases in mainland China show a general upward trend from 2014 to 2024, with peaks in late summer and early autumn. <br/>
- **Mortality Rate:** The mortality rate remains very low, with only three deaths reported in the entire period (2014, 2015, 2016). <br/>
- **Seasonal Fluctuations:** There is a notable seasonal pattern, with higher incidence rates during the warmer months (July to October). <br/>
- **Current Situation:** As of June 2024, the number of cases is stable compared to the previous year, with 131 cases reported, indicating ongoing monitoring and control efforts. <br/>

The typhus data from mainland China (June 2014 - June 2024) reveals a consistent seasonal pattern, with higher cases from June to October, peaking typically in September or October. This seasonal surge is evident across all years, such as 257 cases in October 2014 and 227 in September 2023, suggesting a link to environmental factors that may influence the spread of the disease.

Over the ten-year period, there's a noticeable increase in the annual number of cases, with significant jumps observed in certain years. For example, from 2017 to 2018, cases rose from 88 in September 2017 to 138 in October 2018. The trend culminates in 2023 with the highest recorded peak of 227 cases. Despite this increase, the fatality rate remains low, with only three deaths reported in the entire period, indicating effective management and treatment of the disease.
