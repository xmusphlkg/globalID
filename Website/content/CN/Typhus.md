# Typhus

Version: August 2024

## Introduction

Typhus is a group of infectious diseases caused by Rickettsia bacteria, transmitted to humans through vectors like lice, fleas, and mites. The primary types include epidemic typhus, scrub typhus, and murine typhus. Symptoms commonly involve high fever, chills, headache, rash, and muscle aches. Severe cases can lead to complications such as pneumonitis, encephalitis, and multi-organ failure. Early diagnosis and antibiotic treatment, typically with doxycycline, are crucial for reducing morbidity and preventing outbreaks. Improved sanitation, vector control, and public health awareness are essential preventative measures.

## Highlights

- **Steady increase in cases**: Typhus cases in mainland China show a rising trend from 2017 to 2024, with peaks during the summer months, particularly from June to September.<br/>
- **Seasonality observed**: The disease consistently shows higher case numbers in the summer months, with August often reporting the highest cases each year, reflecting a clear seasonal pattern.<br/>
- **Mortality is low**: Despite the increase in cases, the number of deaths remains very low, with only two deaths recorded between 2015 and 2016.<br/>
- **Recent surge**: August 2024 recorded 207 cases, marking a continuing upward trend compared to previous years, suggesting heightened transmission. <br/>

The reported data for Typhus cases in the Chinese mainland from 2014 to 2024 indicates fluctuating but generally increasing trends in cases over the years, with notable seasonality. The peak months consistently appear to be from July to October, suggesting a seasonal pattern likely associated with environmental factors such as temperature, rainfall, and vector activity. For instance, in 2014, cases rose from 193 in August to a peak of 257 in October before declining. Similarly, in 2023, cases increased significantly from 119 in April to a peak of 227 in September, reflecting this recurring seasonal trend.

While there is an overall rise in the number of cases over the years, with more pronounced peaks in recent years (e.g., 227 in September 2023), the mortality rate remains extremely low, with only two deaths reported across the entire dataset (one each in August 2015 and September 2016). This low mortality rate, despite the variability in case numbers, suggests effective clinical management and public health interventions. However, the upward trend in cases, particularly in recent years, indicates a need for continued surveillance, targeted control measures, and public health education to address potential outbreaks, especially in peak transmission periods.

The analysis of the typhus death data for the Chinese mainland between 2014 and 2024 reveals a strikingly low mortality rate in relation to the number of reported cases. Across the entire period, only two deaths were reported: one in August 2015 and another in September 2016, despite a relatively stable and moderate incidence of cases each year. The data shows annual case numbers typically peaking during the summer months, with a slight fluctuation between seasons, but the absence of significant mortality over such a long period highlights the high survivability of typhus in this region, likely reflecting improved medical treatment and public health interventions.

Interestingly, the case fatality rate remained exceedingly low, despite the steady increase in reported cases over the years, particularly after 2021, where monthly cases frequently exceeded 200 by 2023 and 2024. The rarity of typhus-related deaths suggests that the disease is either being managed well through early detection and intervention or that the strains prevalent in the region may have lower virulence. Nonetheless, continuous monitoring and preventive measures remain crucial, especially during peak periods of transmission to maintain this low mortality rate.
